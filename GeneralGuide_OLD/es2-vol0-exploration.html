Early Game

The decisions you make in turns 1‚Äì40 compound into everything that follows. This volume covers how to structure your opening turns, how probes and scouting actually work mechanically, how to exploit pirates as a resource, and how curiosities and anomalies function as a hidden planet-modifier layer that most players never understand correctly.

01

The First 20 Turns: Priority Order

Most players send their colony ship to the nearest system on turn 1 ‚Äî that's almost always wrong
üöÄTurn-by-Turn Priority FrameworkMECHANIC‚ñæ

Turn 1 gives you a colony ship, an explorer, and a home system. The instinct is to colonize immediately. The problem: you're committing your first expansion vector before you know which systems are worth having, whether nearby planets have crippling anomalies, or where opponents are heading.

  • T1Send the explorer outward immediately. Move along the star lane away from your home system. Every unscouted system is a system an opponent may claim first. Every curiosity they investigate before you is permanent loot lost. Information is your scarcest turn-1 resource.
  • T1Assign your starting hero as governor of the home system ‚Äî not into an explorer fleet. Governors earn XP from every completed system improvement. A high-Industry home system completing 2‚Äì3 projects in the first 15 turns delivers a level 5‚Äì6 hero before turn 20. That far outperforms a level-1 fleet admiral for the same turns invested.
  • T1‚Äì3Replace the home system's default build queue with an Industry improvement. Colonizing requires building colony ships fast, which requires Industry. Clear whatever is queued by default and slot the best flat-Industry improvement you have.
  • T3‚Äì6Send the colony ship toward the highest-value scouted system, not the nearest one. Check planet count, resource deposits, star lane position, and anomaly status. A 1-planet barren system 1 hop away is almost always worse than a 4-planet system 3 hops out. Each colonization hits your empire-wide Approval ‚Äî make it count.
  • T8‚Äì12Build a second explorer. One ship cannot map a 4-player galaxy efficiently. A second scout doubles curiosity investigation rate and ensures you identify chokepoints before opponents close them.
  • T15‚Äì20Research Military Stage 1 to unlock Outpost ships. Outposts are cheap and fast to build. Placing an outpost at a chokepoint or luxury-resource system prevents any opponent from colonizing it, even if you can't develop it yet.
CONQUEST VICTORY CONNECTION
The first 20 turns define your empire's geographic shape for the entire game. Conquest Victory requires capturing a set percentage of all systems ‚Äî which means the chokepoints you claim now determine both how easily you expand and how defensible your borders are. Identify the 1‚Äì2 star lane bottlenecks separating your territory from opponents and claim them with outposts before anything else. You'll spend the rest of the game expanding behind a defensible frontier rather than fighting to recover lost ground.

Probes, Scouting & Expedition Power

Probes have two completely different uses ‚Äî most players only ever discover one of them
üî≠The Two Functions of ProbesMECHANIC‚ñæ

Probes are a consumable resource launched from explorer ships. They serve two distinct purposes that most players conflate:

  • FUNCTION 1Off-lane scouting. A probe fired into uncharted space reveals systems not connected to your star lane network ‚Äî isolated clusters, nebula pockets, asteroid-field systems. Ships cannot reach these without wormhole tech (Science & Exploration Stage 3), but probes reveal their composition for planning. Use this only after your reachable network is fully mapped.
  • FUNCTION 2Curiosity investigation. When a planet pulses, a probe fired at it investigates the curiosity ‚Äî revealing a reward or activating a hidden anomaly. You need no probe to see a system's basic planet data; probes are specifically for curiosity nodes. This is by far the more valuable use in most games.

Two stats control curiosity access: Curiosity Detection Level determines which tiers are visible (a Tier 3 curiosity is invisible without the matching detection tech), and Expedition Power determines which tiers you can actually investigate once seen. Both are unlocked through the Science & Exploration tree. Check your current levels in the F4 Science screen.

PROBE PRIORITY ORDER
When probes are limited, spend them in this order:
1. Curiosities on planets you're about to colonize (eliminate risk first)
2. Tier 2‚Äì3 curiosities within your current Expedition Power
3. Off-lane scouting only after your reachable network is fully mapped
Probing random open space is almost always wasted.
üó∫Ô∏èTwo-Phase Scouting StrategySTRATEGY‚ñæ
  • PHASE 1 (T1‚Äì25)Coverage first. Move explorers along every reachable star lane as fast as possible. Goal: find opponents, identify chokepoints, locate high-value systems. Don't stop to investigate every curiosity ‚Äî mark systems and return. First contact with factions also matters here: you cannot trade, negotiate, or make diplomatic agreements until first contact is established.
  • PHASE 2 (T25+)Systematic harvest. Retask explorers to investigate every marked curiosity from Phase 1. By now you likely have better Expedition Power, making previously-locked Tier 2‚Äì3 curiosities accessible. Curiosities don't expire from age ‚Äî but an opponent who reaches the same system can investigate them first. Prioritize border-zone systems in Phase 2.
RIVALS CAN STEAL CURIOSITIES
If an opponent's explorer reaches a system and investigates curiosities first, those rewards are permanently gone. A unique ship module going to an opponent instead of you is a double loss: you didn't gain it, and they did. On competitive maps, investigate border-system curiosities aggressively during Phase 1 rather than deferring everything.

Pirate Management: From Threat to Asset

Pirates are a resource to exploit ‚Äî not a nuisance to ignore or a war to start
üè¥‚Äç‚ò†Ô∏èThe Pirate Lair System: Three Productive ApproachesMECHANIC‚ñæ

Pirates spawn from Pirate Lairs (skull icons on the galaxy map). Each Lair has an internal countdown timer; when it expires, a fleet spawns and moves toward the nearest non-allied empire system. Fleet strength scales with game age ‚Äî the longer you wait, the stronger they get. Three productive actions are available:

  • HACKUse the Hacking Network (Penumbra DLC) to run "Hack Autodroids" on the Lair ‚Äî this steals some of its stockpiled resources without combat. Other useful pirate hacking results include "Falsify Logs" (improves relations), "Eavesdrop" (‚àí50% pirate diplomacy cost), and "Sabotage Supplies" (delays the next pirate fleet spawn by +4 turns). See Vol.4: Diplomacy & Espionage for the full hacking system.
  • NAPBuy a Non-Aggression Pact from the diplomatic popup when orbiting. The Dust cost varies by threat level. At 35‚Äì40 Dust/turn early income, a 150‚Äì200 Dust NAP typically protects for 15+ turns ‚Äî strongly positive return when your fleet is too weak to fight back. Buy NAPs on Lairs within striking distance of your systems.
  • DESTROYInvade and eliminate the Lair once Medium-hull ships are available. Permanently removes that spawn point, gives a large one-time loot drop, and sometimes completes quest rewards. Prioritize Lairs on your borders ‚Äî eliminating a flank threat frees your military for forward operations.
ALWAYS ARM YOUR EXPLORERS
Investigating curiosities has a ~5‚Äì10% chance of spawning a pirate trap fleet in the system. A weaponless explorer is destroyed instantly. 1‚Äì2 small weapon modules on your explorer cost little in design slots and eliminate this risk. They also allow you to hold position near Lairs long enough to initiate hacking operations without risking your fleet.

Curiosity Tiers, Types & Rewards

Tier 3+ curiosities are a major power source ‚Äî the game never explains this
üåüTier Breakdown and What Each Can Give YouMECHANIC‚ñæ

Every curiosity has a Tier (1‚Äì4) that determines value range, and a Type (Geological, Archaeological, Atmospheric, Biological) that hints at reward category. Tier is far more important for prioritization than type.

TierReward RangeResearch RequiredPriority
Tier 1Common resources, small FIDSI lump sum, minor or null anomalyNone (default)Low ‚Äî only if convenient
Tier 2Better resources, medium FIDSI bonus, positive anomaly, free pop unitScience & Exploration Stage 1Medium ‚Äî investigate en route
Tier 3Rare resources, unique ship modules, tech acceleration, free hero unlockScience & Exploration Stage 2High ‚Äî route explorers to these
Tier 4Once-per-galaxy unique improvements, very powerful modules, permanent pop bonusesScience & Exploration Stage 3Critical ‚Äî highest exploration priority

The most powerful reward category is unique ship modules ‚Äî items not in any tech tree, going directly into your module inventory for use on any ship design. Examples: weapons with penetration stats double the researched equivalent, manpower transport modules, defense modules at twice the normal tier capacity. Always check your ship design module list after a major curiosity run.

OTHER TIER 3‚Äì4 REWARDS WORTH KNOWING
Tech acceleration: Completes your current research partially or fully ‚Äî saves 10‚Äì20 turns on key techs.
Free hero: A civilian ship arrives at your nearest system with a hero for your Academy pool. A free turn-15 hero governed in an Industry system reaches level 7‚Äì8 by turn 40.
Positive anomaly: Permanently adds a FIDSI bonus to the planet (e.g. "+3 Industry/pop") ‚Äî free scaling output that grows with every pop unit added for the rest of the game.

Anomalies: Activation, Risk & Removal

The most counterintuitive system in ES2 ‚Äî anomaly effects don't exist until you trigger them
‚ö†Ô∏èThe Dormancy MechanicMECHANIC‚ñæ

Anomaly effects ‚Äî positive and negative ‚Äî are completely dormant until the curiosity hiding them is investigated. A planet with a "‚àí80% Food" anomaly behind an un-probed curiosity is just a normal planet. The moment you fire a probe at that curiosity, the anomaly activates and immediately begins affecting your FIDSI output.

This creates a real strategic choice for every un-investigated curiosity on a colonized planet:

  • INVESTIGATE NOWRisk activating a negative anomaly, but gain immediate access to any positive reward. Correct when you have Anomaly Correction tech ready, or when the planet's profile makes a positive outcome likely (e.g. an Archaeological curiosity on an Arctic planet trends toward Science bonuses).
  • WAITLeave the curiosity un-probed. The planet runs at baseline with no modifier ‚Äî no bonus, but no penalty either. Correct when Science & Exploration Stage 3 isn't researched yet and the planet is critical to your system's output.
SeverityExamplesCorrect Response
Severe Negative‚àí80% Food, ‚àí50% Industry, ‚àí3 Approval/popDon't investigate until Xenobiology (Science Stage 3) is researched. Mark it and wait.
Mild Negative‚àí10% FIDSI, ‚àí5 system ApprovalUsually worth absorbing to access the potential positive on the same probe.
Positive+3 Industry/pop, +15% Science, +2 Food/popInvestigate immediately ‚Äî these scale permanently with every pop unit you add.
MixedBarren World (‚àíFood, +Industry, +Dust)Evaluate against the planet's role. On a dedicated Industry planet, often net positive.
TRIPLE-ANOMALY PLANETS
Some planets spawn with three curiosity slots already stacked. Triple positive anomalies = one of the best planets in the game. Triple negative = functionally worthless until all three are removed. Hover over the curiosity icon to count the stacked markers before committing a probe.
üîßRemoving Negative AnomaliesSTRATEGY‚ñæ
  • TECH REMOVALResearch Xenobiology in Science & Exploration Stage 3. Any planet with an active anomaly then gets a buildable improvement: Anomaly Correction. Building it costs Industry (scales with severity) and takes 1‚Äì3 turns. After removal, the planet slot is freed and a new curiosity sometimes appears. Plan your Science & Exploration progression to reach Stage 3 before mid-game if you have severe unresolved negatives.
  • BEHEMOTH TERRAFORMA Behemoth with terraforming modules can change the planet type entirely (e.g. Barren ‚Üí Fertile). Changing the type clears all anomalies tied to the old type and opens new curiosity slots appropriate to the new type. More expensive and tech-intensive than Anomaly Correction, but produces a fundamentally better planet. Worth doing for your highest-priority systems. See Vol.2 for full Behemoth mechanics.

Minor Factions: Free Permanent Bonuses

Minor factions give passive income, unique pop types, and border control ‚Äî no war required
üåçThe 5-Level Relationship SystemMECHANIC‚ñæ

Minor factions are non-player civilizations found in systems across the galaxy. They don't expand or declare war. Their relationship bar moves through 5 levels ‚Äî each unlocking better benefits:

LevelWhat You GainHow to Reach It
NeutralSmall passive Dust or luxury trickle beginsMake first contact by orbiting their system
FriendlyResources increase; free pop unit of their race delivered to your nearest systemInvestigate their planet curiosities, or spend Influence in F6
AlliedFull resource sharing; opponents cannot diplomatically approach them while you hold thisContinue Influence investment or repeated curiosity investigation
AssimilatedSystem absorbed into your empire; their pop type permanently available to grow empire-wideBuild Assimilation Center (Empire Dev Stage 2), then trigger assimilation

The free pop unit at Friendly is the most immediately impactful milestone. Many minor faction populations have FIDSI bonuses unavailable to any major faction ‚Äî e.g. +Influence/pop types that no major race provides natively, or +Science/pop bonuses on the right planet type that rival even Sophon output.

FASTEST RELATIONSHIP GAIN
1. Investigate their planet curiosities: Each probe gives a large free relationship jump.
2. Spend Influence in F6: Praise/Bribe actions advance the bar directly.
3. Hacking (Penumbra DLC): "Improve Image" costs only Bandwidth, not Influence.
üéØWhich Minor Factions to PrioritizeSTRATEGY‚ñæ
  • 01Prioritize factions whose pop type fills your FIDSI gap. Weak on Influence? A minor faction with +Influence/pop is an outsized find. 3‚Äì4 of those units in your best system generates more Influence than most buildable improvements.
  • 02Prioritize border-zone factions regardless of bonus quality. A minor faction at Allied status on your border physically blocks opponents from assimilating them ‚Äî forcing them to use military resources instead of diplomacy to reach that star lane corridor.
  • 03Match their political ideology to your Senate goals. Minor faction pops vote for a specific party in every system they're assigned to ‚Äî free election influence without consuming any specialization slots.
SCORE VICTORY CONNECTION
Minor factions at Allied or Assimilated status contribute to your Score (the fallback victory condition in turn-limit games). A wide network of Allied minor factions can meaningfully swing a contested Score race in the final 20 turns without any military investment required.
