ES2 // Vol.3 // Heroes & the Academy

Heroes &
the Academy

Heroes are the highest-leverage units in the game. A well-managed hero outperforms any system improvement by turn 20 and any single ship by turn 40. They govern your systems, lead your fleets, accelerate your colonization, and influence your elections. This volume covers what each hero class does, how to level them efficiently, which skills to prioritize, how to manage reassignments, and how the Galactic Academy changes everything once it appears.

01

Hero Classes: What Each Type Does

Every hero belongs to a class that determines their skill tree and primary role ‚Äî but all heroes can be assigned anywhere
ü¶∏ The Four Hero Classes and Their Roles OVERVIEW ‚ñæ

Every hero has a class that shapes their skill tree. A hero assigned as a fleet Admiral uses their Admiral skills; the same hero reassigned as a system Governor uses their Governor skills. Class determines which skills are available at each tier ‚Äî it does not lock the hero to a single assignment type.

ADMIRAL
// FLEET COMMANDER
Leads a fleet in combat. Admiral skills buff fleet attack, defense, accuracy, evasion, initiative, and grant unique combat abilities. An Admiral's level directly determines fleet combat effectiveness ‚Äî a Level 10 Admiral commanding a mediocre fleet consistently beats a Level 3 Admiral commanding a superior fleet. Always have at least one Admiral per active war front.
GOVERNOR
// SYSTEM MANAGER
Assigned to a system. Governor skills amplify the system's FIDSI output, Approval, population growth, and improvement construction speed. A high-level Governor in your capital can be worth more Dust and Science per turn than multiple system improvements combined. Always have at least one Governor in your highest-output system.
COUNSELOR
// CONQUEST SPECIALIST
Assigned to a system. Counselor skills specialize in post-conquest management: forcing Ecstatic Approval on newly captured systems, reducing occupation unrest, accelerating assimilation of conquered populations, and providing empire-wide influence bonuses. Indispensable for Conquest and Supremacy victory paths.
SEEKER
// EXPLORER / SCOUT
Leads an exploration fleet. Seeker skills improve probe count, movement range (including off-lane movement), curiosity investigation rewards, and provide combat bonuses against pirates. The most important Seeker trait for non-Lumeris factions: off-lane movement, which allows the Seeker's fleet to reach systems outside the star lane network ‚Äî normally a Lumeris-exclusive capability. Several factions' starting heroes are Seekers.
THE HYBRID REALITY ‚Äî MOST HEROES SERVE TWO ROLES
Every hero has both an Admiral skill tree and a Governor/Counselor/Seeker skill tree, unlocked at alternating tiers. A hero you recruited as a fleet Admiral also has Governor skills at Tiers 3, 5, and 7. This means a fully leveled hero can serve in either role at a high level. The practical implication: heroes are reassignable assets, not fixed roles. Your turn-1 explorer hero will become your turn-30 capital Governor. Plan leveling sequences around the role they fill right now, not just their final destination.

XP & Leveling: How Heroes Grow

XP sources are not equal. The fastest path to Level 5 takes 8‚Äì10 turns. The slowest takes 40.
‚≠ê XP Sources and Early Leveling Strategy MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Heroes gain XP from multiple sources. Understanding which sources are available early vs. late ‚Äî and how to maximize them ‚Äî determines how quickly your heroes become impactful.

XP SOURCES ‚Äî RANKED BY EARLY-GAME VALUE
Curiosity investigation ‚Äî hero fleet investigates a planetary anomaly
HIGH / TURN 1
Winning a space battle ‚Äî fleet commanded by the hero defeats an enemy
HIGH / AS NEEDED
System population growth ‚Äî governed system gains a new population unit
MED / ONGOING
System improvement completion ‚Äî governed system finishes building an improvement
MED / ONGOING
Completing a tech research ‚Äî empire finishes a technology (XP to current system Governor)
LOW-MED / ONGOING
Completing a faction quest stage
BURST / PERIODIC
Dust payment to level up ‚Äî pay Dust directly to add one level (diminishing returns)
EMERGENCY ONLY
THE TURN-1 IMPERATIVE ‚Äî CURIOSITY FARMING
Attach your starting hero to your explorer fleet immediately on turn 1. Send them to investigate every curiosity in reach before doing anything else. Each investigation grants a flat XP bonus to the hero ‚Äî a hero that investigates 8‚Äì10 curiosities in the first 10 turns typically reaches Level 3‚Äì4 before most factions' heroes hit Level 2. This early level gap compounds: a Level 4 hero governs your capital with meaningful FIDSI bonuses by turn 10, a Level 2 hero contributes almost nothing. Curiosity farming is the single highest-yield early XP action available.
THE TECH COMPLETION TRICK
When a tech research completes, the XP is awarded to whoever is currently assigned as the governed hero of your home system ‚Äî not necessarily your best governor. If you have a hero about to level up from a small amount of XP, reassign them to your home system the turn before a tech completes to capture that XP boost. This is a minor optimization but matters in the early game when every XP point between levels 2 and 4 affects when you unlock key skills.
PAYING DUST TO LEVEL ‚Äî WHEN IT'S WORTH IT
The Dust cost to manually level a hero escalates steeply with each level. It is worth paying in exactly two situations:

1. War is 2‚Äì3 turns away and your Admiral is one level short of a key combat skill. The combat skill's impact across a 10-battle war is worth the one-time Dust cost.
2. You just recruited a new hero from the market or Academy and need them operational immediately ‚Äî bring them to Level 3 via Dust if the situation demands it. Never pay Dust to level past Level 5 in normal play; natural XP takes over efficiently from there.

Admiral Skills: Fleet Combat Priorities

Not all Admiral skills are equal. Two categories dominate: damage amplification and initiative. Everything else is secondary.
‚öîÔ∏è What to Take and What to Skip SKILLS ‚ñæ

Admiral skill trees vary by faction hero type. The priority framework below applies generally ‚Äî specific numbers vary by hero.

ADMIRAL SKILL PRIORITY FRAMEWORK
TIER 1‚Äì2
Damage amplification ‚Äî any skill that adds a flat % bonus to all fleet weapon damage. These apply multiplicatively with weapon module bonuses. At Tier 2, a +20% fleet damage skill effectively makes every weapon module 20% stronger with no additional resource cost. This is the highest per-level Admiral contribution to combat power.
MUST TAKE
TIER 2‚Äì3
Initiative bonus ‚Äî determines which fleet fires first in each combat phase. High initiative means your fleet gets the first volley in every phase; against an evenly matched opponent, going first can eliminate 1‚Äì2 enemy ships before they deal a single point of damage. Any Admiral skill adding +Initiative is extremely high value.
MUST TAKE
TIER 3‚Äì4
Accuracy / evasion bonuses ‚Äî improve the hit chance of your weapons or lower the hit chance of incoming weapons. Valuable but less impactful than damage amplification at equivalent levels, since they only affect a % of shots rather than all damage dealt. Take these when damage and initiative options are exhausted at the same tier.
STRONG
TIER 4‚Äì5
Shield Interference Beam (where available, Tier 4 military tech) ‚Äî disables enemy shields for a phase before your weapons fire. Effective specifically against heavy-shield opponents. Situationally powerful; take it if you're fighting opponents who invest heavily in energy shields.
SITUATIONAL
ANY TIER
Fleet movement bonuses ‚Äî movement speed on the galaxy map. Useful for rapid repositioning across a large empire but contributes zero to combat effectiveness. Take only after all combat-relevant skills at the same tier are acquired. Exception: if your strategy relies on out-maneuvering opponents to avoid unfavorable engagements, movement becomes more valuable.
LOW PRIORITY
ANY TIER
Manpower capacity increases ‚Äî raises the deployment limit for ground invasions. Only relevant if you're actively invading systems this game. Skip entirely if your strategy is blockade-and-negotiate rather than conquest.
CONDITIONAL
FACTION HERO DISPARITIES ‚Äî KNOW YOUR STARTING DISADVANTAGE
Some factions' starting heroes have dramatically stronger Admiral trees than others. Sophon and Vodyani heroes have Tier 2 skills that add +40% fleet energy damage ‚Äî a bonus available by Level 3 that outperforms most other factions' Level 7 Admiral trees. If you're playing a faction with a weak Admiral tree (Lumeris, Riftborn), reaching the Galactic Academy to recruit a better combat hero should be an explicit strategic priority, not an afterthought.

Governor Skills: System Output Multipliers

A Tier 4 Governor in your capital is worth more per turn than most system improvements. The Science bonus unlocks this early.
üèôÔ∏è Governor Skill Priorities and System Placement SKILLS ‚ñæ
GOVERNOR SKILL PRIORITY FRAMEWORK
TIER 1
+Science per turn ("Blue Skies" or equivalent) ‚Äî the most universally impactful early Governor skill. +15‚Äì30 Science per turn in the early game, when your empire generates 40‚Äì80 Science total, is a 20‚Äì40% Science boost from a single skill. This directly accelerates every tech you research for the rest of the game. Always the first Governor skill to take.
MUST TAKE
TIER 2‚Äì3
FIDSI % amplifiers ‚Äî skills that add a flat % to all system FIDSI output (e.g., +15% to all production). These apply to the base system output before luxury resource multipliers, meaning they compound with everything else the system produces. Place your Governor in your highest-output system to maximize the absolute value of these percentage bonuses.
MUST TAKE
TIER 3‚Äì4
Approval bonus to governed system ‚Äî adds flat Approval to the system the Governor manages. This stacks with your empire-wide Approval and can push a system above a breakpoint (70 or 85) that the rest of your empire hasn't reached yet. Particularly valuable when placing a Governor in a newly colonized or conquered system that is dragging down your empire-wide Approval.
STRONG
TIER 4
Force Ecstatic Approval (Counselor tree but available to some Governors) ‚Äî the governed system is forced to Ecstatic Approval regardless of actual modifiers. Instantly converts a newly conquered, heavily penalized system into a productive colony. Covered in detail in the Counselor section.
STRONG (CONQUEST)
ANY TIER
Population growth acceleration ‚Äî speeds up how fast the governed system gains population. Population = production capacity, so this is a compounding investment: more population now ‚Üí more FIDSI now ‚Üí better system for the rest of the game. Valuable in your highest-food systems where growth rate is the limiting factor.
GOOD
WHERE TO PLACE YOUR GOVERNOR
Always place your primary Governor in your highest-FIDSI system ‚Äî usually your home system (capital). The % FIDSI amplifiers scale with the system's existing output: a +15% skill on a system generating 200 FIDSI adds 30 FIDSI. The same skill on a system generating 60 FIDSI adds only 9. Maximizing the system output before placing the Governor maximizes the Governor's returns.

Exception: newly captured systems ‚Äî if a freshly conquered system has a severe Approval penalty dragging down your empire-wide average, a temporary Governor assignment to stabilize its Approval can be worth more in the short term than the capital bonus. Rotate back to the capital once the system stabilizes.

Counselor Skills: Conquest Management

The Force Ecstatic skill at Tier 4 is one of the most impactful abilities in the game for aggressive players
üß≠ Why Counselors Matter for Conquest Paths SKILLS ‚ñæ

Counselors are the most underappreciated hero class because their value is invisible on peaceful playthroughs. On conquest paths, they're irreplaceable.

COUNSELOR SKILL PRIORITY FRAMEWORK
TIER 2‚Äì3
Occupation Approval bonus ‚Äî adds flat Approval to a conquered system under occupation. Without this, a newly captured system runs at Angry or Unhappy Approval (‚àí30 to ‚àí50 penalty), dragging down your entire empire's FIDSI multiplier. The Counselor provides enough Approval bonus to bring the occupied system out of penalty range within 1‚Äì2 turns of occupation.
MUST TAKE
TIER 4
Force Ecstatic Approval ‚Äî the governed system is forced to Ecstatic Approval (85+) regardless of all other modifiers. A newly conquered system with ‚àí40 Approval, enemy population, war penalties, and overcolonization hits Ecstatic the turn the Counselor is assigned. This is the ability that makes rapid conquest economically viable ‚Äî without it, each new conquest temporarily tanks your empire's FIDSI. With it, each new system is productive within 1 turn.
MUST TAKE
TIER 3‚Äì5
Assimilation speed ‚Äî reduces the number of turns it takes to convert conquered enemy population into your faction's population. Conquered populations generate less FIDSI and may have political effects (voting for opposing parties). Faster assimilation removes these penalties sooner. Priority scales with how many systems you're conquering per game.
STRONG (CONQUEST)
ANY TIER
Empire-wide Influence bonus ‚Äî some Counselor trees provide flat Influence per turn to the empire regardless of system assignment. Influence powers Senate lobbying, law enactment, and certain diplomatic actions. A Counselor generating +20 Influence/turn is effectively a Senate seat factory.
GOOD
THE COUNSELOR ROTATION ‚Äî ONE COUNSELOR FOR MANY CONQUESTS
You don't need a Counselor permanently stationed in every conquered system. The workflow:
Turn 1 of occupation: Assign Counselor ‚Üí system hits Ecstatic, occupation Approval stabilizes.
Turns 2‚Äì5: System builds basic improvements, population begins assimilating.
Turn 5‚Äì8: Reassign Counselor to the next freshly conquered system. The first system's Approval will drop when the Counselor leaves, but by then basic happiness improvements are in the build queue.

One Counselor can cycle through 3‚Äì4 systems in a war, providing the critical Ecstatic window in each and then moving on. This requires planning your conquest sequence so each new system is captured approximately when the Counselor is available.

Assignment Strategy: The Lifecycle of a Hero

The same hero fills three different roles across a game. The transitions between them ‚Äî and their timing ‚Äî matter more than most players realize.
üîÑ Reassignment Cooldowns and the Three-Phase Hero Arc MECHANIC ‚ñæ
THE REASSIGNMENT COOLDOWN ‚Äî PLAN 2 TURNS AHEAD
When you reassign a hero from a fleet to a system (or vice versa), there is a cooldown period before they are active in the new role. During this cooldown, they contribute to neither their old role nor their new one ‚Äî they are in transit. This cooldown is typically 2‚Äì3 turns. The implication: if you need your hero governing a system by turn 10, reassign them from the fleet by turn 7‚Äì8. If you wait until turn 10 to reassign, they won't be productive until turn 12‚Äì13. Plan reassignments proactively, not reactively.

A starting explorer hero typically moves through three phases across a game:

TURNS 1‚Äì8
Fleet assignment ‚Äî investigates curiosities, farms XP, scouts galaxy. Reaches Level 3‚Äì4.
TURNS 8‚Äì30
Capital Governor ‚Äî contributes +Science, FIDSI bonuses. Continues leveling from improvements and population growth.
TURNS 30+
Reassigned based on need: back to fleet as Admiral if war looms, or stays as Governor if economy is the priority.
HOW MANY HEROES SHOULD YOU MAINTAIN?
The general answer scales with game phase:

Early game (turns 1‚Äì25): 1‚Äì2 heroes. One explorer/governor, one optional second if available from the market cheaply.
Mid game (turns 25‚Äì60): 2‚Äì3 heroes. One permanent capital Governor, one Admiral for your primary combat fleet, one Counselor if you're conquering.
Late game (turns 60+): 3‚Äì5 heroes. Each major system and each active war front benefits from a dedicated hero. Hero upkeep cost (Dust per turn per hero) scales with their level ‚Äî factor this into your Dust budget before recruiting.
HERO UPKEEP ‚Äî THE HIDDEN DUST COST
Every hero costs Dust per turn proportional to their level. A Level 10 hero costs significantly more upkeep than a Level 3 hero. In the early game when Dust is tight, maintaining 3 heroes you can't fully utilize is worse than maintaining 1 hero you develop rapidly. Recruit heroes when you have a specific role for them ‚Äî not speculatively. The hero market refreshes each election cycle; a hero you pass on this cycle may not return, but another suitable hero usually will.

The Hero Market

The market refreshes each election cycle. It sells heroes from all factions ‚Äî including ones with skill trees your faction can't normally access.
üõí Market Mechanics and What to Buy MECHANIC ‚ñæ
WHAT TO BUY
Heroes with top-tier Admiral damage skills from powerful factions (Sophons, Vodyani) ‚Äî these are game-breaking in fleet combat and available to any faction that can afford them. A Sophon Admiral hero bought by a Lumeris player brings the same +40% energy damage to the Lumeris fleet. Counselors with Force Ecstatic skill if pursuing conquest. Any hero filling a role you currently have no hero for.
WHAT TO SKIP
Heroes who duplicate a role you already fill well. Heroes at very high levels with steep upkeep costs when your Dust budget is tight. Heroes whose best skills are in late tiers you won't reach for 20+ turns ‚Äî the market will refresh before they're useful; buy them closer to when you can use those skills.
MARKET TIMING
The market refreshes at each election (every ~20 turns). If a hero you want is in the current market, buying them this turn vs. next turn makes little difference ‚Äî but waiting until the next refresh cycle to "see what comes next" usually costs you the hero you wanted. Decide each cycle: buy now or wait for next refresh.
COST STRUCTURE
Hero purchase price is paid in Dust and scales with their current level. A Level 1 hero costs a fraction of a Level 5 hero. Buying low and leveling yourself is almost always more Dust-efficient than buying a pre-leveled hero ‚Äî the exception is crisis situations where you need a specific skill immediately and can't wait 15 turns to level into it.

The Galactic Academy

A mid-game system that grants a permanent hero recruitment advantage. Controlling it changes the hero landscape for the rest of the game.
üèõÔ∏è Academy Mechanics and Why It's Worth Colonizing MECHANIC ‚ñæ
WHAT THE ACADEMY DOES
The Galactic Academy is a special system that appears mid-game (typically around turn 20‚Äì35, revealed by exploration or quest). The faction that colonizes and controls the Academy system gains access to an expanded hero recruitment pool ‚Äî heroes become available faster, at lower cost, and occasionally with unique skills unavailable on the standard market. The controlling faction also gains a Science bonus per turn from the Academy's system improvements.

Importantly, the Academy's hero pool includes heroes from all factions, with slightly preferential access for the controlling faction. For a Lumeris player, controlling the Academy is often the fastest path to acquiring a high-tier Sophon Admiral hero.
THE COLONIZATION PRIORITY CASE
The Academy is frequently located in an isolated or off-lane system ‚Äî accessible via off-lane colonization for Lumeris but requiring a fleet expedition for most other factions. This means:

For Lumeris: If you've scouted the Academy location early, you can claim it the same turn you probe it if you have the Dust. No other faction can respond in time via standard colonization.
For all factions: Colonizing the Academy system before opponents is worth diverting a colony ship from other targets, because the long-term hero recruitment advantage compounds across the entire remaining game. Even 10 turns of Academy control before an opponent contests it accelerates your hero development significantly.
USING THE ACADEMY STRATEGICALLY
Once you control the Academy:

Recruit a high-tier Admiral as your first Academy action ‚Äî particularly a Sophon or Vodyani hero if available, whose damage skill trees are otherwise inaccessible to your faction.
Use Academy heroes to fill role gaps ‚Äî if your empire lacks a Counselor and you're planning a conquest campaign, the Academy's broader pool makes finding one faster than waiting for the standard market cycle.
If contested: An opponent colonizing the same Academy system triggers a diplomacy or military response situation. Decide whether the Academy is worth defending militarily ‚Äî it usually is, as losing it reverses the hero recruitment advantage entirely.
ACADEMY QUEST CURIOSITY TRAP
Several faction questlines have a stage requiring you to "discover the Academy location" by investigating specific atmospheric curiosities. The Academy's location is not revealed on the map until this quest stage is completed ‚Äî meaning other factions may reach it first if their questline triggers earlier. If you have explorers near suspected Academy locations (typically in central or cross-lane systems), investigate atmospheric curiosities proactively. Finding the Academy before the quest triggers still counts toward quest completion.
