Heroes & the Academy

How to hire, grow, assign, and squeeze maximum value out of the game's most powerful asymmetric resource.

Hero Classes: What Each Type Does

Four archetypes, each with a distinct skill tree and hero ship ‚Äî your assignment matches their tree or you waste both
üéì The Four Classes at a Glance MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Every hero in ES2 belongs to one of four classes: Seeker, Guardian, Overseer, or Counselor. The class determines their class-specific skill tree and the hull of their personal hero ship. In addition to class skills, all heroes share access to universal skills and have two unique innate skills (non-upgradeable) that define their individual identity. Finally, heroes also get a set of faction skills tied to their origin faction.

XP & Leveling: How Heroes Grow

XP thresholds, sources per role, and the early-game curiosity window
üìà XP Thresholds & Skill Tree Structure MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Heroes start at level 1 and need 25 XP to reach level 2, then 50 XP total for level 3, increasing by 25 XP per additional level. Leveling up grants one skill point to spend in the hero's skill tree.

The skill tree has four tiers. Tier 2 requires 4 total skill points invested before unlocking; Tier 3 requires 8; Tier 4 requires 12. This means T4 skills are not accessible until a hero has invested 12 points, regardless of how many levels they have. Many skills can receive a second point investment, often doubling the bonus ‚Äî and sometimes adding an entirely new effect.

SKILL POINT ECONOMY
Because T4 requires 12 invested points, you need to spend points through tiers 1‚Äì3 you may not fully want. Plan your T4 target skill first and work backward to choose the least-wasteful path through the lower tiers.

EARLY-GAME CURIOSITY WINDOW
Attach your starting hero to your scout fleet for the first 8‚Äì12 turns. Six XP per curiosity investigated adds up fast ‚Äî a hero that resolves 6 curiosities reaches level 3 on curiosity XP alone, before governor XP becomes substantial. After the early exploration phase, transition to governor when population and project queues provide steady passive ticks.
PROJECT XP: THE ~8% RULE [T3 ‚Äî Reddit, u/Kashakunaki, 9y old ‚Äî verify if patched]
Community testing (Normal speed, no XP modifiers) found that project completion XP scales at roughly ~8% of the Industry cost of the completed project ‚Äî or about 1 XP per 12‚Äì12.5 Industry spent. Multiple completions in the same turn stack: finish a 280-Industry building and two 210-Industry ships in one turn, and the governor earns XP based on the combined 700 Industry total. This applies equally to buildings and ships. Buyout via Dust or Influence grants the same XP as building via production. Note: colonizing planets, terraforming, and industry-conversion projects (‚ÜíDust, ‚ÜíScience) do NOT generate project XP ‚Äî only the base 1 XP/turn applies on those turns.
WONDER XP EXPLOIT [T3 ‚Äî community-confirmed, same source]
Wonders have extremely high Industry costs ‚Äî completing one can level a hero multiple times in a single turn. If you have a low-level hero you want to accelerate, temporarily assign them as governor to a system that is about to complete a wonder, collect the massive XP burst, then reassign them. The assignment cooldown applies after the swap, but the XP spike is immediate on completion.

üè• Hero Injury & Recovery MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Heroes become injured and unavailable for assignment in specific scenarios: their fleet is completely destroyed; the system they govern is captured or razed during invasion; an Obliterator fires on a system they govern; or a hacking operation targets and injures them. If at least one non-hero ship survives when the hero ship is destroyed, the hero is spared and their ship is restored at minimal HP.

Injured heroes cannot be assigned for 20 turns at Normal speed. This can be skipped by spending a large quantity of Dust. A few modifiers accelerate recovery rate (which halves the wait time at +100%):

  • HORATIOPerfect Genes II: +100% hero recovery rate
  • VODYANI50-population collection bonus: +100% hero recovery rate
  • MINOR FACSisters of Mercy assimilation: +50% hero recovery rate
  • EMPIRE DEVTier 5 Dust-9 Production "Uplift Institute": +100% hero recovery rate
GOVERNOR RISK DURING WAR
Unassigning a governor before enemy invasion reaches the system is free. If you raze a system with a hero still inside, the hero is injured. Keep this in mind when razing occupied systems with your own governor present ‚Äî the injury cost may outweigh the tactical gain.

Seeker Class: Philosophy and Skills

The exploration and movement specialist ‚Äî excellent early, transitions to combat fleet commander
üî≠ Seeker Role Summary MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Seekers are primarily built for fleet command. Their skill tree focuses on movement speed, vision range, probe generation, and damage amplification. As governors they provide Science and Influence bonuses, making them usable in systems early in the game before better governors arrive. As senators, a Seeker in power grants +1 Movement to all empire fleets ‚Äî a strong empire-wide passive.

Guardian Class: Philosophy and Skills

The pure combat specialist ‚Äî unmatched fleet survivability and damage, but zero movement bonuses
‚öîÔ∏è Guardian Role Summary MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Guardians are the strongest fleet commanders in the game for raw combat. Their skill tree concentrates on fleet health, hull plating absorption, shield capacity, damage, and manpower refill rate. They also get access to a special battle strategy unavailable to other classes. As governors they boost Industry ‚Äî a useful but secondary niche. As senators, they reduce the Influence cost of treaties.

THE MOVEMENT TRADEOFF
Guardians grant no movement bonuses. A Guardian-led fleet will hit harder and survive longer than any other, but it moves slower relative to a Seeker-led fleet of equivalent size. For defensive postures and siege operations this is acceptable; for hit-and-run or reactive defense across a wide empire, the lack of movement can be a strategic liability. Overseer Class: Philosophy and Skills

The resource governor ‚Äî at their best on Fertile or Hot systems with rare resource deposits
üè≠ Overseer Role Summary MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Overseers are the dominant governor class. Their skill tree covers Food (especially on Fertile planets), Industry (especially on Hot planets), Dust, strategic and luxury resource generation, and reductions to overpopulation/overcolonization happiness penalties. As fleet commanders they are adequate but suboptimal, offering Manpower refill, Movement, and Shield capacity. As senators they boost resource generation empire-wide.

ContextKey bonuses
Governor+Food (Fertile bonus), +Industry (Hot bonus), +Dust, +Strategic/Luxury resource gen, ‚àíovercolonization penalty
Fleet Commander+Manpower refill, +Movement, +Shield capacity
Senator+Strategic and luxury resource generation empire-wide
PLACEMENT PRIORITY: RARE RESOURCE SYSTEMS
The single highest-value Overseer placement is a governor assignment in a system that generates rare strategic resources ‚Äî Quadrinix, Orchalcix, or any single system with many individual resource deposits. The T2 resource generation skill multiplies every deposit independently, making a system with 5 different resources dramatically more productive than one with 5 of the same resource.
UNDERSTOOD & APPLIED
‚≠ê The Senator Case for Overseers STRATEGY ‚ñæ

An Overseer senator provides empire-wide resource generation bonuses when their party is in power. For resource-dependent strategies ‚Äî and especially for factions that rely on rare strategics for ship modules ‚Äî getting an Overseer into the senate is a high-priority political goal. This synergizes with factions like Cravers or anyone running high military throughput that burns through Titanium or Hyperium.

To maximize the senator bonus, ensure the Overseer has invested points in skills that carry the "On Senate" tag. The political leader of a party (who provides the senate skill bonuses) is the hero with the most "On Senate" skills, with ties broken by level.

Counselor Class: Philosophy and Skills

The Influence and Approval governor ‚Äî and the secret weapon for occupied system recovery
üåê Counselor Role Summary MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Counselors excel as governors, particularly on Sterile systems where their Dust bonus shines, and in any situation where Influence or Approval management is the bottleneck. Their fleet commander role is acceptable but Seekers and Guardians will outperform them in combat. As senators, Counselors grant empire-wide Influence bonuses.

üè≥Ô∏è The Occupation Recovery Use Case STRATEGY ‚ñæ

The Counselor's T4 skill sets system Approval to Ecstatic regardless of all other factors. This is an extremely powerful ability when conquering systems: freshly captured systems suffer massive Approval penalties from the ownership change, which collapses Food and Influence production. Moving a high-level Counselor into the newly captured system as governor immediately negates those penalties, allowing Food to recover and preventing population starvation.

OCCUPATION MANAGEMENT ROTATION
If you're running a conquest campaign and regularly capturing systems, keep a Counselor hero dedicated to "occupation cleanup." Move them into each newly captured system for 5‚Äì10 turns until Approval stabilizes, then rotate them to the next capture. This prevents the food crashes that can lose population in newly acquired systems and turns the Counselor into a force multiplier for an aggressive expansion playstyle.
VISION BONUS: COUNSELOR FLEET COMMANDER NICHE
The Counselor's T3 fleet skill provides a massive vision range bonus. This can be valuable for recon and exploration even in the mid-game ‚Äî a Counselor leading a small fast fleet acts as an early warning system and long-range scout at lower opportunity cost than pulling a dedicated Seeker off their primary fleet.

Assignment Strategy: The Lifecycle of a Hero

Phase-based thinking ‚Äî hero value is not static, and the best assignment changes as the game evolves
‚ôªÔ∏è The Three Assignment Phases STRATEGY ‚ñæ

The optimal assignment for most heroes evolves through three broad phases during a game. Treating hero assignment as a lifecycle ‚Äî rather than a permanent decision ‚Äî yields significantly better output than set-and-forget.

  • EARLY GAMEExploration Phase: Most heroes benefit from being attached to a scout fleet to harvest curiosity XP (6 XP each), discover systems (+4 XP each), and accumulate level-ups quickly. Even a governor-type hero (Overseer, Counselor) often earns more XP in the first 10‚Äì15 turns by exploring than sitting on an underpopulated home system.
  • MID GAMEGovernor Phase: Once your home system has 4+ population and a reasonable build queue, governor XP (0.5 per pop per turn + 1 base + project ticks) rivals or exceeds explorer XP. Transition Overseer and Counselor heroes to their priority systems. Keep Seekers and Guardians in fleet command as the combat environment heats up.
  • LATE GAMESenate Optimization: As heroes reach higher levels and accumulate "On Senate" skills, deliberately plan which political parties you want them to lead. A high-level Overseer senator generating empire-wide resource bonuses is worth more than any individual governor placement at this stage. Begin shaping hero investments toward senate skills if you have heroes who are not in active combat roles.

Heroes' Political Affiliations

How hero parties interact with the senate ‚Äî and why it matters which hero leads which party
üèõÔ∏è Hero Senator Mechanics MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Every hero belongs to a political party. When that party is in power in the senate, the hero with the most "On Senate" skills becomes the political leader for their party ‚Äî their senator skill bonuses activate empire-wide. Ties between heroes with equal senate skill counts are broken by hero level.

The number of heroes that can simultaneously serve as senators depends on government type: Democracy allows 3 parties (and thus 3 hero senators); Republic and Federation allow 2; Dictatorship and Autocracy allow only 1 (but with a 1.5√ó senator skill bonus to compensate).

SENATE SKILL PLANNING
If you want a specific hero to become the senator for their party, they must have more "On Senate" skill investments than any other hero of the same party. Review this proactively ‚Äî if two Militarist heroes are both leveling up, the one with more senate skills leads. Deliberate skill investment, not just leveling, determines who controls the senate bonus.

The Hero Market

Buying heroes directly with Dust ‚Äî cheap, first-come-only, and strategically significant
üí∞ Market Mechanics MECHANIC ‚ñæ

The marketplace is unlocked by the tier-3 technology Impactless Sites. It shares a UI panel with the resource trade market (unlocked by the tier-2 Galactic Commodities Exchange). The hero marketplace allows you to directly hire heroes for Dust ‚Äî often just a few thousand Dust, making them significantly cheaper than buyout-rushing even early-game buildings.

The available pool is shared across all players in the session. Heroes purchased by one player are no longer available to others. The AI purchases market heroes very rarely, so the risk of losing a good hero to an AI purchase is low but not zero. The pool is entirely random in class and faction, refreshing over time.

MARKET VIGILANCE
Check the marketplace every few turns in the mid-game. A high-quality hero of the class you need (Overseer for a resource system, Guardian for a combat fleet) can appear and disappear. The cost is usually trivial relative to Dust income ‚Äî there is almost never a reason not to buy a useful hero from the market when one appears. Heroes in ES2 have negligible upkeep relative to their value.
DON'T SELL HEROES
Selling heroes on the market is technically possible but never advisable. Hero upkeep in ES2 is tuned to be essentially free relative to Dust income, and the sell value will not recoup meaningful resources. Keep all heroes ‚Äî park underused ones in secondary governor roles where their passive XP ticks still accumulate value.

The Galactic Academy

The passive hero generation engine ‚Äî and how to make it work faster for you
üåü How the Academy Works MECHANIC ‚ñæ

The Galactic Academy is a special system that appears somewhere in the galaxy. Once any player discovers it (or you build an Academy Embassy), the Academy becomes active for all players. From that point, all empires passively accumulate "Hero Unlock Points" toward receiving a free hero.

The required points scale linearly: 100 + (400 √ó number of heroes already owned). Every new hero you acquire ‚Äî whether from the Academy, the market, or a quest ‚Äî raises the threshold for the next one. Building the Academy Embassy improvement (requires the Xenology technology) reduces required points by 20% permanently, and is worth building even if the Academy system was already discovered.

When you reach the required point threshold, at the start of the next turn you are presented with a choice of three heroes. You may pick only one ‚Äî the others remain in the shared pool for other players to potentially acquire.

Governor-heavy play biases toward Overseers and Counselors. The Academy screen shows current point progress and the class probability breakdown.

Hero class selection is also weighted by your current political affiliations ‚Äî heroes whose political party matches your dominant parties appear more frequently. The Academy Power (the level new Academy heroes start at) equals the average level of all heroes currently in the game.

üéÆ Hacking the Academy: The Fastest Unlock Method STRATEGY ‚ñæ

The most efficient way to accelerate Academy unlocks is hacking the Academy's systems. Each successful hack on an Academy system additively adds 5% of your progress toward the next hero. This percentage is fixed ‚Äî it does not slow down as the absolute point requirement scales up with each new hero. Multiple simultaneous hack operations further multiply the speed.

HACKING PRIORITY FOR NON-PENUMBRA FACTIONS
Even factions without a Hacking-focused playstyle should consider hacking the Academy once Penumbra tech is available. Twenty consecutive successful hacks provide 100% progress ‚Äî essentially a free hero regardless of point threshold. Owning systems adjacent to the Academy also reduces hacking range requirements. This does not interact with the 20% Academy Embassy reduction; it is a separate and additive acceleration.
ACADEMY POWER SIDE EFFECT
Rapidly accumulating many heroes through market purchases and hacking also raises Academy Power (average hero level in the game). This means future Academy-granted heroes start at a higher level ‚Äî but it also means your competitors' Academy-granted heroes start higher too if they are recruiting heroes. The system is global, not player-specific.
