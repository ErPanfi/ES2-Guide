Vodyani ‚Äî The Nomadic Ark Civilization

No traditional colonies. No planet FIDSI. Everything comes from Arks orbiting systems.
ü¶ã Core Mechanic: Arks Replace Colonization MECHANIC ‚ñæ
‚óà VODYANI ‚Äî THE LOST

The Vodyani do not colonize planets. They cannot build System Improvements, cannot grow population on planets, and are completely immune to the Approval system. Instead, they build and upgrade massive ships called Arks. An Ark orbiting a system "claims" that system and generates all FIDSI from its own internal modules ‚Äî completely independent of the system's planets.

RULE BREAK 1 ‚Äî NO COLONIZATION
Vodyani never build colony ships. Systems are claimed by moving an Ark into orbit. The Ark's modules determine what FIDSI the system produces. The system's planets are irrelevant to Vodyani FIDSI output ‚Äî though the system's star lane position and resource deposits still matter.
RULE BREAK 2 ‚Äî ESSENCE INSTEAD OF FOOD
Vodyani population does not grow from Food. Instead, population units (called "the Lost") are generated by consuming Essence ‚Äî a special resource harvested from minor factions. To gain a new population unit, you must orbit a minor faction's home system with an Ark and extract Essence from them. Each extraction reduces the minor faction's population and relation with you. Minor factions can be harvested to death.
RULE BREAK 3 ‚Äî NO APPROVAL SYSTEM
Vodyani have no Approval mechanic at all. There is no happiness modifier on their FIDSI. Expanding as rapidly as possible has no Approval cost. The constraint instead is Ark construction cost and Essence availability.
VODYANI PRIORITY ORDER
1. Build your first additional Ark as fast as possible (your home Ark has limited module slots).
2. Explore the galaxy to find minor factions ‚Äî your Essence supply. Scout specifically for minor faction locations, not necessarily good planet systems.
3. Upgrade Ark modules: FIDSI modules on each Ark determine your empire's output. Treat Ark upgrades exactly as you would system improvements ‚Äî Industry modules first, then Science, then Dust.
4. The Ark also serves as a fleet ‚Äî a well-upgraded Ark can fight alongside military ships. Don't neglect its combat modules entirely.

Cravers ‚Äî The Devouring Hive

No Approval system. No diplomacy. Planets degrade over time and must be abandoned. Conquest is not optional ‚Äî it's survival.
üêõ Core Mechanics: Depletion & Forced Expansion MECHANIC ‚ñæ
‚óà CRAVERS ‚Äî CONCRETE HIVE
RULE BREAK 1 ‚Äî PLANET DEPLETION
Every planet in a Craver system degrades over time. Each colonized planet has a Depletion counter that increments each turn. As the counter rises, the planet's FIDSI output progressively decreases ‚Äî starting at ‚àí5% and reaching ‚àí50% or more on fully depleted planets. Depletion cannot be stopped or reversed. A Craver player must continuously conquer new systems to replace depleted ones, or their empire's production collapses from within.
RULE BREAK 2 ‚Äî NO DIPLOMACY
Cravers cannot form treaties, alliances, or trade agreements. No other faction will sign agreements with them. The only diplomatic state available to Cravers is hostility. This means no Trade Company route extensions through neighbor territory, no Research Agreements, and no Senate coalition building. Every mechanic in Vol.3 is inapplicable to Cravers.
RULE BREAK 3 ‚Äî MODIFIED APPROVAL
Cravers do not experience standard Approval penalties from expansion. Instead, they have a unique "Pacification" mechanic: conquered populations are pacified rather than unhappy, and the Approval equivalent is managed through the Pacification buildings rather than Happiness improvements. Rapid expansion has much lower penalties for Cravers than for any other faction.
THE DEPLETION TIMELINE ‚Äî PLAN YOUR CONQUEST CALENDAR
Depletion accelerates around turns 40‚Äì60 on your earliest colonies. Before that window, you must have new systems ready to absorb your population and production needs. The correct play: always have your next conquest target identified 15‚Äì20 turns before you need it. If your home system is at 40% depletion and you have no new system ready to colonize, you're already behind. Cravers always win fast (Conquest) or lose slowly (depletion spiral).
EXPLOITATION BUILDINGS: EXTRACT MAXIMUM VALUE BEFORE DEPLETION
Cravers have access to unique "Extraction" improvements that massively boost FIDSI output of a specific resource ‚Äî but at the cost of dramatically accelerating depletion on that planet. When a system is already heavily depleted and you're planning to abandon it anyway, build Extraction improvements to squeeze maximum Industry or Science from the dying planets before you move on. Extract, deplete fully, abandon, conquer new. That's the Craver loop.

Riftborn ‚Äî The Manufactured Civilization

Population is not grown from Food ‚Äî it is built in factories using Industry. Every system mechanic changes as a result.
‚ùÑÔ∏è Core Mechanic: Population as Industrial Output MECHANIC ‚ñæ
‚óà RIFTBORN ‚Äî THE ANNEXERS
RULE BREAK 1 ‚Äî MANUFACTURED POPULATION
Riftborn do not grow population from Food. Food is completely irrelevant to their population growth. Instead, population units are produced as a buildable item in the system's construction queue ‚Äî exactly like building a ship or a system improvement. Each pop unit costs a set amount of Industry and takes a set number of turns to complete. Food improvements, Food specializations, and Food planet types provide zero population growth value to Riftborn. Redirect every slot that would be spent on Food to Industry.
RULE BREAK 2 ‚Äî VIRTUAL PLANETS (POCKET RIFTS)
Riftborn can build "Pocket Rifts" ‚Äî virtual extra planets that exist in a system without needing a physical planet slot. Pocket Rifts can be populated and specialized just like real planets but are constructed improvements rather than natural terrain. This means Riftborn can exceed the natural planet cap of any system by adding Pocket Rifts ‚Äî a unique population and FIDSI expansion path unavailable to any other faction.
RULE BREAK 3 ‚Äî COLONIZATION PREFERENCE
Since Food is irrelevant, Riftborn don't care about Fertile planets. Their colonization preference is entirely based on Industry potential (Lava/Volcanic planets) and planet count. An all-Lava system that would be mediocre for any other faction is ideal for Riftborn ‚Äî maximum Industry output fuels their manufactured population engine.
RIFTBORN PRIORITY ORDER
1. Maximize Industry above everything else ‚Äî it is both your ship-building AND your population-building resource simultaneously. Every Industry improvement is twice as valuable as for other factions.
2. Prioritize Lava/Volcanic systems on colonization, not Fertile/Jungle. Fertile systems with no Industry output are nearly worthless.
3. Build Pocket Rifts in high-Industry systems to expand their population cap and unlock more FIDSI potential. Each Pocket Rift can then be specialized for Science or Dust.
4. Queue population units during turns when your Industry is briefly idle between ship construction. Never let your Industry queue sit empty.

Umbral Choir ‚Äî The Possessors

No direct colonization. They possess other factions' systems and use them as their own ‚Äî completely invisible.
üëÅÔ∏è Core Mechanic: Possession Instead of Colonization MECHANIC ‚ñæ
‚óà UMBRAL CHOIR ‚Äî THE HIDDEN ONES
RULE BREAK 1 ‚Äî POSSESSION MECHANIC
The Umbral Choir's primary expansion mechanic is Possession: they can "possess" systems owned by other factions, including opponents, and extract a portion of those systems' FIDSI output as their own ‚Äî without the owner knowing. A possessed system continues to appear normal to the owner, who sees no change in their output (they don't lose anything visible). But the Umbral Choir receives a shadow income from those systems behind the scenes. One Umbral empire can feed off multiple opponents simultaneously.
RULE BREAK 2 ‚Äî SHADOW PRESENCE
The Umbral Choir maintains a fleet of invisible ships ‚Äî ships that cannot be detected by opponents on the galaxy map under normal circumstances. These shadow ships can orbit opponent systems to set up Possession, investigate curiosities without being seen, and conduct espionage actions without triggering the normal detection probability. Counter-hacking and advanced sensor techs are the only ways opponents can reveal Umbral ships.
RULE BREAK 3 ‚Äî LIMITED DIRECT COLONIZATION
The Umbral Choir can colonize systems normally, but their direct FIDSI from owned systems is intentionally weaker than other factions. Their power scaling comes from Possession income stacked across many opponent systems. A well-played Umbral Choir in a 4-player game might be receiving shadow income from all three opponents simultaneously ‚Äî total FIDSI extraction that no single traditional empire can match.
POSSESSION RISK: DETECTION
Possession is not risk-free. Opponents who invest in hacking counter-measures and high-level sensor tech can detect Umbral shadow ships orbiting their systems and force them to retreat. Each detected Possession breaks the income link for that system. The Umbral Choir must balance aggressive Possession (maximizing income) against the risk of detection ‚Äî prioritize possessing opponents who are not investing in Hacking Network defense.
UMBRAL PRIORITY ORDER
1. Build shadow ships and send them toward the richest opponent systems as early as turn 10‚Äì15.
2. Possess the highest-FIDSI systems first ‚Äî opponent Science hubs and Industry centers provide the best shadow income.
3. Maintain a minimum real military to deter direct attack ‚Äî opponents who discover you may retaliate militarily even if they can't see your shadow fleet.
4. Best victory paths: Science (Possession from high-Science opponents accelerates research) and Score (broad FIDSI extraction inflates total score). Conquest is difficult with limited real military.
