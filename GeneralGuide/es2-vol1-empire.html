FIDSI: How Your Resources Are Actually Calculated

The calculation order matters ‚Äî multipliers compound, and Approval hits everything last
‚öôÔ∏èThe 5-Step Calculation OrderMECHANIC‚ñæ

FIDSI (Food, Industry, Dust, Science, Influence) is not a simple sum of building outputs. Each resource is calculated in a specific sequence ‚Äî and understanding that order changes which improvements you prioritize.

  • STEP 1Base planet output √ó population. Each planet produces a base rate of each FIDSI per population unit. Planet type determines this: Lava ‚Üí heavy Industry; Arctic ‚Üí heavy Science; Fertile ‚Üí high Food. Actual output = base rate √ó number of pop units on that planet.
  • STEP 2Racial bonuses added. Each population unit's racial bonus is added to their per-unit output. A Sophon pop on an Arctic planet adds +1‚Äì4 Science/pop (depending on tech level) on top of the planet's base. This stacks per unit ‚Äî 5 Sophon pops on one planet = 5√ó the racial bonus before any building multipliers apply.
  • STEP 3Flat building bonuses applied. All "+X per pop" or "+X per planet" improvements are added here. These are additive with each other.
  • STEP 4Percentage multipliers applied. All "+X% FIDSI" improvements multiply the sum from steps 1‚Äì3. This is why percentage bonuses are much stronger in developed systems ‚Äî they multiply a larger base. On a system producing 5 Science, +20% = 1 Science. On a system producing 50 Science, +20% = 10 Science.
  • STEP 5Approval modifier applied last. The final output from steps 1‚Äì4 is multiplied by the system's Approval modifier: +20% at Elated, 0% at Content, ‚àí30% or worse at Angry. This hits all FIDSI simultaneously ‚Äî including your percentage bonus buildings. A system at Angry approval might effectively halve a system you spent 30 turns developing.
PRACTICAL IMPLICATION: BUILD ORDER MATTERS
Build flat-bonus improvements first to establish a large base, then layer in percentage multipliers once the base is high. A +20% Science building on a 5-output system gives 1 Science. The same building on a 40-output system gives 8 Science. Percentage multipliers are mid-to-late game investments, not early ones.
üò§Approval: the Hidden Multiplier That Silently Destroys Bad EmpiresMECHANIC‚ñæ

Approval is calculated per system, not empire-wide. Each system has its own Approval percentage that acts as a final multiplier on all FIDSI produced there (Influence is immune to Approval). The empire-wide HUD average hides that some systems may be at Angry while others are Elated.

THE CONQUERED SYSTEM TRAP
When you capture an enemy system, its Approval crashes ‚Äî often below 30%. All that Industry and Science is running at 70% efficiency or worse. Before using that system for anything, build 1‚Äì2 happiness improvements and assign a Counselor Hero. A high-level Counselor's Tier 4 skill forces system Approval to Ecstatic regardless of base penalties ‚Äî making freshly conquered systems immediately productive.

System Development: The Most Underused Mechanic

A permanent empire-wide multiplier hidden in the Economy screen ‚Äî most players never find it
üèóÔ∏èWhat System Development Is and How It WorksMECHANIC‚ñæ

System Development is a three-tier upgrade accessed via F3 (Economy Screen) ‚Üí System Development panel on the right side. It is not in any system's normal build queue. Each level is unlocked by advancing the Economy & Trade tech tree to the corresponding stage:

  • UNLOCKL1 (Basic) unlocks at Economy & Trade Stage 3. L2 (Advanced) at Stage 4. L3 (Endless) at Stage 5. You must push Economy & Trade to at least Stage 3 to access this at all ‚Äî another reason never to ignore that branch.
  • CHOOSEOnce unlocked, you open the panel and choose luxury resources to commit: L1 = 1 luxury type, L2 = 2 different types, L3 = 3 different types. This choice is permanent for the entire game. Hover each resource option to read its per-system bonus before confirming. The bonus type ranges from flat Industry, to per-pop FIDSI, to percentage multipliers, to Approval bonuses.
  • BUILDAfter confirming, every system in your empire gets a new queued improvement: "Level X Modernization." It costs 0 Industry and takes only 1‚Äì3 turns to complete. Queue it in all your systems simultaneously ‚Äî there is no reason to stagger. It also unlocks Spaceport slots for population movement between systems.
ECONOMIC VICTORY CONNECTION
Economic Victory requires accumulating 450,000 Dust total. System Development is one of the highest-leverage tools for this goal: a single well-chosen L1 bonus (e.g. +% Dust or large flat Dust/system) applied to 8‚Äì10 systems immediately adds hundreds of Dust per turn permanently. Reaching Economy Stage 3 fast and triggering System Development L1 as early as possible is the central strategy for Economic Victory runs.
üéØChoosing the Right Luxury ResourcesSTRATEGY‚ñæ

Since the choice is permanent, treat it like a faction-defining decision. Match to your victory path:

Your SituationBest Resource TypeWhy
Science Victory+% FIDSI or +Science/pop bonusesMultiplies research output across all systems simultaneously
Economic Victory+% Dust or large flat Dust/systemCompounds with Trade Company income multiplicatively
Rapid Conquest expansion+Approval resourceOffsets the approval hit from colonizing many systems fast
High-pop systems+X per PopulationScales linearly with every pop unit ‚Äî enormous in 5-planet systems
Industry-focused buildsLarge flat Industry bonusHits every system immediately, regardless of pop size
CHOOSE RESOURCES YOU ACTUALLY OWN
You must own at least 1 unit of the chosen luxury at selection time. The bigger risk is choosing a resource that an opponent can blockade or that you've already traded away your entire stock of. Always choose resources reliably generated within your own territory.

Planet Specialization: Per-Planet Roles

Specializations scale with population ‚Äî match them to a planet's natural strength, not the system's identity
üåçHow Planet Specializations WorkMECHANIC‚ñæ

Planet Specialization (the wrench icon when clicking an individual planet) grants a per-population bonus to that specific planet ‚Äî unlike system improvements, which apply to the whole system. Specializations scale directly with how many pops are on that planet and automatically upgrade as you research higher-tier versions.

RULE: AMPLIFY THE PLANET'S NATURAL STRENGTH
Match the specialization to the planet's highest base output. A Lava planet with high natural Industry output benefits enormously from an Industry spec. That same spec on a 2-Industry Arctic planet is nearly wasted. Always: Arctic/Tundra ‚Üí Science spec. Lava/Volcanic ‚Üí Industry spec. Fertile/Jungle ‚Üí Food spec. Planets sitting on strategic resource deposits ‚Üí Resource Extraction spec.
SCIENCE VICTORY CONNECTION
Science Victory requires researching 4 Endless Technologies across 4 different tech branches. Dust specializations on high-pop planets compound with Trade Company income for Dust, while Science specializations on Arctic planets directly accelerate the research required to reach Stage 5 in all four tech branches. Biasing your specializations toward Science on your best Arctic planets is the single highest-leverage action for a Science Victory run.

Trade Companies & Trade Routes

The passive Dust engine ‚Äî route length matters more than destination richness
üí±How Trade Company Income Is CalculatedMECHANIC‚ñæ

Trade Companies generate Dust (and some Science) through trade routes connecting an HQ system to Subsidiary systems via star lanes. The income formula compounds multiple factors:

FactorImpactAction
Route length (star lane hops)Very HighPlace Subsidiary as far from HQ as possible ‚Äî every system on the route contributes income
Population on route systemsVery HighHigh-pop systems anywhere along the path multiply route income
Trade Company levelVery HighLeveling the company (F3 ‚Üí invest Dust) multiplies all income ‚Äî highest ROI action available
Luxury resources in HQ/SubHighLuxury production in HQ and Subsidiary boosts Luxury income component
Dust Freighters purchasedMediumBuy after the company is leveled to at least 5 ‚Äî level matters more early
System Development levelMediumHigher-level systems produce more trade value at HQ and Sub locations
ROUTE DISRUPTION IS A STRATEGIC THREAT
Trade routes follow star lanes exactly and break when any system on the route is blockaded or captured. If an enemy cuts your trade routes, your Dust income collapses. Always defend HQ and Subsidiary systems with fleets. When planning routes, prefer paths that stay entirely within your territory.
üìçHQ Placement, Subsidiary Strategy & Network ExpansionSTRATEGY‚ñæ
  • 01Place HQ in your richest, most-developed system. The HQ system's Dust output, population, System Development level, and luxury resources all directly boost company income. Your home system is usually best in early-to-mid game.
  • 02Place Subsidiaries at maximum star-lane distance from HQ. Route length is the dominant income multiplier. Find the system in your empire requiring the most hops to reach from HQ ‚Äî that's your first Subsidiary. Second Subsidiary goes in the opposite deep direction.
  • 03Research "Galactic Security Charter" (Economy Stage 3) for +2 Subsidiaries. More Subsidiaries = more routes = more income. Research it as soon as your first company is running.
  • 04Sign Trade Agreements with peaceful neighbors. Routes can extend into partner territory, and their systems become valid endpoints ‚Äî doubling effective route length without you owning those systems. Best early-game income leverage.
  • 05Level the company before buying Freighters. In F3 ‚Üí Trade Company, invest Dust to raise company level. Early levels have the highest ROI. Always reach at least level 5 before spending on Freighters.
ECONOMIC VICTORY ‚Äî THE CORE MECHANISM
Economic Victory requires 450,000 total accumulated Dust. A well-managed Trade Company network at level 8‚Äì10 can generate 300‚Äì500+ Dust per turn. At that rate, Economic Victory is achievable by turn 130‚Äì160 even without optimal FIDSI. The key levers in sequence: (1) Get to Economy Stage 3 for System Development + Trade Company unlock. (2) Place HQ in best system, Subsidiaries at maximum distance. (3) Level companies aggressively. (4) Sign Trade Agreements with all non-hostile neighbors. (5) Use Pacifist party laws for +Dust bonuses. Track your cumulative Dust total in F3 ‚Äî Economic Victory is the only victory condition where you can calculate exactly how many turns remain.

Population Mechanics & Movement

Population is your primary FIDSI multiplier ‚Äî and once Spaceports unlock, it's actively deployable
üë•How Population Growth and Output WorkMECHANIC‚ñæ

Population is the multiplier for almost every FIDSI output ‚Äî a planet with 6 pop produces 6√ó its base per-pop rate. Growth is driven entirely by Food surplus: (Food produced ‚àí Food consumed per turn). Food consumed ‚âà 10 per population unit. When surplus fills the growth bar, a new pop unit is added to the highest-FIDSI available planet in the system.

Key rules:

  • CAPEach planet has a population cap by type and size. A small Lava planet caps at 2 pop; a large Fertile planet may cap at 6. Once a system hits total cap across all planets, Food surplus is wasted ‚Äî colonize more planets in the system or move pops out via Spaceports.
  • RACEEach pop unit has a race tag determining its per-pop bonus and preferred planet type. Sophon pops on Arctic planets add high Science/pop. Placing the right race on the right planet type is free FIDSI from racial preference bonuses.
  • ASSIMILATIONMinor faction pops (acquired through diplomacy) often have unique bonuses unavailable to major factions ‚Äî e.g. +Influence/pop or +Dust/pop on specific planet types. These are among the highest-value pop types in the game and should be grown deliberately on matching planet types.
üöÄMoving Population Between SystemsSTRATEGY‚ñæ

Once System Development L2 is built (Economy Stage 4), Spaceport slots unlock in your systems. Spaceports allow you to transfer population units between systems ‚Äî turning population from a passive resource into an active deployment tool.

  • HOWClick a system ‚Üí click the Spaceport slot ‚Üí select "Transport" ‚Üí choose a pop unit and a destination system. The pop moves in a few turns and begins generating FIDSI at the destination immediately.
  • USE 1Match race to planet type. Move Sophon pops to Arctic planets in your Science systems, Craver pops to Lava planets in your Industry systems. These racial preference bonuses are entirely free FIDSI from correct placement.
  • USE 2Move capped pops to developing systems. If your home system is at population cap, pops there are generating FIDSI but can't grow further. Moving some to a developing system with open planet slots immediately starts their FIDSI contribution there while also enabling growth.
  • USE 3Fix conquered systems rapidly. Move your high-value pops into freshly conquered systems to immediately boost their output, or move foreign pops out to reduce racial Approval penalties.
POP MOVEMENT CAUSES APPROVAL PENALTY
Moving a population unit into a system triggers an Approval penalty in the receiving system ‚Äî "immigrants" cause local unrest. Assign a Counselor Hero to the receiving system before transferring, or build Approval improvements first. The penalty is temporary (5‚Äì10 turns), but tipping an already-low system into Angry status collapses its FIDSI output at the worst possible moment.

Heroes: Classes, Leveling & Skill Investment

Heroes are your strongest economic and military lever ‚Äî if used in the right role, leveled correctly
ü¶∏The 4 Hero Classes and Their Correct RolesMECHANIC‚ñæ

Heroes come in 4 classes. The critical mistake is cross-training ‚Äî assigning a Guardian as a governor hoping for both fleet and system bonuses. A Guardian's fleet skills do nothing when governing, and vice versa. Match class to role strictly.

SENATE ROLE ‚Äî THE THIRD USE FOR HEROES
The highest-level hero of a political party can serve as that party's Senator when the party holds power. Senator heroes apply their governor/fleet skills as empire-wide passive bonuses ‚Äî not just to one system, but to all of them. A high-level Counselor Senator gives empire-wide Approval and Dust bonuses. This makes leveling Counselors a priority even if they never see combat.
‚¨ÜÔ∏èHow XP Works ‚Äî and How to Level Heroes FastMECHANIC‚ñæ

Governors level dramatically faster than fleet admirals. A governor gains XP every turn from the system's output, plus a large XP bonus whenever a system improvement or ship completes construction. A fleet admiral gains XP only from exploring or winning battles ‚Äî and only if the enemy holds their ground.

  • LEVELING TRICKTo level a combat-destined Guardian quickly, assign them as governor of your highest-Industry system first. They'll gain levels rapidly from construction completions. Once they reach your target level (8‚Äì10 for strong fleet bonuses), move them to the combat fleet. You'll have a well-leveled admiral in half the turns it would take from combat alone.
  • SKILL DISCIPLINEHeroes have 4 skill tiers unlocking at 4/8/12/16 skill points. Commit to one role from level 1. Investing any points in governor skills on a future admiral wastes the slots that would unlock better combat Tiers later. No exceptions.
  • WORKFLOWTurn 1: Assign starting hero as governor of home system. Turn 15‚Äì20: Hero is level 5‚Äì6 from construction XP. If they're a Guardian, move to combat fleet now; hire a Counselor replacement from the Academy. Turn 30+: 3+ heroes ‚Äî Guardian in fleet (full fleet skills), Counselor governing your best economic system, Overseer on your production system.

SCIENCE VICTORY ‚Äî TECH TREE REQUIREMENT
Science Victory requires 4 Endless Technologies from 4 different tech branches. All 4 Endless Technologies are only available at Stage 5 of their respective quadrants. You must reach Stage 5 in all four quadrants to win. This means Science Victory is the only victory condition that demands balanced tech tree progression ‚Äî you cannot afford to ignore any quadrant if this is your goal.
THE STAGE GATE TRAP
Unlocking Stage 3 requires 2‚Äì3 techs from Stage 2. Players who go deep in one quadrant while barely touching others arrive at mid-game unable to colonize Arctic planets, unable to build Large ships, and lacking System Development. Map the minimum Stage progression required across all quadrants for your victory path ‚Äî then don't fall behind on any of them.
üó∫Ô∏èEarly-Game Research Priority Order (Turns 1‚Äì40)STRATEGY‚ñæ
  • T1‚Äì5Economy & Trade Stage 1: Pick any tech to start accumulating Stage 1 progress. "Domestic Trade" gives early Industry bonuses; "Graviton Research" unlocks strategic resources. Both are fine starting points.
  • T5‚Äì15Military Stage 2 (Medium hulls): Your starting fleet is Small-hull only. Medium hulls have significantly more module slots and HP. Without this, you lose mid-game combat regardless of your other strategy.
  • T15‚Äì25Economy Stage 2‚Äì3: Getting to Stage 3 unlocks System Development L1 and Trade Companies. This is your economic turning point ‚Äî every turn delayed here is a turn your systems produce at unboosted rates.
  • T25‚Äì35Science & Exploration Stage 2: Colonization of Barren and Arctic planets ‚Äî common in the mid-galaxy and inaccessible without this. Also unlocks Tier 2 curiosity detection and investigation.
  • T35+Diverge based on victory path. Science ‚Üí push all quadrants toward Stage 5. Conquest ‚Üí Military Stage 3 (Large hulls) + Empire Stage 3 (Autonomous Administration). Economic ‚Üí Economy Stage 4‚Äì5 (System Dev L2/L3 + Trade Company expansion).
THE STAGE BOUNDARY BONUS ROW ‚Äî MOST PLAYERS MISS THIS
Between stage tiers in the tech screen, there are small icons representing free bonuses unlocked just by reaching that stage ‚Äî no separate building required. Reaching Economy Stage 3 automatically gives System Development L1 access even before building anything. Reaching Military Stage 3 gives a free fleet movement bonus. Hover the icons between stages to see what each boundary grants.

The Political System: Parties, Laws & Elections

6 parties, galaxy-wide laws every ~20 turns ‚Äî controlling this is a meaningful competitive edge
üó≥Ô∏èHow Parties, Votes & Laws WorkMECHANIC‚ñæ

6 political parties (Industrialists, Scientists, Militarists, Pacifists, Ecologists, Religious) each hold Senate seats based on empire-wide political support. The party with the most seats enacts an automatic law in effect between elections (roughly every 20 turns). The law grows stronger as the party stays in power: Established ‚Üí Entrenched. An Entrenched Industrialist law might give +15% Industry empire-wide ‚Äî a free bonus on par with multiple research upgrades.

Political support comes from:

  • POP TYPEEach population unit has a political ideology. Sophon pops ‚Üí Scientist support; Craver pops ‚Üí Militarist/Industrialist; minor faction pops ‚Üí varies. The more of a given pop type you have, the more that party is supported empire-wide.
  • SPECIALIZATIONSIndustry specs ‚Üí Industrialist votes; Science specs ‚Üí Scientist votes; Food specs ‚Üí Ecologist; Dust specs ‚Üí Pacifist; Influence specs ‚Üí Religious. This is the most controllable lever ‚Äî you can swing elections by changing your specialization focus over 10‚Äì15 turns.
  • HEROESHeroes have political leanings visible on their hero card. Assigning politically-aligned heroes as governors adds that party's support in that system, multiplied by hero level. A high-level hero significantly swings their system's political balance.

ECONOMIC VICTORY ‚Äî PACIFIST LAW
For Economic Victory, the Pacifist Entrenched law is a direct Dust income multiplier empire-wide. Combine it with Dust planet specializations, Trade Company investment, and System Development Dust bonuses for stacked compounding income. If the Pacifist law is active and Entrenched for 50+ turns, it alone can contribute tens of thousands of Dust toward the 450k requirement.
SWITCHING PARTY POWER

Typical arc: early game ‚Üí Industrialists (Industry builds fastest). Mid game ‚Üí Scientists (for Science Victory) or stay Industrialists (for Conquest). Late game ‚Üí Militarists (fleet capacity for large-scale war).
