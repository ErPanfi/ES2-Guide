ES2 // Fac.06 // The Unfallen

The
Unfallen

The galaxy's living root system. The Unfallen don't colonize — they vine. Their expansion is a slow, unstoppable organic network that doubles movement speed, locks out enemy territory, boosts allied economies, and enables instant colonization without ships or Industry. The faction looks peaceful and plays peacefully — until you realize the vine network is one of the most powerful strategic tools in the game, the Pacifist senate engine is printing FIDSI every turn you have friends, and the moment your vine frontier reaches someone, their planets are already half yours.

00

Faction Identity: The Network That Wins By Growing

Every mechanic rewards the same behavior — spread vines, make friends, keep the peace as long as profitable.
🌿 The Unfallen at a Glance OVERVIEW ▾
🌿 UNFALLEN — FAC.06

The Unfallen are a territorial network faction. Their vine system completely replaces standard colonization — instead of building colony ships and establishing outposts, they extend a web of celestial vines from their home system outward, and colonize vined systems instantly, for free, without consuming any ship. The vine network is not just an expansion mechanic: it defines their movement speeds, their territorial influence, their hacking efficiency, and the geographic boundaries of their military power.

Three separate systems all compound together around the same behavior — expanding the vine network and maintaining peaceful relations:

VINE NETWORK
Expansion, movement, territory, and hacking all flow through the vine graph. Wider network = faster travel, stronger economy, more territory.
GUARDIANS
Unique population type scattered across the galaxy on fertile planet types. Each one gives +5 Approval to every system in your empire (not just the one they're on), plus substantial Influence. Discovering more is a passive approval and influence engine.
PACIFIST FIDSI ENGINE
The Pacifist senate passive law gives +20% FIDSI empire-wide when Pacifist leads the party and you have peace/alliance treaties active. Described by expert players as the most powerful senate passive law in the game. Every friend you make multiplies your economy.
THE VINE CAPTURE RULE — CRITICAL
Any system you acquire — whether by siege, by minor faction quest, or by buyout — that is not connected to your vine network is instantly destroyed. You get nothing. This shapes every war decision you will ever make as the Unfallen. You can only profitably fight enemies whose systems touch your vine frontier.

The Vine Network: Core Mechanics

How vining works, vineship math, range rules, and the Vine Capture Rule.
🌱 How Vining Works MECHANIC ▾

Vining is the Unfallen's core action. Move a vineship into any star node within range, press the vine action (similar to probe exploration), and keep the fleet there for the required number of turns. Once the turn counter completes, the system is permanently vined — it stays vined even if you later lose the vineships.

  • PROGRESSVining builds up from 0% to 100% over the required turns. If interrupted — fleet moves, retreats, or is destroyed — progress decays back to 0% at roughly twice the rate it built up. A half-vined system is not half-safe.
  • PAUSEYou can pause mid-vine, add more vineships to the fleet, and resume immediately without losing any progress. This means an early slow vine can be accelerated mid-process by reinforcing the fleet.
  • FREE COLONYOnce a system is vined, you can instantly colonize any planet in it that you have colonization tech for. No ship in orbit needed. No Industry cost. No outpost phase. Instant full colony.
  • BLOCKEDYou cannot colonize a vined system if any enemy outpost exists in it. Siege enemy outposts down first. Friendly outposts from allied factions also block you until diplomatically resolved.
⚠️ THE VINE CAPTURE RULE
Any system you acquire by any method — siege victory, minor faction assimilation quest, buyout, praising to threshold — that is not connected to your vine network is instantly destroyed and removed from the galaxy. You gain nothing. This is not obvious, not well-documented, and has destroyed many Unfallen campaigns. Before declaring war or completing any minor faction assimilation, confirm the target system has the blue vine-outline in the galaxy view. If it does not, you cannot safely take it.

ROUNDING NOTE
The actual divisor is fractional — 3 ships gives 20/3 = 6.67 turns, which rounds up to 7. The game always rounds up, never down. Plan accordingly: 7 ships gives 20/7 = 2.86, which rounds to 3 turns — not 2. You need 8 ships to reliably achieve 2-turn vining (20/8 = 2.5, rounds to 3... except the wiki confirms 8 ships = 2 turns based on the 7.5 real threshold rounding up). The practical targets are 8 ships for 2-turn vining and 15 ships for 1-turn vining.

The pause-and-resume rule is strategically important: if you started vining with 3 ships and a fourth finishes construction mid-vine, merge the new ship into the fleet and the remaining turns immediately re-calculate at the new count. You do not lose progress. Conversely, if you split the fleet, vining stops entirely and progress decays.

📐 Vine Range & Special Nodes MECHANIC ▾

Vining range is 23 units from any already-vined node to the target system. Starlane connections are not required — any system within 23 units of your vine network is a valid target, including isolated systems not on any hyperlane. This range equals what a ship with speed 23 could travel in one turn.

STARLANE EXCEPTION
Systems directly connected to a vined system via starlane can always be vined regardless of the 23-unit distance rule. This matters for very long wormhole links where the visual distance may exceed 23 units.
SPECIAL NODE VINING
The Unfallen can vine non-planetary special nodes: Nebulae, Black Holes, and Asteroid Fields. When you do, the resource bonus from that node pipes to your home system only (not the nearest system). This effectively tags the node as owned territory and — critically — denies enemy factions access to that node. Vining a resource-rich Black Hole near a contested border is both an economic and a denial play.
TERRITORY REPLACEMENT
Vines replace the normal influence-radius circle that defines owned territory. A vined system is inside your territory boundary; an unvined system is outside it, regardless of proximity to your empire. This means Pacific Conversion eligibility is determined by vine coverage, not a circular radius. Systems you want to convert must first be vined.

🛡️ Vineship Economics: The Cost Trap STRATEGY ▾

Vineships are the Unfallen's colony ship equivalent — but they're permanently reusable (never consumed) and dramatically more expensive. The base hull cost is 400 Industry, plus another 100 for the Vine Beacon module itself. But the critical detail is the military tech multiplier:

VINESHIP COST FORMULA
Hull: ((Military Stage − 1) × 200) + 400 Industry
Module: +100 Industry (Vine Beacon)
Standard colony ship: ((Military Stage − 1) × 25) + 50 Industry
Stage 1: Vineship = 500 Industry vs Colony Ship = 75 Industry
Stage 2: Vineship = 700 Industry vs Colony Ship = 100 Industry
Stage 3: Vineship = 900 Industry vs Colony Ship = 125 Industry
⚠️ DO NOT RUSH MILITARY TECH
Every Military quadrant technology you research inflates vineship costs by 200 Industry. A Stage 3 military tech vineship costs nearly 1,000 Industry before the module. This is a permanent, non-negotiable tension in every Unfallen game: you need military tech to field competitive combat ships, but each advance makes replacing lost vineships more punishing. Delay military quadrant research until you have sufficient vine coverage or a genuine combat need.

The empty hull strategy: Because vineships are so expensive in Industry but the Unfallen tend to run Dust surplus, the standard technique is to build bare hull designs (no modules, no engine, minimum cost), then use Dust to upgrade the hull to a design with an engine and Vine Beacon after construction. This converts Industry cost into Dust cost — exactly the Unfallen's surplus resource.

⏱️ Intra-System Colonization: Fixed Turn Cost MECHANIC ▾

When expanding within an already-vined system — colonizing a second or third planet where you already have population — the Unfallen use a fixed turn cost instead of Industry, similar to a luxury-resource development project. The turn cost by planet type:

Planet TypeTurns (Normal Speed)Notes
Fertile1 turnForest, Terran, Jungle, Savannah, etc.
Non-Fertile / Non-Sterile2 turnsArid, Tundra, Boreal, etc.
Sterile3 turnsAsh, Lava, Desert, etc.
Gas Giant4 turnsRequires Gas Giant colonization tech

For brand-new systems, other factions typically spend 10–20 turns and 50–150+ Industry to colonize a second planet. The Unfallen spend 1–4 turns and zero Industry. The one downside: you cannot Dust-buyout planetary colonization. The turn cost is fixed and cannot be bypassed.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATION
In systems with high Industry and many population slots, this fixed turn cost is occasionally slower than what a highly developed colony could achieve with Industry. In practice, this edge case costs "one turn more than usual" at worst — and saves many turns at best in most new colonies. The asymmetry strongly favors the Unfallen across an entire game.

👥 Minor Faction Assimilation Gate MECHANIC ▾

The Unfallen must vine a minor faction's system before assimilation can be completed. You can interact with them (spend Influence, praise them, receive bonuses) before vining, but the final assimilation step — whether via quest or Dust buyout — requires the vine network to cover their system.

DESIGN INTENT
This is an intentional check against the Unfallen's enormous Influence output. Without this gate, the Unfallen would absorb almost every minor faction encountered before any other empire had a chance to compete. In practice, it adds roughly 5–20 turns of lead time depending on how far the minor faction is from your vine frontier — but this is manageable with proper vine routing.
THE VINE CAPTURE RULE (MINOR FACTIONS)
Completing a minor faction assimilation quest when their system is NOT vined destroys the system. You do not get the minor faction. You do not get their buildings or population. The system ceases to exist. Verify vine coverage before completing any assimilation.

🌀 The Wormhole Chain Exploit ADVANCED MECHANIC ▾
⚡ WORMHOLE CHAIN: INFINITE TRAVEL ACROSS VINED CORRIDORS

This is one of the most powerful and least-documented mechanics in the game. When a wormhole is vined, traversing it costs only half your remaining movement points instead of all of them. But there is a critical implementation detail:

Normally, wormhole travel sets your movement to exactly zero after transit. But for the Unfallen, wormholes subtract half your remaining movement rather than zeroing it out. Dividing any number by 2 never gives exactly zero. This means your fleet always retains a fractional movement value after a vined wormhole — even if the UI displays 0.

That fractional value (e.g., 0.45 moves) is enough to use another wormhole. And after that transit, dividing again still leaves a fraction. This cascades indefinitely along a chain of vined wormholes. The result: a fleet can traverse an entire chain of back-to-back vined wormholes regardless of starting movement speed, as long as every wormhole in the chain is vined.

Strategic implication: a vine network traced through wormhole corridors creates functionally unlimited travel range along that corridor. This acts as a long-range highway before wormhole technology is even researched, and remains one of the strongest mid-game mobility tools in the game.

🎯 Strategic Use of Vine Effects STRATEGY ▾

Most players understand that vines help movement. Fewer exploit the full portfolio of effects:

MOVEMENT AS POWER PROJECTION
Your vined starlane network effectively halves all transit times for your ships. During peacetime this means faster exploration and vineships reaching new systems in half the expected time. During war it means your response speed to threats is dramatically higher than the enemy's, even if their ships have the same base speed stats.
SLOWING ENEMIES DEFENSIVELY
Vining border systems in a defensive posture slows incoming enemy fleets. An attacker entering your space through vined starlanes moves at half speed — turning a 5-turn approach into a 10-turn approach and giving you time to intercept. This is passive, free, and invisible to players who don't know the rule.
SPECIAL NODE DENIAL
Vining Black Holes, Nebulae, and Asteroid Fields in contested regions denies their bonuses to all other factions permanently. Even if you'll never use a given node, vining it prevents the enemy from benefiting. The cost is just vineship travel time.
SHARING WITH ALLIES
The 2× movement bonus applies to allied ships on your vine network too. If you're in an alliance, your partner can use your vine highways to deploy forces rapidly — this is a meaningful diplomatic incentive to ally with the Unfallen and a strong argument for pursuing alliances aggressively.

This law occupies a law slot, but the correct pattern is not to keep it permanently active. Enable it when you need to negotiate a major peace treaty or complete expensive minor faction actions, then cancel it to free the slot. The cost discount is enormous but the need is infrequent.

The wiki notes inconsistent behavior with Guardian curiosities: sometimes exploring before colonizing works, sometimes the Guardian does not appear after colonization. To be safe, only count on receiving a Guardian after a planet is fully colonized. Do not make strategic decisions contingent on exploratory Guardians from uncolonized planets.

Traits, Senate & The FIDSI Engine

The Pacifist passive law and Mutual Understanding together make larger friend networks exponentially more powerful.
⚖️ The Right Finger Rule: Core Economic Multiplier STRATEGY ▾

When the Pacifist party leads the Senate, they activate a passive senate law — referred to by players as "The Right Finger Rule" or "Right Thing Rule" — that provides +20% FIDSI on all planets empire-wide, provided you have active peace or alliance treaties with other major empires.

THE PACIFIST FIDSI ENGINE

This is, according to expert Unfallen players, the single most powerful senate passive law in the game — stronger than the Ecologist colonize-all-worlds passive. A +20% FIDSI multiplier applied empire-wide compounds every industry improvement, every science bonus, every food production, and every Dust generator simultaneously. The larger your empire and the more developed your systems, the more absolute value this produces.

The Unfallen are uniquely positioned to exploit this: their population is almost entirely Pacifist-aligned, making Pacifist leadership near-permanent. And their diplomatic incentives (Harmonious II, Mutual Understanding I, Guardian Influence bonuses) all reward maintaining peace treaties. The pacifist gameplay loop and the FIDSI engine are designed to reinforce each other.

Expert play deliberately delays declarations of war to keep extracting this bonus as long as possible — even when war is intended. The FIDSI income from an extra 10–15 turns of peace can outweigh the economic gain from earlier conquest.

PACIFIST LEADERSHIP IS NEAR-PERMANENT — BUT NOT GUARANTEED
The Unfallen start with 2 Pacifist reps and 1 Ecologist in Senate. Unfallen and Guardian pops both push Pacifist support heavily. Getting Industrialist or Ecologist as the leading party requires either minor faction population diversity from assimilation or deliberate event manipulation. Panczasu confirms: it is easier to get Industrialist to lead than Ecologist. The game strongly guides the Unfallen toward permanent Pacifist rule — work with it, not against it.

Faction Quest

The Unfallen questline has no hero rewards — plan your hero acquisition strategy separately.
📜 Quest Structure & Reward Choices MECHANIC ▾

The Unfallen faction quest runs through several chapters. Early chapters present completion choices between quick, low-value rewards and longer, higher-value rewards. Expert advice consistently recommends preferring objectives that give technology or useful strategic bonuses over quick science gains from anomaly possession (the fast option typically grants a technology you may not want, which also inflates the cost of future research).

Chapter 1 — Choice Node
Fast path: Possess 2 anomalies → random tech reward (risky, may be useless).
Slow path: "Grow" objective → Red Sang luxury resource. Red Sang gives +Food, which is largely redundant for Unfallen. Either way the choice is low-stakes.
Chapter 2 — Choice Node
"Light" path: relatively easy, weak reward (Renewal System module — rarely useful on Unfallen ships).
"Fire" path: more demanding, rewards Coasa Kinetics — high-damage kinetics with critical hit chance. Panczasu rates this as clearly the correct choice.
Quest Completion Reward
Two module options: Heart's Shield (shield upgrade, redundant if you already have good shields) vs Heart's Hammer (short-range beam weapon, costs 2 Hyperium, does decent damage). Panczasu rates Heart's Hammer as marginally useful, Heart's Shield as redundant for most builds.
Post-completion: "Understand" path
After first quest completion a second quest opens. "Integrate" is described as near-impossible to fulfill. "Understand" completes near-automatically with normal play. Take Understand.
⚠️ THE UNFALLEN GET NO HEROES FROM THE FACTION QUEST
This is unique among major factions. Every other faction receives at least one hero as a faction quest reward. The Unfallen do not. This creates a chronic hero scarcity problem throughout the game. Plan around it explicitly: research General Services Exchange to unlock the hero marketplace, or build an Academy Embassy to accelerate hero availability. Do not assume a hero will arrive from the questline — it will not.

🌋 Fireforming: The Terraforming Payoff STRATEGY ▾

The Unfallen start with Planetary Landscaping pre-researched, giving them terraforming capability from the very first turn. The faction quest eventually unlocks Fireforming — an advanced terraforming capability that allows the Unfallen to convert planets into forest-type (fertile) planets, skipping intermediate terraforming steps.

FIREFORMING
Converts a snow or cold planet directly into a Forest planet — without requiring the standard multi-step terraforming chain. Described by players as the capability that makes Unfallen "the best terraforming faction in the game" in late-game. A barren system covered in snow planets can be transformed into a highly productive fertile system.
TERRAFORMING MECHANICS — CROSS-REFERENCE
For a detailed breakdown of terraforming types, chains, resource costs, and timing across all factions, see Vol.2 — Topic 5 of the General Guide.

The combination of instant colonization (no Industry cost), fast intra-system expansion (1–4 turns per planet), and fireforming means a mid-to-late-game Unfallen empire can systematically upgrade entire constellations from marginal to productive. The planet type no longer limits what a system can become.

🦸 Hero Management & Scarcity STRATEGY ▾

The Unfallen have a structural hero scarcity problem that no other major faction faces as severely. The faction quest awards zero heroes. Combined with natural hero unlock rates, this means the Unfallen can go a very long time with only their starting hero — Lunabarxes — available.

NO FACTION QUEST HEROES — PLAN AROUND THIS
Research General Services Exchange (Economy & Trade tree) to unlock the hero marketplace — this lets you purchase heroes from the market. Alternatively, build an Academy Embassy in one of your systems to accelerate the Academy's hero unlock cadence. Either path requires deliberate planning; neither is free.
HERO CLASSES FOR UNFALLEN
Admiral (Fleet assignment): Unfallen heroes have a unique faction ability granting fleet health regeneration. This means battles are cheaper to recover from — ships heal between engagements rather than limping to dock. Assign your primary hero as Admiral early and keep them there.

Seeker class: Lunabarxes is a Seeker. Seekers give bonus movement and fleet-level XP. Useful as scout commander in the early game, especially since Unfallen ships run off-lane paths regularly. Seeker is the weakest class overall but has a niche for Unfallen's explorer-heavy early game.

Overseer class: Panczasu's preferred mid-game target. Overseers boost strategic resource deposit values, which is valuable as Unfallen economy scales and Hyperium/Titanium demand increases for advanced ships.
WONDER LEVELING TIP
Always assign a hero to a system the turn you complete a Wonder. The Wonder completion XP is awarded to whatever hero is present as governor at that moment — the hero does not need to have contributed to its construction. Free, easy levels. Never complete a Wonder with an unassigned hero slot.

⚔️ Ground Combat: The Tank Composition Rule STRATEGY ▾

The Unfallen start with high Manpower (one of the stronger starting pools) and the Guardian ground battle damage bonus. Their systems are naturally more defensible. When invading, however, there is a critical troop composition rule that applies to all factions but matters more when you're the Unfallen planning aggressive vine-frontier wars:

TANK-ONLY INVADING FORCES
Once you have access to tanks, populate your invading fleets with tanks only — remove all infantry. Why: when infantry die in ground combat, the game automatically converts some of your surviving tanks into infantry replacements. This degrades your armor-heavy force mid-campaign without you choosing it. A pure-tank composition cannot degrade this way. Infantry do get shut down by some specific counter units (planet-side defenses in certain configurations), so a hybrid is sometimes necessary — but the default should be all-tank for most invasions.

The Unfallen's high starting Manpower (240) and Guardian +200 ground damage bonus mean they are genuinely difficult to invade early game. This defensive resilience buys time for vine network expansion during the critical first 15–20 turns.

First 30 Turns Playbook

Build the vine foundation before turn 10. Everything after that is acceleration.
🗓️ Opening Sequence STRATEGY ▾
TURN 1
Laws: Enact Super Tax Act (drops Approval slightly but gives Dust) + Toys for Boys (pushes to Ecstatic threshold). Democracy's 2-law start is the dividend here.
Hero: Assign Lunabarxes as system governor to level up via system builds. Optionally refit her ship with 2 probes + engine for early exploration.
Questioner: Refit with 2 probes if not already. Send toward nearest curiosities — Guardian curiosities are the highest priority.
Vineships: Send each toward candidate systems for early vining. Scout the constellation before committing vine direction.
Research: Xenolinguistics first (standard) — gives +30 Industry on fertile planets from Xeno Industrial Infrastructure improvement. Alternatively Xenobiology if you need Warp Drive access for a critical isolated system, or if Scientist party support is useful early for movement speed law.
T2–5
Exploration: Send Questioner and hero ship toward Guardian curiosities. Do not skip Guardians even if they're on non-immediately-colonizable planets — explore the curiosity and colonize later when you have the tech.
Vine routing: First vine target should be the richest adjacent system, but factor in connectivity — a central hub that enables vining in 3+ directions from one location is worth more than a marginal system. Avoid vining dead ends first.
Production: Build Drone Networks → Interplanetary Transport Networks. Prioritize Industry improvements on home system to reduce vineship construction time.
T5–10
First vine complete: Immediately colonize the best planet in the newly vined system. No Industry cost — do it the same turn the vine lands.
Vineship fleet: Aim to have 2–3 vineships in service by turn 10. 2 vineships = 10-turn vining; 3 = 7-turn. Target: send one fleet per direction to vine in parallel.
Research path: After Xenolinguistics, pivot to Xenobiology (+30 Science on fertile from Xeno Research Institute) → Public-Private Partnerships (unlocks efficient buyout chains).
Contact: Once you meet a major faction, open diplomacy immediately. Even a non-binding Peace Treaty starts the Pacifist FIDSI engine and Harmonious II Approval — Panczasu estimates this as +20% FIDSI on all planets, which is substantial by turn 10.
T10–20
Vine network: 3–5 systems vined with colonies placed. The vine snowball is beginning — each new vined system enables instant colonization of all planets within, accelerating population growth.
Colonize every eligible planet: Unlike other factions, colonizing an extra planet in a vined system costs zero Industry and 1–4 turns. Do not leave colonizable planets unclaimed.
Minor factions: Identify which minor factions are within vine range. Begin praising them (extends the praise modifier timer at each step). Do not complete assimilation until the vine covers their system — verify the blue outline.
Guardian sweep: With 3+ Guardians, the empire-wide Approval collection bonus is already +15. If Harmonious II is active with 2 peace treaties, home system Approval is likely already Ecstatic. Do not over-invest in Approval buildings.
T20–30
By turn 30 benchmark (4xalch): 5+ vined systems, 8+ population on strong systems, ~460 Dust/turn, ~800 Science/turn, +67 Influence/turn, point leader by ~36 points over Cravers.
Vineship scale: Aim for 5 vineships by turn 25 for 4-turn vining. The cost in Industry can be offset by the empty-hull/Dust-upgrade strategy. Each additional vineship is a permanent asset — they are never consumed.
Trade Companies: The Lumeris-style Trade Company Headquarters provides a significant Dust bump and can be timed for turn 28–32 with the standard research path.
War timing: Delay any declaration of war as long as the FIDSI engine benefit exceeds the economic gain from conquest. Panczasu holds peace with Lumeris until he has sufficient fleet strength and vine coverage — every extra turn of peace is +20% FIDSI. When you do declare war, ensure the vine frontier covers every system you intend to take.
THE UNFALLEN SNOWBALL
The faction has a slow first 8 turns (expensive vineships, limited Industry, one direction of vine coverage) and then an explosive middle game. Turn 15–30 is when the combination of instant colonization, vine movement speed, Guardian Approval stack, and Pacifist FIDSI engine all come online simultaneously. If you feel behind at turn 10, you are likely on schedule.

📌 Strategic Weaknesses & Counter-Play STRATEGY ▾

Understanding the Unfallen's weaknesses is as important as exploiting their strengths — and knowing the counters helps when you face them.

VINESHIPS ARE THE SOFT UNDERBELLY
Vineships are expensive (500–900 Industry depending on military stage), slow (limited module space for engines), and entirely undefended unless you escort them. They are the primary anti-Unfallen target. Attacking enemy vineships early — before their vine network is established — is the most effective way to contain an Unfallen player. As Unfallen, you must either escort vineships with a combat fleet or accept the risk of loss. Budget for replacement.
MILITARY TECH TENSION
Every Military quadrant advance inflates vineship costs by 200 Industry. You need military tech to build competitive ships; those same advances make your most important non-combat ship dramatically more expensive. This tension is permanent and unresolvable — it must be managed, not solved. Delay military tech until you have wide vine coverage and a stable construction industry.
SPARSE MAP / SMALL GALAXY WEAKNESS
Dense home constellation: strong start. Sparse galaxy: weak start. Isolated systems off the hyperlane network require off-lane vining, slowing spread. Small maps with few factions reduce Mutual Understanding bonus, reduce Right Finger Rule FIDSI impact, and reduce Guardian discovery opportunities. The Unfallen strongly prefer 8-player large maps.
GEOGRAPHIC WARFARE CONSTRAINT
The vine capture rule constrains all military operations to the vine frontier. You cannot rapidly respond to a distant threat, cannot exploit an enemy's exposed rear, and cannot meaningfully participate in wars far from your vine network. The Unfallen wage positional wars — slow, methodical, vine-first. Players expecting rapid decisive force projection will be frustrated.
COMPETING COLONIZERS — BLOCK EARLY
Factions with buyout colonization (Lumeris) or fast colony ships can plant outposts in systems you intend to vine before your vine arrives. Once an enemy outpost exists in a system, you cannot colonize it even after vining. Scout your intended expansion corridor and pre-vine contested systems before opponents can outpost them. Panczasu describes this as his primary early-game stress point against Lumeris specifically.
