ES2 // Vol.6 // The Lumeris

The
Lumeris

The Venetians of space ‚Äî merchants, brokers, and opportunists. The Lumeris generate more Dust than any faction in the game, convert that Dust directly into territory and ships, and turn Trade Company routes into Influence. They also have the most misunderstood identity in ES2: new players assume they are a peaceful economic faction. They are not. They are a snowball faction that wins by spending money faster than opponents can respond.

Faction Identity: What the Lumeris Actually Are

Their Pacifist party alignment is a trap. Read this before anything else.
üí° Core Traits and the Misunderstood Identity OVERVIEW ‚ñæ
‚óà LUMERIS ‚Äî THE BROKERS OF SPACE
PLANET BROKER ‚Äî COLONIZE ANYWHERE FOR DUST, NO SHIP REQUIRED
The Lumeris have no colony ship. Instead, they pay Dust to instantly place an outpost on any planet within their explored vision ‚Äî regardless of star lane connectivity. The first outpost costs 250 Dust, then 350, 650, and escalates steeply from there. Off-lane outposts grow slower than lane-connected ones but require no fleet positioning and cannot be physically blocked by opponents.
OPTIMISTIC ‚Äî THE STRONGEST LUMERIS TRAIT
Lumeris receive +20 Approval empire-wide passively. This is not a minor stat pad ‚Äî it is the foundation of their entire economic engine. Combined with basic happiness laws, it lets them maintain Ecstatic (+30% Dust and Science) across a larger empire than any other faction can sustain at equivalent size. It also buffers overcolonization penalties, allowing 1‚Äì2 extra colonies before hitting the same Approval threshold that would cripple another faction.
TRADE INCOME ‚Üí INFLUENCE ‚Äî THE HIDDEN ADVANTAGE
For Lumeris, Trade Company income generates Influence in addition to Dust. Longer trade routes therefore don't just make more money ‚Äî they directly power the Lumeris's Senate presence and diplomatic tools. This is the mechanic that ties their economic and political engines together.
BLOCKADE BREAKER ‚Äî SITUATIONAL
Lumeris can break blockades at 50 population empire-wide. In practice this is rarely the deciding factor in a game, but it prevents a single blockading fleet from entirely shutting down trade income during a war.
CRITICAL: LUMERIS ARE NOT A PEACEFUL FACTION
Their Pacifist starting party is a mechanical default, not a strategic directive. The Lumeris win by snowballing ‚Äî expanding aggressively early, spending Dust on territory faster than opponents can colonize conventionally, then converting their economic lead into military dominance before opponents reach their endgame power spikes. A Lumeris player without a strong fleet will be bullied into irrelevance. The AI identifies weak militaries and targets them specifically. Peace through superior firepower is the only sustainable peace.

The Dust Engine: Market, Resources & Inflation

Dust is your currency and your weapon ‚Äî but it also inflates, and mismanaging it destroys your own economy
üí∞ How the Dust Economy Works for Lumeris MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Lumeris Dust income has two distinct layers that must be understood separately:

  • LAYER 1Per-turn FIDSI Dust ‚Äî the number shown on your system screens. This scales with your Approval (Ecstatic gives +30% to all FIDSI), your planet specializations, luxury resources, and system improvements. The Optimistic trait's +20 Approval directly multiplies this income permanently.
  • LAYER 2Market income ‚Äî selling strategic and luxury resources on the galactic market. This is frequently the larger source of Dust in the early-to-mid game. A single haul of Hyperium or Titanium sold at peak prices can provide 1,000‚Äì1,400 Dust in one turn ‚Äî enough to place 2‚Äì3 outposts or buyout a major improvement instantly. Scout aggressively to find resource deposits; each one is a potential cash injection.
THE MARKET TIMING RULE ‚Äî SELL IN BATCHES OF 10‚Äì50
The galactic market uses a supply-demand curve: each unit you sell reduces the price of the next unit. Selling your entire Hyperium stockpile in one transaction tanks the price to almost zero. Instead, sell 10‚Äì50 units per turn across multiple turns to stay near the price peak. Use Shift+Click on a resource in the market to sell the entire stack instantly when you intentionally want to dump ‚Äî but do this only when the market is currently high on that resource and you want an immediate lump sum.
DUST INFLATION ‚Äî THE LUMERIS SELF-DESTRUCT MECHANISM
ES2 has a galaxy-wide Dust inflation mechanic: as more Dust is generated per turn across all empires, the purchase cost of improvements, ships, and outposts inflates. Because Lumeris are the highest per-turn Dust generators, they inflate the market faster than anyone else ‚Äî effectively raising prices on themselves. In the late game, buyout costs that were 1,000 Dust on turn 20 may be 8,000+ Dust by turn 80. Two consequences: (1) use buyouts early and aggressively before inflation compounds; (2) planet broker outpost costs (250 ‚Üí 350 ‚Üí 650 ‚Üí ‚Ä¶) escalate even faster than the base formula in high-inflation games. On max AI count, inflation can be severe enough to make buyouts impractical by turn 30.
DUST TREE LUXURY ‚Äî THE LUMERIS STAPLE RESOURCE
Dust Trees are the luxury resource most directly synergistic with Lumeris. Each Dust Tree grants bonus Dust per population unit on the system, and a system with multiple Dust Trees on fertile planets generates dramatically more Dust per pop than any improvement can provide alone. Prioritize colonizing systems with Dust Trees visible on planets. The system improvement that converts Dust Trees to "National Pride" (your designated luxury) is worth rushing if your capital or primary system has Dust Trees already.

Off-Piste Colonization: The Lumeris Expansion Weapon

Colonize systems that have no star lane connection to you ‚Äî this changes every expansion priority you have
üåç The Mechanic and Its Strategic Implications MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Every other faction must physically send a colony ship along star lanes to colonize a system ‚Äî this means systems deep in a neighbor's territory or isolated off-lane pockets are unreachable without warp tech or military force. Lumeris pay Dust and a system appears instantly, anywhere in their explored vision, no lanes required.

FOOD DRAIN ‚Äî THE CRITICAL CONSTRAINT
When a Lumeris outpost is placed, the game automatically routes Food from your nearest connected system to feed the outpost's growth. This drain is significant: ~32 Food per turn per outpost, deducted from the source system's surplus. If your capital has 40 Food surplus and you place two outposts simultaneously, your capital goes negative ‚Äî population begins shrinking immediately. The correct response: only place a second outpost when your largest food-producing system can sustain the drain without going negative. Alternatively, place off-piste outposts (isolated by star lane gaps) ‚Äî these grow slower but do not pull Food from your connected systems, eliminating the drain entirely.
TREE OF WORLDS ‚Äî THE OFF-PISTE ACCELERATOR
A system containing a "Tree of Worlds" planetary anomaly provides enormous bonus Food to its outpost growth ‚Äî so much that a Tree of Worlds outpost can mature to full colony in 5‚Äì6 turns regardless of how much Food your capital is sending. When you find a Tree of Worlds system, colonize it immediately regardless of its other stats. The fast growth alone pays for the outpost cost within a few turns of the colony becoming fully productive.
STRATEGIC USES OF OFF-PISTE
Beyond the obvious (colonizing isolated systems), off-piste colonization has three tactical applications:

1. Claim resource deposits before opponents reach them by lane. An opponent must travel 5 star lanes to reach a Hyperium system; you can claim it the turn you probe it if you have the Dust.

2. Establish a forward outpost inside or adjacent to an opponent's territory. The outpost generates resource income immediately and forces the opponent to respond diplomatically or militarily. Lumeris selling systems they don't want can also be used as a diplomatic tool.

3. Claim the Academy system ‚Äî the Galactic Academy often appears in isolated locations. Lumeris can frequently claim it before any other faction if they've scouted it and have the Dust. The Academy's hero recruitment advantage is enormous.
OUTPOST TIMING: THE MERCHANTS AND MONEY TECH
The "Merchants and Money" improvement (Economy tech tree) doubles the natural growth speed of all outposts. Its cost in Industry scales with the number of colonies you own ‚Äî at 1 colony it costs ~150 Industry; at 2 colonies, ~250; at 3, ~350. If you plan to place multiple outposts in quick succession, build Merchants and Money at 1 colony, before the price escalates. Every outpost you ever place will benefit from the doubled growth rate for the entire rest of the game.

Trade Companies, Routes & Influence

5 companies instead of 3, and routes convert to Influence ‚Äî two advantages that compound throughout the game
üè¢ How Trade Works for Lumeris and What Breaks It MECHANIC ‚ñæ

The Trade Company system is covered in depth in Vol.1. For Lumeris, two modifiers change the calculus significantly compared to the generic guide:

5 COMPANIES ‚Äî THE LUMERIS STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGE
All other factions cap at 3 Trade Companies. Lumeris can operate 5 simultaneously. Since each Company's income scales with its level, and leveling is fueled by the Dust circulating through the system, a fully developed 5-Company Lumeris empire generates Trade income in a completely different tier from any competitor. Build toward all 5 as fast as your Dust and Industry allow ‚Äî but level each Company to at least 4‚Äì5 before building the next. A Level 7 Company generates far more per turn than two Level 3 companies combined.
ROUTE LENGTH = INFLUENCE ‚Äî THE DUAL PAYOFF
For Lumeris, Trade Company income splits into Dust and Influence. Longer routes produce more of both. This means: (a) signing Trade Agreements with peaceful neighbors isn't just about Dust ‚Äî it directly extends route length and increases your Senate seat count; (b) systems far from your core territory that would be marginal economic colonies are more valuable as Subsidiary endpoints because they extend route length; (c) your diplomatic posture directly funds your political engine ‚Äî keeping neighbors friendly is economically mandatory, not just diplomatically pleasant.
TRADE ROUTE VULNERABILITY ‚Äî YOUR BIGGEST MILITARY LIABILITY
Each Trade Company route follows fixed star lane paths between your HQ and Subsidiaries. An enemy fleet blockading any system along a route breaks the entire route's income immediately. A single opponent fleet at a chokepoint in your trade network can eliminate 30‚Äì40% of your per-turn Dust income. This is why Lumeris must maintain strong patrol fleets along their core trade arteries ‚Äî not just at borders. An opponent who understands Lumeris will target trade chokepoints, not your strongest systems.
HQ PLACEMENT RULE
Place your Trade Company HQ in your single highest-Industry system ‚Äî not your highest-Dust system. The HQ's construction and leveling cost is paid in Industry. Once built, the HQ's income comes from the routes, not from the system's base FIDSI. Place Subsidiaries at the furthest reachable systems from the HQ (through peaceful neighbors' territories via Trade Agreement) to maximize route length income.

THE THREE BREAKPOINTS ‚Äî 30, 70, 85
Approval is not a sliding scale ‚Äî it has discrete tiers. Being at 69 is mechanically identical to being at 31. The thresholds that matter are:

Above 85 ‚Üí Ecstatic: +30% to all FIDSI including Dust. This is the target. For Lumeris, the +30% Dust bonus at Ecstatic is larger in absolute terms than for any other faction because your Dust base is higher.
70‚Äì84 ‚Üí Happy: +15% FIDSI. Acceptable but suboptimal ‚Äî you're leaving ~15% Dust on the table.
Below 70 ‚Üí Content or worse: No bonus or active penalties. Never let Lumeris drop here ‚Äî the Dust income loss compounds across every system simultaneously.
HOW MANY COLONIES CAN YOU HAVE?
The Optimistic +20 Approval effectively gives Lumeris 1‚Äì2 free extra colonies compared to other factions before hitting the same Approval threshold. The exact limit depends on your luxury resources and happiness improvements, but a practical target is: maintain Ecstatic up to 5‚Äì6 systems, then evaluate per colony whether the new system's FIDSI gain outweighs the Approval drop across your whole empire. When in doubt, use the TAKT principle (see section 12): don't expand if the Approval drop will cut your Ecstatic bonus by more than the new colony produces.
THE APPROVAL RECOVERY TOOLKIT
When overcolonization pushes you below Ecstatic, your recovery options in order of speed:
1. Enact "Toys for Boys" law ‚Äî applies +25 Approval empire-wide for 10 turns. Fast and free if Scientists are in power.
2. Luxury resource improvements ‚Äî "Dust Trees as National Pride" and similar improvements give permanent per-pop Approval bonuses in that system.
3. Sell an outpost ‚Äî the planet broker works in reverse. Selling an underperforming outpost removes its overcolonization penalty immediately and refunds Dust. Late game refunds can reach 20,000 Dust. Use this actively, not just as an emergency measure.

Elections, Laws & the Industrialist Pivot

Pacifists start in power ‚Äî but Industrialists win games. How and when to make the switch.
üèõÔ∏è Party Dynamics and the Turn 20 Election MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Lumeris are anti-Militarist by racial default ‚Äî their population votes Pacifist and resists Militarist parties. This is covered in the general guide (Vol.1). The Lumeris-specific politics chapter is about why you need to override this and how.

WHY INDUSTRIALISTS, NOT PACIFISTS
Pacifist entrenched law gives Dust and Approval bonuses ‚Äî appealing for a Dust-focused faction. But Industrialist entrenched law reduces ship and improvement Industry costs by a meaningful percentage. For Lumeris, this is more valuable because:

(1) You buyout ships with Dust, not Industry. But lower Industry costs mean improvements complete faster, freeing Industry for more improvement spam, accelerating system development, and compounding your FIDSI output faster.
(2) Industrialist law enables the questline continuation path ‚Äî the Lumeris faction quest has a critical branch requiring Industrialist dominance for several turns. Arriving at that requirement without Industrialists in power forces you to scramble an expensive last-minute election campaign during a quest deadline.
(3) The Ecologist party's entrenched law (+25 Industry when at Ecstatic) is also a strong secondary option and plays well with the Lumeris Approval advantages ‚Äî consider it if you can't win an Industrialist majority cleanly.
HOW TO WIN THE INDUSTRIALIST ELECTION
Turn 20 is the first major election. The steps to secure an Industrialist victory:

From Turn 10 onward: Spend Dust lobbying for Industrialists. Lobbying costs increase each time and reset each election cycle ‚Äî invest incrementally rather than in one burst. Budget ~200‚Äì300 Dust for pre-election lobbying.

Turn 18‚Äì20: Check the election forecast screen. If Industrialists are at or near 50%+, the standard population vote plus your lobbying should secure the seat. If they're below 40%, spend a larger Dust lump to push them over before the vote resolves.

Assimilated minor factions vote for their faction's preferred party. The Mavros minor faction (if assimilated) votes Scientist ‚Äî helpful for the Science branch but competes with Industrialists. Hissho vote Militarist. Manage your assimilation timing to avoid accidentally flooding your electorate with unfavorable votes before a critical election.
USEFUL LAWS FOR LUMERIS BY GAME PHASE
Early game (turns 1‚Äì30): "Trusted Broker" (cheaper minor faction bribes), "Dirty Hands Act" (boosts Science for early tech).
Mid game (turns 30‚Äì60): "Windfall" (Industrialist law, converts surplus Industry to Dust), "New Colony Boost" (+30% outpost growth when placing new colonies ‚Äî activate immediately before mass colonization turns).
Late game (turns 60+): "Adaptive Taxes" (massive Dust gain scaling with luxury resources), "Political Theater" (+75% of Industry as bonus production empire-wide).

Minor Factions: Investment, ROI & the Curiosity Bribe Loop

100 Dust in a minor faction on turn 3 can return 18+ resources per turn within 10 turns ‚Äî the best early-game ROI available
ü§ù Minor Faction Investment Math and the Curiosity Interaction MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Minor factions (covered in Vol.0) are covered here from the Lumeris-specific angle: Dust generation makes investing in them cheaper per turn relative to income than for any other faction, and the curiosity interaction reduces bribe costs significantly.

THE CURIOSITY-BRIBE INTERACTION
Every curiosity you investigate in a minor faction's home system increases your relationship with them by a flat amount ‚Äî and simultaneously reduces the Dust cost of the next bribe tier. The sequence:

Bribe cost before curiosities: 25 ‚Üí 75 ‚Üí 125 Dust per tier.
After investigating 3 curiosities in their system: 25 ‚Üí 75 ‚Üí (jump is smaller or free). Investigating 3 curiosities before paying any bribes can save 50‚Äì100 Dust across the relationship arc. Always investigate curiosities first, then bribe ‚Äî never bribe before investigating.
INVESTMENT ROI ‚Äî ACTUAL NUMBERS
From the expert playthrough: 100 Dust invested in the Mavros minor faction on turn 5 returned 6 Dust + 8 Science per turn by turn 9 (4 turns √ó ~3.5/turn = 14 Dust recovered, then ongoing). By turn 14, the investment was fully repaid and generating net 18 Dust + Science per turn. With "Trusted Broker" law reducing bribe costs by ~25%, initial investment payback drops to ~3 turns. Every minor faction reached "Friendly" status provides:

‚Äî Resource trickle per turn (Dust, Science, or Food depending on faction)
‚Äî A free population unit of their race delivered to your nearest system (see Vol.0)
‚Äî Curiosity investigation access (their system's curiosities become available)
TOP MINOR FACTIONS FOR LUMERIS
Mavros: +Industry integration trait (‚àí10% Industry cost on all improvements), extra Science per individual pop, extra Industry per individual pop. One of the strongest assimilation targets in the game.
Hissho (minor variant): +20% weapon module damage on integration. Useful if you're building a military-heavy mid-game.
Any minor faction with Dust/Science per pop bonuses ‚Äî these stack with Lumeris's already-high per-system Dust output and compound with Ecstatic Approval multiplier.
ASSIMILATION COST SCALING
Assimilating a minor faction counts as adding a colony for overcolonization purposes. If you're already near your Approval soft cap, assimilating a minor faction may push you below Ecstatic. Assimilate before expanding to new planetary systems, not after ‚Äî the minor faction assimilation is cheap relative to colonizing a new planetary system, and it adds production capacity without the full Approval hit of a new colony.

Questline & Victory Paths

Lancelum (4 planets) is the correct quest path. Every other branch is harder or conditional.
üìú Quest Branches and Victory Type Recommendations MECHANIC ‚ñæ
QUESTLINE: ALWAYS TAKE LANCELUM
The Lumeris faction quest offers multiple starting branches. Lancelum (requires controlling 4 planets) is consistently the correct choice:

‚Äî 4 planets is achievable by turn 15‚Äì20 in a normal game
‚Äî Other branches require assimilating a specific minor faction (unreliable ‚Äî AI can steal it) or surveying 50%+ of the galaxy (turn-intensive and map-dependent)
‚Äî Lancelum leads into the Eco vs. Industry branch choice, both of which have viable rewards

Eco branch followup: Requires controlling 3 minor or major faction home systems ‚Äî potentially difficult or impossible depending on map. Evaluate before committing.
Indy branch followup: Requires 3 large planet types (Desert/Ash/Lava) ‚Äî also map-dependent but more within your control via colonization targeting.
Final reward: Both branches eventually offer a unique upgrade. The military buyout option (allows buying out ships instantly with Dust during battle) is the default best choice ‚Äî it transforms the Lumeris's Dust wealth into real-time tactical flexibility.
VICTORY PATH RECOMMENDATIONS
Economic victory exists in theory but is effectively blocked in practice ‚Äî other factions (particularly Unfallen with their +15% Dust/Science per ally bonus) reach the 450,000 cumulative Dust threshold faster. The Lumeris Trade Company cap advantage helps but doesn't compensate entirely for the time cost.

Best paths for Lumeris:
Conquest ‚Äî use the early territorial expansion advantage. Claim 40%+ of systems by turn 50 by combining off-piste colonization with aggressive military action against weaker neighbors.
Score ‚Äî the broadest development path suits the Lumeris's wide-expansion playstyle. Ecstatic Approval across many systems produces high score efficiently.
Wonder ‚Äî achievable if you maintain Ecstatic Approval and Industrialist law for the Empire Development chain. High Industry + buyouts make Obelisk construction fast.
Supremacy ‚Äî requires a strong military push late game, but the Dust buyout ability (from questline) lets you cash-rush an Obliterator or combat fleet when needed.

Fleet Philosophy: The Swarm Economy

Lumeris don't out-design opponents ‚Äî they out-fund them. Numbers win, and numbers can be bought.
üöÄ The Quantity Doctrine and Dust Buyouts MECHANIC ‚ñæ
THE SWARM DOCTRINE
Lumeris have no inherent fleet bonuses ‚Äî no damage multipliers, no special module unlocks, no faction-specific battle card beyond what their ships provide. Their fleet strength comes entirely from quantity funded by Dust. The correct doctrine: build more ships than opponents can respond to, not better ships. A fleet of 6 average Hunters bought with Dust in 3 turns beats a fleet of 3 excellent Hunters that took 15 turns of Industry to produce ‚Äî because the Lumeris fleet is already deployed and winning territory while the opponent is still in the queue.
DUST BUYOUTS ‚Äî THE TACTICAL MULTIPLIER
Any ship or improvement can be completed instantly by paying its remaining Industry cost in Dust. For Lumeris, this transforms their economic advantage directly into military readiness:

Reactive buyout: An opponent declares war unexpectedly. You have 4 partially-built ships and 3,000 Dust. Buyout all 4 in one turn and meet them with a full fleet instead of an empty space lane.
Pre-battle buyout: Before initiating a war, queue 5‚Äì6 ships and let them build for 3‚Äì4 turns. On the turn you declare, buyout everything simultaneously ‚Äî opponent's scouts see no fleet buildup until the fleet appears fully formed.
Questline military buyout (final quest reward) allows buying out ships during combat ‚Äî reinforcements appear mid-battle. This is Lumeris's most powerful late-game military tool.
INDUSTRY IS STILL REQUIRED
Buyouts don't eliminate the need for Industry ‚Äî they accelerate the conversion of Industry work into ships. A system producing 50 Industry/turn that builds a 300-Industry ship will need 2,000 Dust to buy it out on turn 1 vs. 1,500 Dust on turn 3 vs. 1,000 Dust on turn 5. Letting ships build partially before buying out is always more Dust-efficient than buying out immediately. Only full-instant-buyout when time pressure demands it. Maintain dedicated Industry in 1‚Äì2 shipbuilding systems even as a Dust-focused empire.

Lumeris hulls are among the best in the game when upgraded ‚Äî but the carrier is the exception, and small hulls become obsolete
üîß Hull-by-Hull Analysis and Module Priorities MECHANIC ‚ñæ

The general Vol.2 ship role framework (Hunter, Coordinator, Protector) applies to Lumeris. What follows is Lumeris-specific hull analysis based on cross-faction comparisons and module slot counts.

ATTACKER (SMALL)
2 weapon + 2 defense + 1 utility when unupgraded. Decent early survivability for pirate clearing and minor faction coercion. After upgrade: gains a 4th defense slot and extra utility ‚Äî becomes one of the two best attackers in the game, rivaling Riftborn. Use early, then replace with Hunters in mid-game. Do not bother upgrading ‚Äî invest that research in Hull tiers instead.

PROTECTOR (SMALL)
6 modules ‚Äî 1 more than typical protectors. Top-mounted weapon gives 270¬∞ arc vs. standard 120¬∞ broadside. Can go head-to-head with many attacker designs. One of the two most damaging protectors in the game. Useful early; transition to Coordinator support in mid-game but keep 1 in each fleet for its Flak and damage absorption.
OMAHA-CLASS
HUNTER (MEDIUM)
The core of your fleet. 9 modules unupgraded ‚Äî already top 3 among all faction hunters. After upgrade: the most modules of any hunter in the game (+4 modules including a large top slot). Scales with tech level ‚Äî the better your modules, the more this hull pays off. A base Hunter is worth approximately 4√ó an Attacker for 3√ó the command points. Never skip this hull.
LONG CALLUM-CLASS
COORDINATOR (MEDIUM)
After upgrade: the tankiest coordinator in the entire game. 4 extra modules on upgrade (more than any other coordinator). Its role: bullet sponge protecting your Hunters from enemy carriers. Equip with Flak + defense modules, not weapons. Do not upgrade unless you intend to use it as a troop transport (many utility slots when upgraded make it excellent for ground invasion support). Generally, skip the carrier tech and invest in Hunter upgrades instead.
ERA-CLASS (CARAVEL)
EXPLORER
No colony ship exists for Lumeris ‚Äî the Caravel fills an outsized early-game role. Add extra engines and probe modules as priority. Late game: refit with maximum engine modules and deploy as forward scouts deep in enemy territory. Cheap enough to lose, fast enough (12+ movement with engine mods) to give advance warning of incoming fleets. 1‚Äì2 late-game scout Caravels are worth keeping indefinitely.
HULL SIZE VS. MODULE QUALITY ‚Äî THE KEY INSIGHT
Each module on a Hunter hull is worth approximately 2√ó the same module on an Attacker hull in effective combat value. Heavy Weapon mounts on Hunters are 4√ó the equivalent Attacker slot. This means: a Tier 2 weapon module on a Hunter hull outperforms a Tier 2 module on an Attacker hull by a factor of 2 in combat impact. Skip upgrading small hull tech entirely ‚Äî invest the research in reaching Hunter hulls instead. Once you have Hunters, small hulls become cheap early-game stopgaps only.

Weapons, Defense & Battle Card Strategy

Mix kinetics and missiles early. Switch to laser-dominant mid game. One kinetic slot always, for counters.
‚öîÔ∏è Weapon Tier Progression and Defense Split MECHANIC ‚ñæ

Weapon system mechanics are covered in Vol.2. This section covers the Lumeris-specific weapon progression and the reasoning behind it.

Game PhasePrimary WeaponSecondary SlotReasoning
Early (Tier 1‚Äì2) Kinetic Slugs Missiles Slugs + missiles do the most raw damage in Tier 1‚Äì2. Missiles are countered by slugs ‚Äî so having your own kinetics means you both deal missile-level damage AND counter the opponent's missiles. Use Turtle battle tactic (long range) to maximize missile accuracy while your slugs handle the mid-range fallback.
Mid (Tier 3+) Lasers (primary) 1√ó Kinetic Slug Lasers deal nearly 2√ó the damage of Beam weapons, cost fewer strategics, and excel at medium range. As long as at least 1 of the 3 combat phases hits medium range, lasers outperform beams overall. Keep one kinetic slug slot to counter enemy missiles, boarding pods, and squadrons ‚Äî removing these threats is worth more than the marginal extra laser slot.
Late (Tier 4‚Äì5) Lasers + Railguns Boarding Pods / Swarm Missiles Shield Interference Beam (Tier 4 hero tech) disables enemy shields ‚Äî activate before your laser barrage. Railguns penetrate armor. Swarm missiles overwhelm flak. Boarding pods capture enemy ships intact. At this stage, fleet composition matters more than individual weapon choice.
DEFENSE MODULE SPLIT ‚Äî ALWAYS HALF/HALF
When filling defense slots, split evenly between hull plating and energy shields. Both types have diminishing returns the more you stack of the same type ‚Äî stacking 4 hull plating slots provides less total damage reduction than 2 hull + 2 shield. If you have an odd number of defense slots and must choose, favor hull plating: most ships have higher base HP than shield value, so percentage reductions on hull provide larger absolute damage absorption.
CROSS-LANE WEAPON CONSIDERATION
In the 3-lane combat system, ships on the top or bottom lanes firing at the opposite lane (top vs. bottom) always use long-range accuracy regardless of the actual combat phase. Having at least one long-range weapon (missile or the hybrid missile/kinetic setup from early game) means that if your fleet wins one lane cleanly, those ships can still support other lanes at reduced but non-zero accuracy. Pure short-range weapon loadouts become dead weight in won lanes.

Hero Management

The Lumeris starting hero is weak. Replace it as soon as possible. Here's how to manage the transition.
ü¶∏ Starting Hero Assessment and Early Leveling MECHANIC ‚ñæ
THE STARTING HERO IS BAD ‚Äî HONEST ASSESSMENT
The Lumeris starting hero (Lumeris/Seeker type) has weak ability trees by faction-hero standards. The top-tier skill for the Lumeris hero line converts trade route income into additional Dust and Industry ‚Äî an ability that sounds thematic but is "pathetic and utterly meaningless" in practice (Steam Guide). The Seeker line's +20% fleet damage is the best combat option but remains below what Sophon or Vodyani heroes provide at equivalent tiers.

The one genuinely valuable Seeker trait: off-lane movement ‚Äî the hero's fleet can move outside star lanes, enabling flexible positioning, surprise attacks, and reaching isolated systems without tech investment.
EARLY HERO USE ‚Äî EXPLORATION XP FARMING
Assign the starting hero to your Caravel fleet on turn 1. Send them to investigate every curiosity in reach ‚Äî each investigation grants hero XP. A hero that investigates 8‚Äì10 curiosities in the first 10 turns reaches Level 3‚Äì4 before most factions' heroes hit Level 2. At Level 3, assign the hero as your home system Governor for the +30 Science per turn bonus (the "Blue Skies" speculator skill). The timing of reassignment matters: you must wait for the assignment cooldown before you can move them from the fleet to a system role ‚Äî plan for the cooldown in your turn scheduling.
ACADEMY ‚Äî GET A BETTER HERO AS SOON AS POSSIBLE
The Galactic Academy appears mid-game and allows recruiting heroes from all factions. Lumeris should prioritize finding and claiming the Academy system via off-piste colonization if possible ‚Äî the Academy's hero pool often contains Sophon heroes (+Science per turn as Admiral or Governor) or military-specialist heroes that dramatically outperform the Lumeris default lines. Recruit a military-capable hero (Admiral class) as soon as the Academy is operational to compensate for the weak Lumeris starting hero in fleet combat.
HERO SLOT EFFICIENCY ‚Äî DON'T WASTE ENGINE SLOTS
When a Guardian Hero (movement specialist) leads a fleet, do not fill ship module slots with extra engines ‚Äî the Guardian's passive movement bonus already maximizes speed. Use those slots for a damage amplifier or targeting module instead. For non-Guardian fleet heroes, one engine per key ship is sufficient; stacking engines beyond that is a waste of offensive potential.
COUNSELOR HERO PRIORITY ‚Äî OCCUPATION MANAGEMENT
If you're pursuing Conquest and regularly invading systems, recruiting a Counselor hero (system governance specialist) is higher priority than a combat Admiral for your second hero slot. The Counselor's Tier 4 governance skill forces Ecstatic Approval on any system they govern ‚Äî transforming freshly conquered systems from Approval drains into productive colonies within 2‚Äì3 turns. This is covered in Vol.2; the Lumeris application is that the Dust value of recovering Ecstatic quickly on a new colony frequently exceeds the combat value of a second Admiral.

First 30 Turns: The Lumeris Playbook

A turn-by-turn framework ‚Äî not a fixed script, but a set of priorities to hit in order
üóìÔ∏è The TAKT Concept and Opening Priorities MECHANIC ‚ñæ
THE TAKT PRINCIPLE

Don't research or spend on anything you can't use immediately. If you're 10 turns away from using a technology, those Science points spent now are Science points that could have accelerated something you are using right now. The same applies to Dust: spending 250 Dust on a new outpost when your capital can't support the food drain for 5 more turns is 250 Dust that inflated the market and bought you nothing. Timing resources to need ‚Äî arriving at each tech, each improvement, each colony exactly when you can deploy it ‚Äî is the single highest-skill concept in high-level Lumeris play. It applies to every faction but matters most for Lumeris because their Dust is their action economy: every misplaced Dust is a missed colonization, a missed buyout, a missed bribe.

TURN 1
Attach hero to Caravel fleet. Do not keep hero as solo ship ‚Äî attach to fleet so they gain XP from curiosity investigations. Refit the Caravel: remove one weapon module, add a second probe launcher (costs ~40 Dust ‚Äî worth it for the curiosity coverage). Check home system approval: if above 70, defer the first law for now and save Influence. Queue Xeno-Industrial Infrastructure in the home system build queue ‚Äî this is first-build for every Lumeris game without exception. Start researching Xenolinguistics as your first tech (unlocks Xeno-Industrial, boosts early Science).
TURNS 1‚Äì5
Scout aggressively for range, not curiosity depth. Cover as many star lanes as possible to map colonization targets. Specifically look for: systems with 4‚Äì5 planets, strategic/luxury resource deposits (especially Hyperium and Titanium), and minor faction locations. Investigate curiosities in minor faction systems before paying any bribes ‚Äî each curiosity reduces subsequent bribe costs. Probe any unconnected systems within visual range; off-piste colonization targets don't need lane access. Check for a Tree of Worlds system ‚Äî if found, it is your first colonization target regardless of other stats.
TURN 3‚Äì6
First bribe ‚Äî 25 Dust on your nearest minor faction after investigating their curiosities. Activate "Trusted Broker" law to reduce subsequent bribe costs by ~25%. Begin selling Hyperium/Titanium on the market in batches of 10‚Äì20 per turn to build toward your first outpost cost (250 Dust). Do not sell your entire stockpile in one transaction.
TURN 5‚Äì8
Hero hits Level 2‚Äì3 from curiosity XP. Reassign hero as home system Governor immediately ‚Äî wait for the assignment cooldown if needed. The +30 Science per turn bonus ("Blue Skies" speculator skill) is your most impactful Governor contribution at this stage. Note: the reassignment has a cooldown timer, so plan the transition 2 turns in advance. First outpost: place on the highest-value system you've scouted. If it's off-piste (no lane connection), food drain is avoided. If lane-connected, confirm your capital food surplus exceeds 35 before placing.
TURN 8‚Äì12
Build "Merchants and Money" immediately in your home system (Economy tech, unlocked after Xenolinguistics). This doubles all outpost growth rates ‚Äî the earlier you build it, the more total outpost turns it accelerates. Cost scales with colony count: build it with 1 colony for minimum cost (~150 Industry). Research Peb-Scale Accelerators (or equivalent arid/tundra colonization tech) if you've found an arid system with the Tree of Worlds or resource deposits you specifically need. Otherwise, continue toward Economy Stage 2 to unlock commercial infrastructure.
TURN 12‚Äì18
Second and third outposts. Place these in rapid succession if your food surplus allows ‚Äî the "New Colony Boost" law (+30% outpost growth, activate before placing) makes this window especially efficient. Begin lobbying for Industrialists ahead of Turn 20 elections (~200 Dust total across turns 12‚Äì19).
TURN 20

At turn 30: run the 4-question mid-game assessment from Vol.2 and commit to your victory path.
