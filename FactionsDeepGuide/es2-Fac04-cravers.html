ES2 // Fac.04 // The Cravers

The
Cravers

A locust swarm wearing the skin of a civilization. The Cravers cannot stop expanding ‚Äî every planet they occupy degrades permanently, so they must always move forward. In the hands of a player who understands the depletion engine, this becomes a weapon: a war machine that fights harder the more it destroys, subsidized by colonies that are deliberately consumed and abandoned. The player who treats depletion as a crisis will lose. The player who treats it as the plan will dominate.

00

Faction Identity: The Consumption Machine

Every trait feeds the same loop: consume, deplete, conquer, repeat.
ü™≥ Core Traits, Government, and Starting Conditions OVERVIEW ‚ñæ
‚óà CRAVERS ‚Äî THE ENDLESS SWARM ‚óà

The Cravers are a unique faction defined by a forced economic clock: their planets deplete over time, permanently losing output until they become worthless. This is not a drawback to be mitigated ‚Äî it is the design. The faction is built to sustain itself through constant conquest and to leverage the depleted planet count as a late-game military multiplier. Understanding this reframes everything: you are not building a stable empire, you are building a consumption machine that happens to have cities on it.

ETERNAL WAR ‚Äî THE DEFINING CONSTRAINT
Cravers cannot sign truces, cold wars, or non-aggression pacts. They begin in a state of permanent war with every major faction in the game ‚Äî except other Cravers. Diplomatic isolation is total. This means there is no "peaceful phase" at any point in a Craver game. You are always at war, always acquiring new systems to offset depletion, and always under threat. The flip side: you are never wasting political capital on diplomacy that cannot yield results. Every Influence and hero moment goes to internal development or military preparation.
AUTOCRACY ‚Äî THREE LAW SLOTS, NO ELECTIONS
Cravers are locked into Autocracy permanently. This means: three law slots (more than Dictatorship's two), no election cycles, no Anarchy risk, and ‚Äî critically ‚Äî only Craver population units vote. Non-Craver pops have zero political influence. The Militarist party typically dominates, which is appropriate: most Craver-optimal laws are Militarist. Three law slots in a stable Autocracy is a genuine advantage that is often underappreciated. Plan your law combination to always fill all three slots productively.
BIG FLEETS II ‚Äî THE STRUCTURAL MILITARY ADVANTAGE
Cravers receive Big Fleets II as a faction trait, giving +2 Command Points on all fleets and ‚àí25% ship upkeep. More Command Points at no tech cost means larger effective fleets earlier than any other faction. Combined with ‚àí25% upkeep, Cravers can sustain large fleets that would bankrupt other factions. This is not a bonus ‚Äî it is the baseline you design around. Never field small fleets. Your fleets should always be at the Command Point cap.
APPROVAL LOCKED TO MILITARIST OUTPUT ‚Äî THE CORE VULNERABILITY
Craver Approval is tied almost entirely to being at war and winning. Their approval sources are predominantly Militarist laws and buildings that require active conflict. In periods of relative inactivity (waiting for fleets to rebuild, navigating early exploration), Approval can crater. The approval crisis toolkit (Topic 02) addresses this directly ‚Äî but the fundamental truth is: a Craver empire at peace is a Craver empire in trouble.
HIDDEN HULL MODIFIER ‚Äî WHAT THE TOOLTIP DOESN'T SAY
Craver ships have a hidden modifier set: ‚àí10% base HP, ‚àí10% base hull production cost, and +10% Manpower capacity per ship. The ‚àí10% HP means individual ships are slightly squishier than tooltips suggest. The ‚àí10% build cost partially offsets upkeep (already reduced by Big Fleets II). The +10% Manpower means every ship carries more ground troops for invasion. Combined with Big Fleets II, the net effect is: build more ships than opponents, fill them with troops, and invade fast.

LUXURY RESOURCES ON CRAVER POPS ‚Äî THE HIDDEN EXCEPTION
Luxury resources normally double the FIDSI output of the population type they match. For Craver pops, this doubling does not apply ‚Äî Craver pops receive only a flat +2 FIDSI from luxury resources regardless of type. This has direct strategic implications: applying luxury resources to your Craver pops is nearly worthless. Applying them to non-Craver (enslaved) pops in your systems, where the normal doubling does work, is dramatically more valuable.

‚è±Ô∏è The Husk: Depletion Clock and Starting Conditions MECHANIC ‚ñæ
WHAT IS THE HUSK?
The Cravers start on a unique planet called the Husk ‚Äî a pre-depleted home world. At game start, the Husk already has approximately 116.5 out of 150 depletion points. Depletion accrues at roughly +2 per turn base. This means the Husk reaches full depletion (and ceases providing useful output) in approximately 17 turns from turn 1 ‚Äî before most other factions have settled their second colony. This is not a soft warning; it is a hard timer. If you are still relying on the Husk as a primary economic system at turn 20, you are behind.
HOW DEPLETION WORKS ‚Äî THE MECHANIC
Every Craver-occupied planet accrues depletion points each turn. Depletion points reduce that planet's FIDSI output proportionally. At 150 depletion points (full depletion), the planet's output is reduced by approximately 50% of its base value (the depletion bonus partially compensates ‚Äî see Topic 01). Non-Craver pops on a Craver-owned planet also contribute to depletion accumulation but at a lower rate than Craver pops. A planet with only non-Craver pops depletes more slowly.
ESCAPING THE HUSK ‚Äî THE TURN 1 PRIORITY
Your first colony ship must leave on Turn 1 or Turn 2, headed for the best available system in your starting neighbourhood. The Husk dying is not a crisis if your second (and ideally third) system is already producing. Treat the Husk as a temporary production platform, not your economic core. By turn 10‚Äì15, your first proper colony should be the engine of your empire. The Husk can still provide Manpower (military staging) and some residual output well past full depletion ‚Äî it is never truly useless, but its economic role ends fast.
DON'T PANIC ABOUT THE HUSK
New Craver players often spend early turns trying to prop up the Husk with buildings. This is a trap. Every Industry point spent improving the Husk is Industry not building your first real colony or your first fleet. Accept the Husk's fate, plan around it, and move on.

Depletion & Population Math

The numbers behind the consumption engine. Slave Driver and the depletion bonus are multiplicative in ways the UI doesn't fully explain.
üìä The Depletion Bonus: +150% FIDSI on Full Depletion MECHANIC ‚ñæ
WHAT THE +150% ACTUALLY MEANS
A fully depleted Craver planet receives a +150% FIDSI bonus. This is an additive modifier on the system's base output ‚Äî meaning the total FIDSI multiplier on a fully depleted system is √ó2.5 (base √ó1, plus 150% = 2.5). This is enormous. A fully depleted system producing 40 Food base now produces 100 Food. The catch is that depletion also imposes its own penalty as it accrues, so the net curve is not linear ‚Äî partial depletion imposes output penalties before the full bonus kicks in. The strategic implication: partial depletion is the worst state. You want systems either fresh (pre-depletion) or fully depleted (bonus active). The transition zone is the pain.
DEPLETION CURVE ‚Äî THE TRANSITION ZONE
As depletion accumulates from 0 to 150, the FIDSI penalty grows proportionally. The bonus only fully activates at 150. This means there is a trough: systems in the 60‚Äì140 depletion range are producing below their base value with no bonus yet. Fast-depleting strategies (all Craver pops, no management) push through this trough quickly. Slow-depleting strategies (mixed populations, fewer Craver pops) reduce trough depth but extend the time before the bonus pays off. The correct choice depends on your overall strategy ‚Äî see Topic 03 for the three strategy archetypes.

‚õìÔ∏è Slave Driver: The Non-Craver Pop Multiplier MECHANIC ‚ñæ
HOW SLAVE DRIVER WORKS
Any planet with at least one Craver population unit receives the Slave Driver modifier on all non-Craver pops in that system: their FIDSI output is multiplied by √ó2. A single Craver pop is enough to activate it ‚Äî additional Craver pops do not stack the bonus further. The Craver pop itself produces normal output (no multiplier, just base stats). The enslaved pops produce double. This creates an optimal configuration: exactly 1 Craver pop per planet, with as many non-Craver pops as possible filling the remaining slots.
THE APPROVAL COST OF SLAVERY
Each non-Craver pop enslaved under Slave Driver imposes a ‚àí10 Approval penalty per enslaved pop, empire-wide. A system with 1 Craver and 4 non-Craver pops costs ‚àí40 Approval to the entire empire. Multiply this across 5 systems and you have ‚àí200 Approval before any other penalties. This is the core tension of the Craver economy: Slave Driver is spectacularly productive, but its Approval cost grows directly with the number of slaves you maintain. Managing this without dropping below the Happy (70) or Ecstatic (85) thresholds is the central challenge of Craver empire management.
OPTIMAL POP CONFIGURATION PER SYSTEM
Per community consensus and 4Xalch analysis: the math favors 1 Craver + (N‚àí1) non-Craver per system where N is the total planet pop capacity. One Craver activates Slave Driver for all others. Additional Craver pops beyond the first reduce the number of doubled-output slaves without adding any bonus. The exception is the Chain Gang Program (below): additional Craver pops serve as Manpower converters if you are running that strategy.
CHAIN GANG PROGRAM ‚Äî CRAVER POPS AS MANPOWER FACTORIES
The Chain Gang Program is a building that converts 300 Food into 300 Manpower per turn per Craver pop in the system. Two Craver pops in a system with Chain Gang Programs can produce 600 Manpower per turn ‚Äî a significant military resource. However, the Food drain is real: a system running 2 Craver pops + Chain Gang needs substantial Food surplus or it will starve. The community strategy guide notes this as the core mechanic of the Standard Militarist strategy: keep Craver pop count low for economic efficiency, use Chain Gang to generate Manpower without relying on conquest alone.

At Autocracy tier the Jingoist Joy Bill gives +25 per major war and +15 per minor war. ‚õ™ Religious Path and Emergency Approval Tools MECHANIC ‚ñæ
SAINTS & SINNERS ‚Äî THE WAR-INDEPENDENT APPROVAL SOURCE
Saints & Sinners (Religious, Level 3) grants +20 Approval to all systems, plus an additional +20 Approval per Hero assigned to each system. This is the only significant Craver approval law that does not require active warfare. In the Religious strategy, placing heroes as system governors across your empire turns Saints & Sinners into an approval floor that can hold you at Ecstatic even during inter-war gaps. Note: this is a flat bonus, not a "content lock" ‚Äî it does not mechanically prevent Approval from dropping, it simply adds a large constant to the total.
SPECIES STABILITY ACT ‚Äî THE RELIGIOUS L1 CORNERSTONE
The Religious Level 1 law Species Stability Act provides +5 Dust per Original Empire Population pop and limits new pop growth to only the Original Empire Population type. For Cravers in a Religious strategy this is significant: it slows (or stops) unwanted non-Craver population growth while providing passive Dust. It also interacts with Us or Them Decree differently than in a pure Militarist game ‚Äî the Dust generation can fund additional hero hirings, which compound Saints & Sinners.
FEEDING PITS ‚Äî EMERGENCY APPROVAL BUILDING
The Feeding Pits system improvement provides +25 Approval for 10 turns and removes the ‚àí10 Approval penalty from enslaved pops in that system for the duration. It stacks with multiple castings. In an approval emergency (about to drop below Happy before a war ends), queuing Feeding Pits across your systems is a legitimate emergency brake ‚Äî it buys 10 turns of headroom while you rebuild the wartime approval base. After 10 turns the bonus expires and you must re-queue or the penalty returns.
CATHEDRAL OF THE SWARM ‚Äî THE LATE-GAME APPROVAL ANCHOR
The Cathedral of the Swarm is a unique Craver system improvement (high tier) that provides substantial permanent Approval bonuses and has secondary effects that compound with depleted planet count. It is the structural endgame approval solution for the Religious strategy and a useful supplement for Militarist paths. Build it in your highest-population system first for maximum effect.
APPROVAL BUDGET REALITY CHECK
A mid-game Craver empire with 6 systems, 3 Craver + 9 slaves per system = ‚àí90 Approval from slavery alone. Add overcolonization: ‚àí30. Total structural penalty: roughly ‚àí120. You need at least +190 from all positive sources combined just to reach Happy (70). Jingoist Joy Bill in 3 major wars: +75. Us or Them with 3 captured Home Systems: +60. Spoils: +occasional. That's +135 from two laws ‚Äî still short. You need Saints & Sinners, or heroes on systems, or Feeding Pits to bridge the gap. Budget this before you expand, not after Approval collapses.

The Three Strategies

Community consensus identifies three distinct Craver archetypes, each built around a different approval solution and population philosophy.
üóÇÔ∏è Standard Militarist ‚Äî The Optimised Path STRATEGY ‚ñæ
PHILOSOPHY
Keep Craver population count low (1 Craver per system for Slave Driver only), use Chain Gang Program to generate Manpower without scaling Craver pops, maintain large slave populations for doubled FIDSI output, and drive approval through Militarist laws with constant active wars. Victory target: Supremacy.
POPULATION TARGET
1 Craver + maximum non-Craver per system. This minimises depletion rate (fewer Craver pops = slower depletion), maximises Slave Driver output, and keeps the per-pop approval penalty manageable. The Craver pop exists solely to activate Slave Driver. Use Chain Gang Program buildings to convert Food into Manpower rather than adding more Craver pops.
LAW COMBINATION
Jingoist Joy Bill + Us or Them Decree + Spoils of War Act. All three are Militarist, all three scale with active warfare. In a 3-major-war game, this combination produces approximately +140‚Äì160 Approval from laws alone, which is sufficient to offset a moderate slave population and overcolonization.
WEAKNESS
Requires constant active wars. In an isolated start (few neighbours), or if all nearby enemies are eliminated too fast, the approval base collapses. Also requires micromanagement of Chain Gang Food drain vs. Manpower output. The most optimal strategy, but the most demanding.

üí£ All-In Militarist ‚Äî Fast Depletion, Fast Victory STRATEGY ‚ñæ
PHILOSOPHY
Lean into Craver pops heavily ‚Äî fill every system with Craver population to maximise depletion speed, reach the +150% bonus faster, and generate massive Manpower for constant invasions. Use Feeding Pits as the approval emergency tool when the Slave Driver penalty accumulates. Victory target: Supremacy via overwhelming military force.
POPULATION TARGET
Maximum Craver pops, accepting that non-Craver pop count (and thus Slave Driver benefit) will be lower. The tradeoff: faster depletion progression toward the +150% bonus, and higher Manpower generation across all ships (from the hidden +10% Manpower trait). This strategy effectively accepts lower FIDSI in exchange for faster military output.
APPROVAL MANAGEMENT
Jingoist Joy Bill remains essential. Feeding Pits are the emergency buffer. With high Craver pop counts, the ‚àí10 Approval per slave penalty is lower (fewer slaves), so the absolute penalty is smaller ‚Äî but the positive approval sources must still be active. This strategy is more tolerant of brief war gaps because the slave penalty is lower.
WEAKNESS
Sacrifices the Slave Driver output advantage. Lower overall FIDSI means slower tech and empire development. Only viable as a blitz strategy: win before the economic disadvantage compounds. If the game goes long, Standard Militarist or Religious outperforms this path.

‚õ™ Religious Cravers ‚Äî The War-Independent Path STRATEGY ‚ñæ
PHILOSOPHY
Replace the warfare-dependent Militarist approval core with Saints & Sinners + hero governor placement, allowing approval to remain high even between wars. Use the three law slots for a hybrid combination: one Militarist (Jingoist or Us or Them), plus the Religious tier laws (Species Stability Act, Saints & Sinners). Species Stability Act (Religious L1) + Saints & Sinners (Religious L3) + Us or Them Decree (Militarist). Saints & Sinners provides +20 flat to all systems, plus +20 per assigned hero. This strategy requires hero acquisition and deployment as system governors across your empire. Every hero slot filled on a system adds +20 Approval to that system empire-wide. Hiring from the Galactic Academy regularly is therefore a mandatory economic action, not a luxury. Budget Dust accordingly.
WEAKNESS
Reaching Religious L3 laws requires influence investment and time. In the early game before Saints & Sinners is available, you are still approval-vulnerable. And the Religious path costs one Militarist law slot ‚Äî losing Jingoist or Spoils of War reduces your wartime Approval ceiling. This path is strictly better than Militarist only once Saints & Sinners is online and heroes are deployed.

Early Game Playbook (Turns 1‚Äì30)

The Husk is dying. Your neighbours are enemies. Use both facts.
üóìÔ∏è Turn-by-Turn Opening Framework STRATEGY ‚ñæ
TURN 1
Colony ship out, hero to Revealer fleet. The colony ship must leave Turn 1. No exceptions. Scout for your nearest habitable system simultaneously ‚Äî two Revealers covering different directions doubles your information. Queue Jingoist Joy Bill immediately; you are already at war with everyone (Eternal War trait), so it pays immediately. Research priority: early infrastructure for your first colony's planet type, and the tech that unlocks Termite-class siege ships.
TURNS 1‚Äì10
Scout hard, find pirates, find minor factions. Craver scouts should be prioritizing: pirate locations (for Manpower, clearable with your starting fleet), minor faction locations (for assimilation and future slave pop acquisition), and nearest major faction home systems (for future Us or Them value). The Husk is still producing; use this window to build your first military ships and queue your first proper colony's infrastructure.

Faction quest choice: When the faction questline presents the early branch decision, the community consensus is to take the Destroy path for the Cyborg Stomachs unique improvement reward, which provides permanent Food bonuses that help sustain the Chain Gang Program. Specific values should be confirmed from the in-game quest screen.
TURNS 8‚Äì15
First colony must be fully operational. This is your economic lifeline after the Husk degrades. Prioritise improvements that match the colony's planet type and that feed your first Chain Gang Program. Begin acquiring non-Craver pops via minor faction assimilation ‚Äî you need slaves to make Slave Driver worthwhile. If the first minor faction you reach has a decent pop type, assimilate them.

First invasion planning: Identify your first conquest target. It should be: a minor faction or weak isolated major faction system within 2‚Äì3 turns of travel. The Termite corvette (if available) deals approximately 30 Manpower damage per turn at siege, making it the preferred siege weapon for early invasions. Send a fleet of Termites to the target system, reduce system Manpower to zero, then invade.
TURNS 15‚Äì25
First conquest completed or in progress. By turn 20, you should have taken at least one system from a neighbour. This activates Us or Them Decree value (if the system was a Home System), provides non-Craver pops for Slave Driver, and ‚Äî if the conquered faction was a major faction ‚Äî gives Approval bonuses from active war. Do not make peace. Ever. Eternal War means this is permanent anyway, but do not prioritise negotiation over expansion.

Luxury resources: Apply luxury resources to your non-Craver pop systems. Do not apply them to Craver pops (flat +2 FIDSI ‚Äî worthless). For enslaved populations, the normal doubling applies on top of Slave Driver, creating a 4√ó multiplier on the base value for correctly luxuriated slaves.
TURNS 25‚Äì35
Second conquest and fleet expansion. With Big Fleets II providing extra Command Points, your fleet should always be at the CP cap. Queue ships continuously. Identify your second conquest target ‚Äî ideally a Home System from a major faction, which advances Us or Them Decree. Begin assessing your approval budget: if slavery penalties are accumulating, now is the time to invest in Feeding Pits or reach for Saints & Sinners on the Religious path.

Ships & Fleet Doctrine

More ships, more manpower per ship, cheaper to run. The design philosophy follows from Big Fleets II.
üöÄ Hull Analysis and Doctrine MECHANIC ‚ñæ
THE HIDDEN HULL MODIFIER ‚Äî DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
All Craver hulls carry: ‚àí10% HP, ‚àí10% build cost, +10% Manpower capacity. The ‚àí10% HP means individual ships trade slightly below their apparent stat line. The ‚àí10% build cost, combined with Big Fleets II's ‚àí25% upkeep, means Craver fleets are cheaper both to build and maintain than any comparable faction. The +10% Manpower per ship means a 6-ship Craver fleet carries ~10% more invasion troops than the equivalent fleet of any other faction. Stack this with fleet CP upgrades and the Manpower laws (Jingoist, Chain Gang) and Craver ground invasion capacity becomes exceptional.
FORAGER / SCAVENGER
ATTACKER (SMALL)
Craver's baseline attacker. With the ‚àí10% build cost modifier, these are the cheapest-to-spam ships in the game. Use them for early fleet padding ‚Äî fill Command Points with Foragers to reach the CP cap cheaply. Do not over-invest in module upgrades on these hulls; they are expendable. Their value is volume. Upgraded Scavenger: gains slots and becomes a more capable weapons platform for mid-game, but still best used in numbers rather than as individual powerhouses.
SODRA / SLICER
HUNTER (MEDIUM)
Craver's primary mid-range combat ship. Per Pangolin's analysis, the base Sodra has a strong slot configuration for its class. The hidden ‚àí10% HP applies here, so avoid committing Hunters into unsupported engagements ‚Äî use them as part of a full fleet, not as solo raiders. Best weapon loadout: beam weapons for breaking through enemy shields at range (effective against 90% of fleet HP across all flotillas, vs. kinetics at ~25%).
MARAUDER
COORDINATOR (MEDIUM)
The Craver coordinator carries invasion modules and troop capacity. The +10% Manpower trait makes these ships exceptionally good invasion platforms. Standard doctrine: load with Boarding Pods or Invasion Modules, bring 3‚Äì4 to any system assault. With the Termite siege strategy (below), Marauders follow after Manpower drain and execute the invasion without needing a long ground war. Note from ESAcademy: scrapping older Marauders when upgrading to a new hull tier is recommended ‚Äî each Marauder scrapped returns significant resources and avoids upkeep on ships below the current tech level.
TERMITE
CORVETTE / SIEGE
The Craver's unique siege asset. The Termite deals approximately 30 Manpower damage per turn at siege ‚Äî no other corvette-class ship matches this. Standard siege doctrine: send 3‚Äì4 Termites to a target system, orbit for 3‚Äì5 turns to drain Manpower to near zero, then bring in the main fleet and Marauder coordinators for the actual invasion. The Termite is not a combat ship ‚Äî do not use it in pitched battles. Its value is entirely in the pre-invasion Manpower drain role.
WEAPON SELECTION ‚Äî BEAM VS KINETICS
Per Pangolin's fleet analysis: beam weapons deal damage to 90% of the total fleet HP spread across all flotillas, while kinetic weapons deal damage to approximately 25% of HP in the targeted flotilla only. This means beams are dramatically more efficient against full fleets. Cravers should default to beam loadouts for their primary combat ships. Kinetics (railguns, mass drivers) are only preferred when specifically targeting shielded protector flotillas that beam weapons cannot penetrate.
TRANSCRIPT02 (Pangolin)
BIG FLEETS II IN FLEET DESIGN
The +2 Command Points from Big Fleets II are permanent and apply to every fleet without any tech or upgrade investment. This means every Craver fleet should be built to its CP cap ‚Äî any unused CP is lost value. When designing fleet compositions, fill the last 2 CP slots with cheap Forager/Scavenger units rather than leaving them empty. Volume is free for Cravers relative to other factions.

Endgame: Empire of Ash

The depleted empire loop ‚Äî when the ash itself becomes a weapon.
üíÄ The Depleted Planet Military Multiplier STRATEGY ‚ñæ
DEADLY INTENT ‚Äî THE ENDGAME LAW
Deadly Intent (Militarist) provides +1% ship damage and +1% troop damage per depleted planet owned. Both bonuses apply simultaneously. At 50 depleted planets: +50% ship damage and +50% troop damage ‚Äî a flat combat multiplier that applies to your entire military. No other faction has access to a scaling late-game combat law of this magnitude. The depleted planet trail your empire leaves behind is not a liability by late game: it is the fuel for a military multiplier that compounds with every conquest.
WIKI_LAWSTRANSCRIPT01 (4Xalch)
THE DEPLETED EMPIRE LOOP
The mature Craver endgame operates as follows: conquered systems begin fresh, are worked by slaves under Slave Driver for production, deplete over time to the +150% FIDSI bonus state, then are used as forward military bases as the Craver empire advances further. Old depleted systems in the rear provide: +150% FIDSI output (from the depletion bonus), their Deadly Intent contribution (depleted planet count), and Manpower from Chain Gang buildings. The economic and military cores of your empire are the same depleted worlds.
TIMING: US OR THEM ‚Üí DEADLY INTENT SWAP
