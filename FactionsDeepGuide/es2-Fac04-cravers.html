The Cravers are a pressure-based faction. Everything about their design creates a ticking clock: their home planet depletes, their population generates disapproval, and their government prohibits peace. The correct mindset is not "how do I stabilize" but "how fast can I convert instability into conquest, and conquest into more instability for my enemies." Every mechanic that looks like a weakness is actually an output throttle.

DEPLETION â€” THE CRAVER POP MECHANIC
Craver population units apply +1 Depletion Point per turn to the planet they occupy. When a planet reaches its Depletion threshold (150 for most planets, though the cap scales with original planet size), it becomes Depleted: all populations on it suffer â€“50% FIDS. Before depletion, Craver pops receive a +150% FIDS bonus â€” which means the total output is Ã—2.5 of their baseline, not Ã—1.5. A Craver pop producing 10 Industry actually produces 25 Industry; the "+15" bonus listed in the UI is the additive addition on top of the base 10. The multiplier applies to the sum-total of all bonuses already applied to that population unit, not just the raw planet output.
SLAVE DRIVER â€” THE NON-CRAVER POP MECHANIC
When at least one Craver unit occupies the same planet as non-Craver pops, every non-Craver pop on that planet produces +100% FIDSI (effectively doubling their output). This is a fixed, binary toggle â€” a second or third Craver on the same planet does not increase the bonus. One Craver is enough. The enslaved Craver will be subtly highlighted in the UI. The cost: â€“10 Approval per enslaved population in the system. This is the central management tension: slave-driving maximizes FIDSI but drains approval, which then throttles food production, influences law availability, and threatens the stability of the empire.
AUTOCRACY â€” THE GOVERNMENT EXCEPTION
The Cravers run an Autocracy, their unique government variant. Three critical differences from other governments: (1) Only Craver (major) population units can vote, so minor pops contribute zero political influence regardless of their numbers. (2) Anarchy is impossible. When approval collapses to Mutinous, pops starve rather than triggering political collapse â€” the Autocracy is structurally stable no matter how unhappy the populace gets. (3) The Autocracy provides 3 law slots, one more than Dictatorship but fewer than Republic or Federation. The combination of Anarchy immunity and Militarist-only politics means the risk of low approval is nutritional and economic (food, influence), not political.
ETERNAL WAR â€” THE DIPLOMATIC CONSTRAINT
The Cravers cannot sign Peace treaties unless the Pacifist party holds a position in their Leading Political Parties â€” which, given their Autocracy and Militarist-only voting base, effectively never happens in a standard game. Every war they declare is permanent until the other side capitulates, is destroyed, or the Craver player manually pursues a Pacifist government restructure (which undermines every other Militarist-based approval mechanic). Strategic implication: once you declare war on someone, plan to finish the job.

POPULATION GROWTH â€” HOW THE SYSTEM PICKS
The game assigns growth by a "chance-to-increment" system:
  • Every pop type starts at 0 and accumulates "chance to increment" each turn it is not chosen.
  • Craver (major pop): base +50 weight per turn not selected.
  • Any minor pop: base +25 weight per turn not selected.
  • The pop type with the highest accumulated weight is selected when growth triggers.
  • After selection, that type resets to 0.
In practice: Cravers grow roughly twice as fast as any individual minor pop. With 2+ minor types in the system, the combined weight of all minors can outpace Craver growth â€” but a single minor type still grows slower than Craver pops.
CHAIN GANG PROGRAM â€” CRAVER POP CONTROL
Unlocked by the Universal Conscription technology. The building consumes 300 Food and produces 300 Manpower. The growth control effect: when the system does not have +300 Food to pay, it instead starves the population type already scheduled for increment â€” and if Cravers were scheduled next, a Craver pop is removed. This is the deliberate Craver pop reduction mechanic: if you are running a food surplus below 300, you can use Chain Gang to trim your Craver population.

This is distinct from ESAcademy's description of "converting 2 Craver pops to 600 Manpower" â€” that is the effect of using Chain Gang and deliberately operating below the food threshold with Cravers in the growth queue. The mechanic being used is the same, but the framing differs. Trust the wiki's mechanical description: the building converts Food to Manpower; the starvation effect is the consequence of insufficient Food.

STANDARD MILITARIST â€” RECOMMENDED DEFAULT
Probably the most optimal general Craver strategy. Requires ongoing micromanagement, but provides the best FIDSI output and approval control across all game phases.
THE CORE LOOP
Keep Craver population deliberately low using Chain Gang Program. Maximize non-Craver slave population for doubled FIDSI. Use warfare (Jingoist Joy Bill) as the primary approval engine rather than buildings. Acquire enemy homeworlds as quickly as possible to unlock Us or Them mid-game.

Victory type: Supremacy (capturing homeworlds). The homeworld collection serves dual purpose: territory and the +20 Approval per homeworld from Us or Them.
PHASE STRUCTURE
Early Game: War with nearest neighbor (major or minor faction) to activate Jingoist. Build approval buildings. Actively move Craver pops off planets approaching depletion thresholds. Locate enemy homeworlds via exploration â€” this is your primary strategic objective.

Mid Game: Us or Them Decree comes online once Militarist party has enough experience. Capturing 2+ homeworlds raises global approval substantially. This window allows a higher slave pop without approval crisis â€” lean into it to maximize FIDSI.

Late Game: When approval begins slipping again from accumulated conquests, execute the kill switch: shift from Us or Them to Deadly Intent Bill (+33% damage), simultaneously boost Craver pop with luxuries (knowing the flat +2 FIDS bonus is acceptable at this stage), and use Feeding Pits aggressively. Planetary depletion accelerates, the 50-planet bonus rises, and the empire pivots to maximum military output for the final push.
VULNERABILITY
Isolated starts (no accessible homeworld within reach in the first 30 turns) significantly degrade this strategy. Without a homeworld target to justify the Jingoist approval from a major-faction war, approval management becomes much harder. Consider Religious path if scouting reveals an isolated start.
ALL-IN MILITARIST â€” HIGH RISK, MAXIMUM EARLY OUTPUT
The most aggressive approach. Front-loads FIDSI and military power at the cost of long-term economic stability. Requires a favorable start position and continuous momentum.
THE CORE LOOP
Instead of minimizing Craver pops, actively grow them using luxury resource boosts. Feed the excess non-Craver pops into Feeding Pits (approval management) rather than using Feeding Pits as an emergency tool. Cravers' natural +150% FIDS on undepleted planets, combined with aggressive luxury boosting, creates a massive early production surge.

Victory type: Conquest or Supremacy. You are building toward the 50 Depleted Planets military bonus as fast as possible by burning planets faster than any other approach.
THE FAILURE MODE
Once conquest momentum stalls, you have burned your own planets for fuel with no economic base left to sustain operations. This strategy requires you to always have a new target before the current economy collapses. It is start-dependent â€” a tight cluster of systems to consume in sequence is necessary. Do not attempt with an isolated start.

Ecstatic approval (from Feeding Pits + Jingoist + having no surviving slaves that generate penalties) means maximal FIDS multipliers. You maintain the Ecstatic production bonus continuously rather than the Content/Happy that most Craver players settle for. RELIGIOUS CRAVERS â€” STABILITY OVER WARFARE
Less micro-management, more resilient to isolated starts. Trades the war-based approval engine for a law-based one, and trades some FIDSI ceiling for a higher FIDSI floor.
THE CORE LOOP
The Religious path uses Saints & Sinners (Religious Level 3, +20 Approval everywhere + +20 per Hero) combined with Species Stability Act (Level 1, only original pop grows) to achieve a stable approval state without requiring constant warfare.

THE DEPLETED EMPIRE LOOP (KNOFBATH)
The mid-to-late game Craver empire follows a specific pattern:

1. Core systems deplete. Your earliest-colonized systems will hit the Depletion threshold first. Remove Craver pops and replace with minor pops. As shown in the population math section, a depleted planet with Slave Driver active returns non-Craver pops to approximately their base output (the +100% from Slave Driver cancels the â€“50% depletion penalty). Net result: productive but no longer exceptional.

2. Craver pops move outward. Fresh conquered systems receive the Craver pops expelled from depleted cores. Fresh planets give Cravers their full Ã—2.5 output â€” so the productive frontier advances continuously as the depleted rear converts to slave-labor maintenance.

3. Depleted count accumulates. Each depleted planet adds +1% to ship and troop damage. A 30-planet depleted empire provides a passive +30% combat bonus, stacking multiplicatively with the late-game Deadly Intent Bill (+33% weapon damage). This is why abandoning depleted systems â€” a commonly suggested "simplification" â€” is wrong: you want that counter as high as possible.
