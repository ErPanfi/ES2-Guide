ES2 // Fac.07 // The United Empire

The United
Empire

The compounding machine. The United Empire has no exotic affinity, no game-changing unique mechanic — just a feedback loop that turns Industry into Influence and Influence into faster Industry, and then keeps going. The player who understands this engine, fuels it early, and protects it through the mid-game will find themselves operating an economy that other factions cannot catch. This deep dive is about understanding the engine at the formula level, then using that understanding to make every strategic decision that follows.

00

Faction Identity: The Compounding Machine

No exotic mechanics. Just numbers that reinforce each other faster than anyone else.
⚔️ Core Traits at a Glance OVERVIEW ▾
◈ UNITED EMPIRE — BY THE EMPEROR'S WILL ◈

The wiki's frank assessment: "The United Empire is a rather vanilla faction with very few special mechanics; they mostly differentiate themselves by numerical bonuses rather than changed mechanics." This is accurate and, properly understood, is exactly why the faction is dangerous. There is nothing to learn that requires a completely different mental model — instead, the UE takes systems every faction uses and runs them harder and longer than anyone else can. Their identity is acceleration, not transformation.

THE TWO-PHASE ENGINE
The single most important thing to understand about the United Empire is that their Influence economy has two distinct phases. In the early game (turns 1–~20), Influence is genuinely scarce — it competes with law costs, minor faction diplomacy, and buyout needs. Every point has an opportunity cost. In the mid-to-late game, once Industry reaches meaningful levels and the passive 0.1 Influence/Industry generation compounds across many systems, Influence approaches abundance. Expert play commentary describes this directly: "I have pretty much infinite Influence as United Empire." The skill is navigating the scarce phase without starving your laws or diplomacy, then knowing when to start weaponizing the abundance.

Emperor's Will: The Core Engine

Industry → Influence → buyout → more Industry. Understand the formulas before you pull the trigger.
⚙️ The Feedback Loop and Buyout Formulas MECHANIC ▾

The United Empire's Emperor's Will affinity does three things. First, it adds a passive generator: every Industry point spent anywhere in the empire generates 0.1 Influence — automatically, every turn, with no action required. Second, it converts Influence into a buyout currency for any System Improvement, Technology, or Marketplace purchase. Third, the Influence buyout is scaled differently from the Dust buyout and is completely insulated from Dust inflation.

DUST BUYOUT COST
Cost = ( IndustryCost ^ 1.2125 + 50 ) × DustInflation
Exponent 1.2125. Scales steeply with Industry cost. Multiplied by inflation, which rises with your Dust income.
INFLUENCE BUYOUT COST (Emperor's Will)
Cost = ( IndustryCost ^ 1.1 ) + 100
Exponent 1.1 — shallower curve. No inflation multiplier. Completely unaffected by Dust income.
TECH BUYOUT COST (Influence only)
Cost = ( ScienceCost ^ 1.01 ) + 150
Exponent 1.01 — nearly linear. The only buyout in the game that scales almost linearly with cost.

The gap between formulas matters most at higher Industry costs. At 160 Industry (early buildings), Dust and Influence costs are comparable. At 1120 Industry (late-tier buildings), the exponent divergence is significant — and Dust cost is further multiplied by whatever inflation your economy has accumulated. A high-Dust economy in the late game is, counterintuitively, exactly when switching to Influence buyout becomes most advantageous.

THE EFFICIENCY RULE: MANY SMALL BEATS ONE LARGE
Because cost scales with Industry cost raised to a power greater than 1, it is always more Influence-efficient to buy out several small projects than one large one. Buying out seven Drone Networks (160 Industry each) costs roughly 25% less total Influence than buying out one Predictive Logistics (1120 Industry). Queue cheap improvements in every new colony, buy them out immediately, and repeat. Each project stays in the low-cost exponent bracket.
THE 1-TURN EXCEPTION
A project completing in 1 turn cannot be bought out. Workaround: switch the queue to a different project, buy that out with Influence, then switch back. The original finishes that turn and you save its Industry cost. Niche but useful when you need something urgently.

🧮 Influence vs. Dust Buyout: Decision Framework STRATEGY ▾

Having two buyout currencies doesn't mean using them interchangeably. The decision is not "Influence OR Dust" but rather "which currency is currently cheaper per unit of progress, and what am I giving up by spending it here?"

The Population Fork: Imperials, Mezari, or Sheredyn

A one-time quest decision with permanent empire-wide consequences. The most consequential UE-specific choice in the game.
🧬 The Quest Decision and What Each Path Gives MECHANIC ▾

During the United Empire questline you will receive a single one-time choice: convert every Imperial population unit in your empire to Mezari or Sheredyn, or change nothing and keep Imperials. This decision is permanent and irreversible. The quest milestone at which this fires varies — check your current quest objectives early and plan your answer before it arrives.

WHICH PATH TO CHOOSE?
Imperials (keep as-is) is strongest for the pure Influence engine. The 15% Influence multiplier at 20 pop compounds with the passive generation and directly accelerates the core buyout snowball. Best for Wonder Victory or Pacific Conversion builds where Influence is the primary resource being optimized.

Mezari directly addresses UE's structural weakness: science output. Terran and Monsoon planets produce poor Science. The −10% Technology Cost at 50 pop is a significant permanent reduction that compounds over hundreds of technologies across the entire game. Strongest for Science Victory paths or any game where tech speed feels like the binding constraint.

Sheredyn locks UE into an aggressive military identity. The Manpower capacity bonuses amplify Patriots II. The 50-pop bonus (+5 Influence per 100 Manpower Stock) creates a secondary Influence source in high-Manpower military empires. Best for early-war Military or Conquest paths.

There is no wrong answer — the choice defines your empire's personality for the entire game. Make the decision intentionally and build your tech tree and senate strategy around it.

Political Management

Keeping Industrialists in power while managing a Militarist surge that is almost inevitable if you fight.
🏛️ The Starting Senate and Industrialist Anchor MECHANIC ▾

The United Empire starts with a Federation government, 3 Industrialist seats and 1 Militarist seat. Industrialist is the primary engine party — their laws provide Dust feedback from Industry, expansion bonuses, and industrial maximization effects that directly power the snowball. Militarist is the natural secondary in any game involving warfare, but can disrupt law progression if it surges too high.

THE INDUSTRIALIST/MILITARIST TENSION
This is an active management challenge, not a passive setup. Every battle won, every war declared, every ship built pushes political sentiment toward Militarists. If Militarist support overshoots your target, Industrialist law tier access can erode, and vice versa. Expert play describes this directly: "I'm afraid that if I support Militarists then Industrialists will suddenly lose and I cannot afford that — I actually care about some cool Industrialist laws." The Industrialist law progression is the anchor. Everything else fits around protecting it.

🗳️ Government Type and the Federation Bonus MECHANIC ▾

Federation government adds +1 overcolonization threshold per Hero currently acting as a political representative for leading parties. A hero-heavy senate stack can grant +3 or +4 bonus threshold systems — meaningful for a wide-expansion faction. Since UE starts in Federation, this bonus is immediately available without any tech investment.

The Xeno Anthropology technology (Empire Development Stage 3) unlocks Government Diversity. The UE-specific variant is called Patriotic Press instead of the generic Pooster Program: it provides +2 systems before overcolonization triggers, compared to the generic factions' +1. This makes the UE variant of this tech strictly more valuable than it is for other factions. It stacks with Expansionist I (−25% penalty) and Federation's hero bonus, allowing significantly wider play before Approval collapses.

Opening Decisions: UE-Specific Turn 1 Optimizations

The moves that are uniquely correct for United Empire — not a generic "how to start" primer.
🚀 Turn 1 Decision Sequence STRATEGY ▾
LAW
Pass Super Tax Act immediately. The −20 Approval penalty is negligible in the first turns when you are far from the overcolonization threshold and happiness is naturally high. The Dust income compounds from turn 1.
DMITRI
Assign Dmitri Lenko as home system governor — not on the scout ship. Dmitri's +10% Industry on his assigned system is active at level 0, shortening Xeno-Industrial Infrastructure by 1 turn at no cost. Putting him on the scout ship instead loses this bonus for many turns and reduces Patrol movement to 4. The math favors the governor assignment unambiguously.
PROBES
Refit the Patrol ship to 4 probes before moving it. Cost: 80 Dust (base refit — gun removal brings it to baseline). You now have 4 probes on the Patrol + 2 on the Colony ship = 6 total. Probes do not consume movement points — fire all 6 on turn 1 before either ship moves. Wide exploration at zero opportunity cost.
QUEUE
Build Xeno-Industrial Infrastructure first. On Raia (Temperate + Fertile), it gives +10 Industry per matching tag = +30 Industry immediately, scaling to +50 on Monsoon systems. This is the most efficient Industry-per-turn investment in the early game and is available from turn 1 because Xenolinguistics is pre-researched.
RESEARCH
Queue Xenobiology first — unlocks second movement tier and is required to progress in the Economy tree. Then immediately queue Public-Private Partnerships (accessible because Off-World Agribusiness is pre-researched): adds +30 Science on Fertile+Temperate planets, tripling early output on Raia and solving UE's early science gap.
SETTLER
Do not commit the Colony ship to a direction until probes reveal a clear candidate. Atoll is the preferred first colony target: 8 Food / 6 Industry baseline vs. Terran's 8/4. The +2 Industry per pop compounds immediately with industrial buildings. Commit only once you know what you're committing to.
PIPELINE
Track minor factions on first contact. Praising a minor faction for ~50 Influence starts relationship building 10–20 turns before you have assimilation capacity. A prepared pipeline means 2–3 of your first 6 systems can be assimilation acquisitions at no colony ship cost — UE's passive Influence generation makes early diplomacy cheap.

Expansion and Approval Management

UE can go wider than anyone — but the snowball requires protecting new systems during their vulnerable early turns.
🌍 The Wide Empire Framework STRATEGY ▾

UE's expansion advantage comes from three stacking effects: Expansionist I reduces the approval penalty per extra system by 25%; the UE variant of Xeno Anthropology (Patriotic Press) adds 2 to the overcolonization threshold rather than 1; and Federation government's hero-senator bonus can add further threshold increases. Together these allow UE to maintain comfortable Approval at system counts that would cripple other factions.

SourceExpansion Benefit
Expansionist I (trait)−25% overcolonization penalty empire-wide
Xeno Anthropology — Patriotic Press (UE variant)+2 systems before overcolonization triggers (vs. generic +1)
Federation government hero-senators+1 threshold per Hero acting as political representative for leading parties
Autonomous Administration (system improvement)+1 system threshold per developed system
BLOCKADE VULNERABILITY: THE NEW COLONY RISK
A newly placed colony under enemy blockade grows significantly slower and can in extreme cases starve and die — losing the system entirely. This is not hypothetical: in competitive play against aggressive neighbors, a freshly converted outpost is a viable harassment target. UE's wide expansion strategy makes this risk structural rather than incidental. Always have at least one ship capable of defending each new colony during its first 5–10 turns, or time colonizations during diplomatic safety windows.
PACIFIC CONVERSION: THE INFLUENCE EXPANSION PATH
Once SLC Systems is researched (ED Stage 4), UE can convert enemy systems via Influence dominance rather than warfare. The conversion clock ticks based on how much more Influence you generate than the system's defensive output. This pairs naturally with Emperor's Shadow and a high-Influence endgame, but requires committing fully to Influence maximization over Industry-first. Understand the engine at full power before pursuing this route.

Tech Tree Priorities

The sequence is always map-dependent. The framework is not.
🔬 Phase-by-Phase Sequencing STRATEGY ▾

The guiding principle: the tech tree serves your current constraints. If your map opens onto Atoll or Forest systems, you can skip much of the Science & Exploration tree and route directly into Economy and Military — exactly as expert play demonstrates on tight-galaxy starts. Map reading replaces rigid tech order. The phases below are a normal-start default; adjust for your actual situation.

Public-Private Partnerships Economy & Trade Solves the early science gap: +30 Science on Fertile+Temperate (Raia qualifies). Accessible because Off-World Agribusiness is pre-researched.
Military Tier 1 Military Improved weapons and ships. Required for pirates and early defense. Don't skip regardless of playstyle.
Phase 2 — Turns ~15–30: Engine Online
TechTreeWhy for UE

Phase 3 — Mid-game: Payoff Techs
TechTreeWhy for UE
Emperor's Shadow Empire Development Stage 4 UE-exclusive. Zelevas Incarnate improvement: Dust and Industry bonuses tied to total empire Influence output. The reverse amplifier that completes the loop. Top priority once Stage 4 is accessible.

WATCH FOR UE FACTION-ICON TECHS
Technologies with the UE faction icon are modified specifically for United Empire and are almost always higher-value than the generic version. Xeno Anthropology (confirmed +2 vs. +1 threshold) is the clearest example. HyperPACs also has a UE variant — specific differences unconfirmed; verify in-game before prioritizing.
ANTI-MATTER: SECURE THE RESOURCE BEFORE THE TECH
Anti-matter is required for Stage 4 Military and late-game ships. Identify anti-matter deposits on your map early and secure those systems — researching the tech without the resource wastes the investment entirely.

Ship Arsenal

Decision space, not build templates. The variables that matter shift with every game state.
🛸 Hull-by-Hull: Strategic Characteristics MECHANIC ▾

UE ships are described by the wiki as "well-rounded with many Squadron slots." This understates the structural advantages that make specific hulls worth understanding in detail. No build templates are given here — as expert play explicitly argues, the right loadout varies dramatically by game state, enemy faction, resource availability, and threat type.

Up to 3 weapon modules with one top-mounted slot. Top-mounted guns have a 270° fire arc compared to broadside guns' 120°. The Attacker can engage enemy ships without requiring broadside orientation — a genuine tactical advantage in the 3-phase combat system. LATE-GAME FLEET COMPOSITION
Expert play converges on a Battleships + Carriers core for the primary fleet, with Corvettes and Patrol ships used as early fillers or niche roles only. The reasoning is scale: at high tech levels, Carriers and Hunters deliver disproportionately more firepower per Command Point. Don't invest heavily in Corvette fleets past the early game — the Industry is better spent on fewer, larger hulls.
GROUND TROOPS: FAVOR TANKS HEAVILY
When loading Manpower for planetary invasions, target approximately 70% tanks. AI opponents over-invest in infantry, and tanks counter infantry effectively. Even if an opponent builds anti-tank units, the AI typically does not optimize counter-compositions. A tank-heavy loadout is the safest default for most invasion scenarios.

Victory Path Playbook

How the engine serves each victory type — and where it strains.
🏆 Victory Paths Through the UE Lens STRATEGY ▾
WONDER VICTORY — THE NATURAL HOME
The Influence engine was effectively designed for this path. The Obelisks of All Space Time (Stage 6 Wonder technology) are among the most expensive constructions in the game — and the UE's buyout cascade is the exact tool for completing them. The path: build Industry → generate Influence → buy out every system improvement → pile up Industry and Influence → research and buy out Wonder technologies → buy out the Obelisks themselves. The snowball's natural endpoint is a Wonder Victory. Focus on maximizing system count, per-system Industry output, and time Emperor's Shadow to amplify the final construction push.
MILITARY VICTORY — EASY SYNERGY
Industrialist + Militarist coexistence is natural for a wide UE empire (many wars push Militarist sentiment; many systems produce more ships). Patriots II provides native Manpower advantage. Priorities: secure anti-matter deposits early, unlock the Hunter hull, build the Battleships + Carrier fleet composition, use Spoils of War Act to sustain Dust during campaigns, and use tank-heavy ground forces for invasions.
CONQUEST VICTORY — SHARES MILITARY INFRASTRUCTURE
Requires total system domination. Minor faction assimilation counts as system control, making UE's natural Influence-based assimilation track a genuine contribution to the win condition. The late-game bottleneck is mopping up remote AI holdouts — Pacific Conversion can assist in final captures without additional warfare.
PACIFIC CONVERSION — INFLUENCE DOMINANCE PATH
Viable but specialized. Requires SLC Systems (ED Stage 4) and Influence output that outcompetes target system defensive production. Emperor's Shadow is the linchpin: once online, it generates Industry from Influence which generates more Influence — reaching output levels that dominate border disputes passively. Combine with Influence luxuries in System Development Projects. This is the most technically demanding UE path and requires committing fully to Influence maximization over Industry-first.
SCIENCE VICTORY — LEAST NATURAL PATH
UE's science weakness is structural: preferred planet types (Terran, Monsoon) produce poor Science, and there is no faction-wide science multiplier. Mezari population (−10% tech cost at 50 pop) and Public-Private Partnerships close the gap but don't eliminate it. To compete with Sophons on a pure Science Victory timeline requires deliberate commitment: Cold planet colonization for science output, Mezari population fork, and using the near-linear tech buyout formula (1.01 exponent) to snap expensive Stage 4–5 techs. Only attempt if the population fork went Mezari and your map has Cold planets in reach.

First 30 Turns Playbook

The optimized sequence from turn 1 to the early-mid-game handoff. Verified against Endless difficulty.
🗓️ Turn-by-Turn Decision Framework STRATEGY ▾
TURN 1
Queue: Xeno-Industrial Infrastructure. Research: Xenobiology. Law: Super Tax Act. Hero: Assign Dmitri as home system governor. Ships: Refit Patrol to 4 probes (80 Dust). Fire all 6 probes before moving anything. Hold Colony ship pending probe results.
T 2–5
Exploration: Probe 2–3 Star Lane directions simultaneously. Identify system quality before committing Colony ship — prefer Atoll and Forest targets. Diplomacy: First contact with minor factions costs ~50 Influence — do one interaction per discovery and track the assimilation pipeline. Relationship builds passively over 10 turns.
T 5–10
Research: Xenobiology completes → queue Public-Private Partnerships immediately. Colony: Commit to best revealed system — Atoll or Forest preferred. Queue: Add Food improvements to home system to sustain colony supply chains. Military: If aggressive neighbor or Cravers adjacent, insert Military Tier 1 here before Commercial Framework.
T 10–20
Engine initializing. Industry growing; Influence following passively at 0.1× rate. Begin minor faction praise actions to advance 1–2 assimilation timelines. Buyout: Start cycling cheap improvements (160 Industry) in new colonies — many-small rule in action. Do not save Influence for large projects at this stage. Research: Route toward Commercial Framework unless military threat demands otherwise.
T 20–30
First election occurring. Verify Industrialist party has maintained tier access. Support them if needed — do not let Militarist surge erase your law progression. Second law slot: My Precious (if Dust is strained from upkeep) or an Industrialist tier-2 law if party experience allows. Xeno Anthropology: If Empire Development Stage 3 is accessible, prioritize — the +2 threshold is immediately valuable.
T 30 STATE
A strong position looks like: 5–7 systems (3 colonized, 2–3 assimilated), Industrialist majority maintained, Influence generation running at 30–50/turn minimum, first assimilation complete, Commercial Framework in queue or researched, no serious Approval collapse. The engine is initialized — mid-game is about amplifying what's running, not correcting structural errors.
