ES2 // Fac.11 // The Nakalim

The
Nakalim

The Last Empire — custodians of a dead civilization's ruins, relic hunters, and patient engineers of influence. The Nakalim pay for every technology twice: once with Industry, and once with a permanent science penalty that never goes away. In exchange, they start with two tiers of tech already unlocked, generate massive Influence through Temples, and possess the only mechanism in the game that converts enemy territory to the Academy peacefully — turning geography itself into a resource. They are not a science faction. They are a faction that has learned to win without science.

00

Faction Identity: The Empire That Outlasted Its Gods

Understanding what the Nakalim trade away — and what they get in return — is prerequisite to every other decision.
🏛️ Core Traits and the True Nakalim Identity OVERVIEW ▾
◈ NAKALIM — THE LAST EMPIRE ◈
⚠ VERSION NOTE — RE-AWAKENING DLC
This guide covers the Re-Awakening DLC version of the Nakalim. The original faction had the trait "Cannot obtain technology from other empires" (hard lock). Re-Awakening changed this to "+300% Influence cost to receive Technologies" — diplomatic tech exchange is now possible at extreme cost. Several other trait values also changed. Where a mechanic is version-sensitive, this guide flags it explicitly. If you are playing the base game without Re-Awakening, some specific numbers may differ.

The Nakalim are the surviving remnants of an empire that served the Endless — and watched them die. Their defining mechanical identity is a structural asymmetry: they begin the game already knowing more technology than any other faction (Forgotten Lore: Tiers 1–2 pre-unlocked), but generate science at a fraction of the normal rate (Beliefs Not Breakthroughs: no science from planets, all science improvements 50% less effective, ship-destruction science halved). This is not a temporary early-game problem — it is a permanent ceiling that reshapes every decision about research, expansion, and military strategy.

The Nakalim's answer to this ceiling is threefold: Relics (which generate flat science independent of the planet malus), Hacking (which allows stealing technologies from opponents), and Temples (which sacrifice FIDS output to generate Influence at a rate that lets the Nakalim out-convert almost any neighbor). The faction that understands how these three tools interact — and prioritizes them correctly in the opening turns — plays a fundamentally different game from the one that simply tries to "play normally and cope with the science penalty."

FORGOTTEN LORE — THE STARTING ADVANTAGE
Nakalim begin with all Tier 1 and Tier 2 technologies already researched. This is the single most valuable early-game advantage in the faction: while opponents spend their first 20–30 turns unlocking basic colonization, food, and production upgrades, the Nakalim can go straight to Tier 3. The correct use of this advantage is not to skip the opening — it's to apply turns that would have been spent on Tiers 1–2 to accelerating the Tier 3 techs that matter most (hacking speed, terraforming, ship upgrades). The wiki frames it plainly: it makes the Nakalim an excellent explorer and early-harasser faction.
BELIEFS NOT BREAKTHROUGHS — THE PERMANENT TAX
Planets generate zero base Science. All science improvements (buildings, improvements) are 50% less effective. Science from destroying ships is also halved. Critically, Relic science (+50 per unassigned relic) is NOT cut in half — this exemption is what makes relics the primary science engine of the faction. The wiki's end-game analysis confirms this deficit never closes: in a full-game LP, the Sophons (science faction) maintained a science lead over the Nakalim for the entire game, even as the Nakalim won decisively on every other metric.

The correct mental model: the Nakalim do not fix their science problem. They route around it — using Forgotten Lore's head start, relic science, and hacking to stay relevant while their influence engine produces advantages that raw science cannot replicate.
THE LAST EMPIRE — RELICS, TEMPLES, AND THE ACADEMY
Nakalim can collect Relics from special map nodes, assign them to generate science or provide empire/hero bonuses, build Temple of the Lost planetary specializations (massive Influence per pop, sacrifices FIDS), and — when a system with a Temple is inside their influence zone — convert that system to Academy ownership. Academy conversion returns Relics, provides a permanent tithe, and counts toward Conquest victory. The full strategic loop: Temples → Influence → Zone expansion → Passive conversion → Relics and tithe income.
POPULATION TRAITS
Each Nakalim pop produces +2 Food and +2 Approval if the planet has a Temple of the Lost (Re-Awakening base values; verify against your version). Collection bonuses: 10 pops raises Religious political output, 20 pops gives +15% Dust on Nakalim systems, 50 pops gives +5% Resource Tithe on Academy-converted systems. The Xirmisala starting citizen is a unique minor faction pop unit with an exceptional growth penalty — detailed separately.
THE NAKALIM IN ONE SENTENCE
Use your tech head-start to explore aggressively and build influence early, route your science penalty through relics and hacking, convert the map to the Academy through temple-powered Influence, and win a Conquest victory that doesn't require you to match your opponents' science output.

The Relic Economy

Relics are simultaneously your science replacement, your hero enhancement system, and your empire bonus engine. Understanding assignment logic is prerequisite to using any of them well.
📦 What Relics Are and How They Work MECHANIC ▾
BASE BEHAVIOR — UNASSIGNED RELICS
Every unassigned relic generates +50 Science per turn. This science is not subject to the Nakalim's 50% science malus — it bypasses the planet-science system entirely. With 4 unassigned relics, you are generating 200 Science/turn in a clean channel that no other mechanic touches. This is why the wiki guide's opening advice is to "slot these relics as soon as possible" — even one relic is worth more than most Tier 2 science buildings for a Nakalim.
ASSIGNMENT IS NON-DESTRUCTIVE AND REVERSIBLE
Assigning a relic to an empire slot or a hero slot does NOT consume it. You can reassign freely with a 1-turn cooldown. This means the same relic can serve as a science generator during peacetime and as a fleet health bonus during a war phase — without losing anything. The wiki is explicit: "you will never 'use up' a relic with any usage." This reversibility changes the calculus entirely. You are not choosing between science and empire bonuses permanently; you are choosing where to park the relic this turn.
RELIC VISIBILITY — COMPETITIVE AWARENESS
Relics are invisible to all other factions except other Nakalim players. They also do not expire and are not time-limited. Against non-Nakalim opponents, you can safely leave relics uncollected until it is convenient to send a ship. The one critical exception: if another Nakalim player is in the game, relics become first-come, only-served and should be prioritized immediately. The wiki notes: "Unless another Nakalim threatens to take them, never spend your hero's precious time on the 4-turn slog of extracting a relic."
THE RELIC HUNTER SHIP
GUD's LP demonstrates a deliberate early build: a colonizer-class hull dedicated to relic retrieval. The colonizer hull has 3 support slots vs. 2 on other hulls, making it the cheapest, fastest dedicated ship for wide-map relic collection. Build this in the first few turns as a conscious decision, not as an afterthought. Stock it with pure engines. Free T1 quest reward ships and minor-faction fleet transfers are also effective relic runners — they spawn far afield and are not worth much in military use after the early game.

Forgotten Lore & the Early Relic Hunt

The tech head-start is a launch window — not a permanent advantage. Use it before opponents close the gap.
🔭 Opening Priorities: Research, Exploration, and the Relic Pipeline STRATEGY ▾

The Nakalim start with Tiers 1 and 2 already researched. This means every turn spent on a Tier 1 or 2 technology by an opponent is a turn the Nakalim can spend on Tier 3. The correct opening exploits this: go straight for the Tier 3 technologies that unlock the strongest mid-game capabilities, while simultaneously running a wide exploration pass to map relic locations and curiosities.

FIRST RESEARCH — THREE OPTIONS (GUD framework)
Autonomous Materials — unlocks an additional hacking operation plus speed. GUD's preference. Makes hacking a mid-game science equalizer immediately. Most relevant when you anticipate multiple opponents to steal tech from.

Colonize Lava — situational. Only correct if your starting system has multiple Lava planets waiting. The faster you colonize, the faster your Temple placement options expand.

Culture Center (Influence improvement) — relevant when targeting the early Academy conversion path. More relevant against opponents already near your starting zone.
EXPLORATION PHILOSOPHY — CURIOSITIES BEFORE RELICS
The wiki's advice is direct: explore curiosities first, with your hero-assigned explorer ships, because curiosity rewards are first-come-only-served. Relics sit unclaimed indefinitely (absent another Nakalim). Your hero's time is better spent gathering curiosity rewards (Dust, resources, quest triggers, free ships) than doing the 4-turn relic extraction slog. Once curiosities in a sector are cleared, then send a dedicated low-priority ship for the relics nearby. Dedicated relic runners (colonizer hull + pure engines, minor faction ships, free quest ships) should handle retrieval independently of your main exploration fleet.
STARTING SYSTEM — SOBRA
Sobra is a desert planet. The early food scarcity is the Nakalim's primary opener bottleneck — the Temple of the Lost reduces planet FIDS by 50%, which would tip a food-deficit planet into starvation. Do not build a Temple on Sobra in the first few turns. Colonize a second non-sterile planet first to create a food buffer, then erect an initial temple on whichever planet you are willing to sacrifice FIDS output on. The wiki notes the influence payoff is worth it "once you have a decent foothold — even as early as just colonizing another non-sterile planet in your home system."
RELIC NOTE — SCIENCE BEHEMOTHS
Science Behemoths (deployed at special nodes) are confirmed viable for the Nakalim — "Science behemoths are 100 OK" (GUD, LP02p01). The enhanced configuration provides approximately +480 Science. Mechanism: each behemoth module essentially replaces a relic in terms of science generation. Useful in mid-game when relic count is still building.

Relic Assignment: Empire Slots and Hero Slots

The same relic can serve three different roles — and you switch between them freely. The question is always: what is this relic worth more as right now?
⚗️ Empire Relic Slots — Cathedrals MECHANIC ▾

Empire relic slots are provided by Cathedrals (covered in Topic 5). Each Cathedral enables a set of empire-wide bonuses activated by assigning multiple relics to a slot. Only one bonus per Cathedral may be active at a time, but switching is free. The available slots:

🧭 EXPLORATION SLOT

+25% Movement Point efficiency, +5 Vision Range on Fleets & Systems. Accelerates the early relic hunt and reduces fleet travel time across the map. High value in opening turns.

🌍 EXPANSION SLOT

+1 Colonization Threshold per Cathedral, no disapproval from planet type on planets with temples. Directly enables wider temple placement without planetary satisfaction penalties.

🤝 DIPLOMACY SLOT

Constant minor faction relation bonus + increased influence radius. Supports the pacific conversion snowball by expanding your zone of influence faster.

⚖️ POLITICAL SLOT

Bonuses scale with active political party. Religious (preferred combo): Heroes earned faster, assignable outside borders. Pacifist: +100% Trade Value on Cathedral systems, +40% fleet health regen in friendly territory. Industrialist: +50% Strategic Deposit generation on Temples.

PRIORITY GUIDANCE
Early game: unassigned (science generation). Transition to Exploration or Diplomacy slot once you have 4+ relics and can spare 2 from the science pool. In war phases: pull relics from science to Political (Religious) for hero acceleration or Pacifist for fleet health regen. This is all reversible — do not overthink permanency.

PRIORITY LOGIC
Because assignment is binary (one relic = all bonuses, no relic = nothing), the decision simplifies to: which heroes benefit most from the package? The opportunity cost is straightforward: one hero relic costs 50 Science/turn from your unassigned pool. That is a real cost — weigh it against the concrete benefit the hero will generate. Reassignment carries a 1-turn cooldown; pull relics off governors before major fleet engagements and re-slot after.

Temples: Mechanics and Placement

The Temple of the Lost is the Nakalim's core expansion tool — not a minor building. Placement decisions determine your mid-game Influence radius and conversion capability.
🏛️ Temple Mechanics — What They Do and What They Cost MECHANIC ▾
⚠ RE-AWAKENING CHANGE — MECHANIC INVERTED
In the original Awakening DLC, temples imposed a -50% FIDS penalty on an otherwise normal planet. Re-Awakening reversed this: all Nakalim planets carry a -50% Base FIDS penalty by default. A Temple does not impose a penalty — it removes it, while redirecting the planet's base Science to Influence. The strategic framing changes entirely: temples are restoration, not sacrifice.
THE CORE MECHANIC
Every untempled Nakalim planet operates at -50% Base FIDS by default. Building a Temple of the Lost on a planet (cost: 1 Relic + 1 turn) does two things simultaneously:
  • Removes the -50% FIDS malus on that planet, restoring full FIDS output.
  • Converts the planet's base Science output into Influence.
The net result: a templed planet produces full Food, Industry, and Dust — but its Science contribution is redirected to Influence generation. This is not a FIDS sacrifice; it is a Science-to-Influence conversion on a planet that would otherwise generate Science at half value anyway.
WHAT THIS MEANS IN PRACTICE
An untempled planet is running at 50% efficiency across the board. The Temple restores that efficiency at the cost of Science — which the Nakalim generates poorly anyway. The correct read: every planet should eventually receive a Temple, not just low-value ones. The priority question becomes sequencing — which planets do you temple first to maximize early FIDS recovery and Influence output — rather than which planets to "sacrifice."

The +2 Approval per Nakalim pop on templed planets remains in Re-Awakening, making temple planets comfortable to operate at scale.
TEMPLES DO NOT AMPLIFY INFLUENCE CIRCLE GROWTH
Temples do not apply a hidden multiplier to sphere-of-influence radius growth. They convert Science to Influence, which feeds into the standard zone expansion mechanic at its normal rate. GUD observed this directly in the pre-Re-Awakening version: "the temple does not seem to have improved our radius as much as I would have expected." The wiki confirms the vanilla formulation applies. Scale expectations accordingly — the Influence output is real and substantial, but it compounds through population count, not through a growth multiplier.

📐 Placement Calculus: Where to Put Temples STRATEGY ▾

Since all planets carry the -50% FIDS penalty by default, the real placement question is sequencing: which planets do you temple first? The answer depends on two factors — Influence priority (how urgently you need zone expansion in a given direction) and Cathedral progress (you need temples on every planet in a system to unlock a Cathedral there).

TEMPLE HIGH-VALUE PLANETS FIRST
With the Re-Awakening mechanic, the priority logic inverts from the original. You want to restore FIDS on your most productive planets as early as possible — temple your best Food and Industry planets first, so they operate at full efficiency sooner. Low-value planets can stay untempled longer since their 50% penalty costs you less in absolute terms. GUD's original advice (temple low-value planets to minimize sacrifice) was correct for the old mechanic; under Re-Awakening, the reverse holds: high-value planets benefit most from having the penalty lifted.
TEMPORARY TEMPLES FOR DIRECTIONAL EXPANSION
GUD demonstrates a specific use case: placing a temporary Temple on a border planet specifically to push the influence zone in a particular direction — toward a target system for pacific conversion — then removing it once the zone has expanded to include that target. This is intentional: the influence zone, once it has expanded, does not retract when the temple is removed (the system still belongs to your influence). Use temples as directional levers for expansion, not only as permanent infrastructure.
THE INFLUENCE BOMB — STACKING ON A CAPTURED CAPITAL
The most powerful application, confirmed in GUD's late-game: capture a large enemy capital system with many planets and high population, place multiple temples on it, then assign a governor hero with the Joy Initiative skill. Joy Initiative sets a system's happiness to Ecstatic unconditionally. Ecstatic population generates a bonus Influence multiplier. The compound result — multiple temples × high pop count × Ecstatic multiplier — produces a sphere of influence that expands fast enough to passively capture adjacent enemy systems through pacific conversion, without further military action. This is the "influence bomb" strategy: it is not a single decision but a structure built over many turns, with the multi-temple capital as its trigger.
ACADEMY CONVERSION PREREQUISITES
A system can only be converted to the Academy when: (1) it is within your sphere of influence, and (2) you are not at war with the system's owner. GUD discovers this directly during the LP — he waits for truces before attempting conversions multiple times. Plan peace windows around your conversion queue.

🏦 Academy Conversion — What You Actually Get MECHANIC ▾

When a system with a Temple of the Lost is inside your influence zone and you are at peace with its owner, the Nakalim can convert it to Academy ownership. The conversion is permanent unless reconquered by the original owner.

MINOR FACTION CONVERSION — NO ASSIMILATION BONUS
If you convert a minor faction system to the Academy, you do not receive their assimilation trait. The assimilation bonus goes to Isyander (the Academy AI). This is a meaningful trade-off: assimilation traits can be powerful, and converting the system sacrifices them permanently. Evaluate whether the relic reward + tithe + conquest credit outweighs what you would gain from assimilating the minor faction conventionally.
LUXURY TITHE — OFTEN THE MOST VALUABLE PART
The wiki notes the luxury resource tithe "tends to be far more valuable" than the science or dust tithe in most game states. The Academy exploits the system's deposits and you receive half — comparable to running mining probes on the system, and permanent. Systems with good luxury deposits are prime early conversion targets precisely for this reason.
LODESTONE QUEST INTERRUPTS RELIC FLOW
When the Academy's Lodestone faction quest triggers, the Academy stops making requests — and therefore stops awarding relics for system donations until the quest resolves. GUD observes this directly: "all the relics in the universe now are all we have for the moment until that quest is complete." Time your major conversion waves around this — do not plan a big conversion push for the same turns when the Lodestone quest is active.

Cathedrals

The payoff for fully templing a system — substantial local bonuses plus empire-wide relic slots that reshape your strategic options.
📖 Cathedral Mechanics — Build Cost, Prerequisites, and System Bonuses MECHANIC ▾
PREREQUISITE — ALL PLANETS MUST HAVE TEMPLES
A Cathedral can only be built in a system where every planet already has a Temple of the Lost. There are no partial shortcuts. This makes the Cathedral a deliberate investment milestone rather than an early unlock — you must fully commit a system to the temple network before the Cathedral becomes available. Practical implication: target low planet-count systems first for your first Cathedral. A 2–3 planet system can be fully templed far faster than a 6-planet system.
BUILD COST AND SYSTEM BONUSES
Cost: 1,120 Industry, no Relics. Once built, the Cathedral provides the following bonuses directly to its host system:
  • +10% FIDSI on the system
  • +30% Influence Radius
  • +5 Vision
  • 5% Enemy Health damaged per turn for fleets within the Influence Radius
  • 20% Friendly Health repaired per turn for fleets within the Influence Radius
  • Increases the number of empire relic slots available (see below)
The fleet health regeneration bonus alone makes Cathedral systems natural fleet staging bases during war — any fleet resting in the Influence Radius of a Cathedral system heals at 20%/turn.

⚗️ Empire Relic Slots — One Active Per Cathedral MECHANIC ▾

Each Cathedral adds one empire relic slot to your pool. Each slot can be loaded with one of four types, but only one slot type may be active per Cathedral at a time. To run two different slot types simultaneously (e.g. Exploration and Diplomacy), you need two Cathedrals. Switching between slot types is free and toggleable.

The exact number of relics required to activate each slot type is not specified in available sources — the changelog states only "multiple relics." Treat this as an in-game UI detail to verify on first Cathedral construction.

🧭 EXPLORATION SLOT

+25% Movement Point efficiency, +5 Vision Range on Fleets & Systems. Most useful in the early-to-mid game when relic collection and map control are the priority.

🌍 EXPANSION SLOT

+1 Colonization Threshold per Cathedral, no disapproval from planet type on templed planets. Scales with Cathedral count — each additional Cathedral adds +1 threshold when this slot is active.

🤝 DIPLOMACY SLOT

Constant minor faction relation bonus + increased influence radius. Supports the pacific conversion snowball by expanding your zone and keeping minor factions aligned.

⚖️ POLITICAL SLOT

Bonuses vary by leading party. Religious (preferred): Heroes earned faster, assignable outside borders. Pacifist: +100% Trade Value on Cathedral systems, +40% fleet health regen in friendly territory. Militarist: -20% retrofit cost, retrofit outside friendly territory.

MULTI-CATHEDRAL PRIORITY
Your first Cathedral enables one active slot. Your second enables a second independent slot — allowing two slot types to run in parallel. The Expansion Slot's colonization threshold bonus also scales (+1 per Cathedral), making each additional Cathedral directly increase how many systems you can hold comfortably. Build toward multiple Cathedrals as a mid-game goal, not a late-game luxury.

✨ Coupled Collaboration — Doubling Slot Output MECHANIC ▾
WHAT IT DOES
The faction quest reward Coupled Collaboration (Chapter 4.2, Pacifist branch) doubles the output of all active empire relic slots, empire-wide. This is a bonus output multiplier — if the Diplomacy slot provides X influence radius increase, Coupled Collaboration makes it 2X. The doubling applies to all Cathedrals simultaneously, not just the Cathedral in the system where the improvement is built.
STRATEGIC TIMING
Coupled Collaboration arrives as a late quest reward, so its full value requires that your Cathedral network is already built and active by the time it triggers. The more Cathedrals you have running when Coupled Collaboration arrives, the larger the immediate empire-wide impact. This makes Cathedral construction in the mid-game a prerequisite for getting full value from the quest reward — not something to defer until the reward is already in hand.

Rebuilding Science: Managing the Permanent Deficit

You cannot fix the science problem. You can route around it. The tools are relics, hacking, behemoths, and the Forgotten Lore head-start — used together.
🔬 Science Sources and the Routing Framework STRATEGY ▾

The end-game verdict from a full LP: "The Sophons had us the entire game. We were never ever gonna catch up to them on science." This is not a failure state — the Nakalim won decisively while never matching the Sophon science output. The correct framing is not "close the gap" but "stay relevant enough, long enough, through other means."

TOOL 1 — RELICS (PRIMARY)
+50 Science per unassigned relic, bypass the malus entirely. Target: 6–10 relics in the mid-game gives you 300–500 Science/turn in a clean channel. Forgotten Lore plus this relic baseline is what makes the Nakalim viable in Tier 3 and 4 research. Prioritize relic collection early; the compound value of having 8 relics at Turn 30 vs. Turn 60 is enormous.
TOOL 2 — HACKING (SECONDARY)
Autonomous Materials tech (recommended first Tier 3 research) unlocks additional hacking operations and speed. Hacking enemy systems via the hacking tree allows technology theft directly. GUD: "hack them and steal their techs." Version note: original Nakalim trait said "Cannot obtain technology from other empires" — this excluded diplomatic tech exchange but hacking operated through a separate mechanic. Hacking also has a second confirmed powerful use: Jam Commands on an enemy capital forces the defender to choose a non-optimal ground tactic, enabling Blitz invasion of systems that would otherwise require many siege turns (GUD LP02p02, confirmed live on Myrop). This is independent of science but central to efficient military operations.

Science Behemoths deployed at special nodes provide ~+480 Science in enhanced configuration. The wiki and GUD confirm this. Treat as mid-game relic equivalents — each behemoth module "essentially replacing a relic per module" in terms of science generation. Build and deploy when Industry permits; do not delay past mid-game.
TOOL 4 — BATTLE SCIENCE SALVAGE
The wiki describes a specific module stack: equip Protector hulls (3–6 utility slots) with multiple science-salvage modules. Payout is multiplicative per module, not flat. The caveat: as of a later patch, science from ship destruction also suffers the 50% malus. This makes the per-battle yield lower than it appears, but still non-trivial in a war-heavy game. The Take Trophies battle tactic stacks with these modules.

Faction Quest & Political Combo

The quest branches gate powerful unique improvements. Political combination shapes the mid-to-late game's economic and military character.
📜 Quest Structure and Branch Selection MECHANIC ▾

The Nakalim questline has multiple branching chapters. The most strategically consequential branch rewards come from Chapter 4:

CHAPTER 4.1 — UNIQUE SYSTEM IMPROVEMENTS
Temple of the Denaserdi: +1 Influence per Nakalim population (Pacifist path) or +2 Influence on Planets, +50% Influence, +10% on Awakened Ruins and Cathedrals (Re-Awakening path). Directly amplifies the influence engine.

Relic Exploitation Site: +3 Industry per Nakalim population (original) or +2 Industry + 50% Industry on Awakened Ruins. Converts Nakalim population into an Industry multiplier.

Galactic Faith Museum: +1 Approval per Nakalim population (original) or massive Approval multipliers on Ruins/Cathedrals. Happiness management.

Center for Denaserdi Remembrance: +3 Dust per Nakalim population. Economy amplifier.
CHAPTER 4.2 — EMPIRE-WIDE REWARDS
Cathedral to the Lost: +3% FIDSI on System per Temple to the Lost built in the Empire. This is the late-game FIDSI multiplier — with 10 temples empire-wide, every system gains +30% FIDSI. Build as many temples as possible before this reward arrives.

Coupled Collaboration: Double effects from relics assigned to empire slots. Immediately doubles every Cathedral empire bonus — paired with good relic management, this is transformative.
REPUBLIC + SPECIES STABILITY — THE EARLY LAW STACK
GUD recommends starting as a Republic and taking the Species Stability law immediately. Effect: every time a new population unit arrives in a conquered system, you receive a Nakalim pop. This accelerates Nakalim population spread through military expansion, which directly scales every per-Nakalim-pop building bonus. The law weakens after a government change, so the value window is in the early-to-mid game while Republic is active.

⚖️ Recommended Political Combination STRATEGY ▾
RELIGIOUS + PACIFIST — GUD'S PREFERRED COMBO
"Religious pacifist is actually my preferred combination with this faction." (GUD, LP02p01)

Religious: Enables cold-war invasion mechanics (fight in cold war, apply economic coercion without formal war declaration). Heroes earned faster and assignable outside borders (via relic Political slot). All Religious political events support Nakalim pop output.

Pacifist: Happiness bonuses, minor faction bonuses. Also directly enables force truce — the ability to unilaterally impose peace on an enemy when at max diplomatic pressure. This is crucial for the conversion strategy: raid an enemy to drain their manpower and resources, then force peace to resume conversion of their systems. GUD demonstrates this explicitly in LP02p02.
DIPLOMATIC PRESSURE AS WEAPON
Active use of imbroglios and the pressure system is a core Nakalim tool with Religious + Pacifist. At maximum pressure, you can demand tribute where refusal costs the enemy -15% to -30% all FIDSI. GUD: "This is why war demands are so 100 guys." He extracts adamantium, quadranix, science agreements, and antimatter from multiple factions through the pressure pipeline without formal war declarations. SCIENCE SALVAGE PROTECTORS — THE BATTLE-SCIENCE STACK
The Nakalim Coordinator (Psalm) has 3 uncontested utility slots and up to 6 total at upgrade. Stack science-salvage modules on Coordinator hulls: each module independently pays out the same science per destroyed CP as a tactic card, multiplied by module count. A fleet with 4 Coordinators each running 5 salvage modules generates significant science income from each decisive engagement. Note: post-patch, ship-destruction science is halved — factor this into your module budget.
ACADEMY INSIGNIA FLEET — MID-GAME MILITARY BUMP
Systems converted to the Academy can generate an Insignia fleet — a purchasable high-power fleet of approximately 5,000 combat power available for Dust. GUD: "Buy a fleet from Academy for 849 Dust — a 5,000-power ship mid-game is huge." This requires a temple in the system to be active. The Xirmisala citizen on the home system has a growth rate penalty so severe it is effectively not a growable population: wiki notes "-2400 chance to increment" (original version). Do not try to breed Xirmisala for collection bonuses — the effort is not worth it without specific mechanics supporting it.

Strategic Directions

Two viable approaches — both use the same faction toolkit, but they prioritize different leverage points and win through different mechanisms.
🕊️ Path A — The Snowball Converter: Temples, Influence, and the Academy STRATEGY ▾
THE PACIFIC CONQUEST LOOP

Temples generate Influence → Influence expands your zone → Enemy systems enter your zone → Academy conversion transfers them → Conversion generates Relics and tithe → Relics fund science → More relics fund better relic bonuses → Influence zone expands further. The loop compounds on itself. Military force is used only when the loop stalls — to break a war that is blocking conversion windows, or to capture a key capital for the influence bomb.

CORE STRUCTURE
1. Expand wide early (Forgotten Lore lets you colonize efficiently from turn 1).
2. Place temples on low-value planets in systems adjacent to expansion targets.
3. Keep Religious + Pacifist political combo active for pressure tools and force-truce.
4. Use the Joy Initiative governor on your highest-pop system (ideally a captured capital with multiple temples) to trigger the Influence Bomb.
5. Convert systems during peace windows. Rebuild pressure during truces. Convert again.
6. Conquest victory: Academy systems count. You do not need to occupy all capitals militarily.
HERO ROLES IN THIS PATH
Overseers with Joy Initiative: placed on the influence-bomb system to maintain Ecstatic happiness → amplified Influence → sphere expansion.
Counselors with Bean Counter: governors on high-output systems for FIDSI efficiency.
Fleet commanders with relics: assigned health/damage relics before major engagements, reassigned to system slots afterward.
THE INFLUENCE BOMB — EXECUTION SUMMARY
Capture a large enemy capital (multi-planet, high pop). Place 3–5 temples on its planets. Assign a governor with Joy Initiative. The system goes Ecstatic. Ecstatic multiplier × multi-temple Influence output × high pop count = sphere expansion fast enough to passively absorb adjacent systems. Then convert them all during the next peace window. GUD achieves this in LP02p02 against Vitarka (the former Riftborn capital), confirms it live. This is the late-game payoff of the entire temple-building investment.

🗡️ Path B — The Early Bully: Forgotten Lore Aggression and Pressure Snowball STRATEGY ▾
THE PRESSURE RATCHET

Forgotten Lore gives you Tier 1–2 military tech from turn 1. Use it to harass early, build imbroglios aggressively, and extract tribute from opponents before they stabilize. Diplomatic pressure at max forces FIDSI penalties without formal war. War demands extract resources. Hack enemy capitals for tech theft and Jam Commands. The Nakalim early bully is not a warmongering faction — it is a coercive faction that wins by making every neighbor pay a constant toll.

CORE STRUCTURE
1. Research Autonomous Materials first (hacking speed + operation unlock).
2. Deploy a combat fleet to the nearest opponent early — use Take Trophies for Dust and science salvage.
3. Build diplomatic pressure through imbroglios and proximity. Never formally declare war unless forced — cold war coercion is cheaper.
4. Use hacking to establish backdoors in key enemy systems. Jam Commands before ground invasions (forces bad tactic, enables Blitz).
5. Extract resources through war demands. Extort quadranix, adamantium, science treaties from weakened opponents.
6. Convert any system that enters your influence zone through the ongoing temple network — this path uses conversion opportunistically rather than as the primary win condition.
HACKING — JAM COMMANDS IN DETAIL
GUD's live discovery: hacking an enemy capital and selecting Jam Commands forces the defender to choose a non-draft ground tactic. Without Draft, their manpower regeneration during the siege is severely limited. A system that would require 10+ siege turns to exhaust its manpower falls to Blitz in one action. GUD: "I have just found my favorite new use for hacking. I am sold one hundred and fifty percent on hacking right now." This is not situational — it is a consistent force multiplier for every military campaign. Always hack the target capital before committing your invasion fleet.

First 40 Turns Playbook

Turn-by-turn priorities for establishing the Nakalim's early systems before opponents close the Forgotten Lore gap.
🗓️ Turn Sequence: Opening to Mid-Game Transition STRATEGY ▾
TURNS 1–5
First research decision: Choose based on strategic context. Default: Autonomous Materials (hacking speed, extra operation) — gives you the pressure toolkit online soonest. If your start has lava planets you want immediately: Colonize Lava. If your start is tight and you want to rush Academy influence: Culture Center (Influence improvement).

Exploration fleet launched immediately. Assign your hero to the explorer ship. Map the starting region for curiosities, minor factions, and relic locations. Curiosities are time-sensitive; relics are not.

Relic hunter built in turns 1–3: Colonizer hull (3 support slots) with pure engines. This ship runs the relic circuit independently of your hero. Do not send your hero to collect relics.
TURNS 5–15
Food buffer before temples: Colonize at least one non-sterile planet in your home system or a nearby system. Do not build a Temple on Sobra until this food buffer is in place — the 50% FIDS penalty tips food-deficient planets into starvation. Once colonized, your home system can absorb the penalty on a lower-value planet.

First temple placement: Identify the lowest-value planet in your home system (minimal FIDS contribution). Place the Temple there. The influence output begins immediately and grows with every pop on that planet.

Hacking foundation: If you researched Autonomous Materials, begin building hacking infrastructure. Identify the nearest opponent capital for future backdoor installation. A backdoor in a neighboring capital is worth 20+ turns of preparation before the first war.
TURNS 15–25
Second colony + temple: Your second colony should include at least one planet suitable for a Temple. Place it on the lowest-value planet in that system. Each temple system now generates Influence from two locations, compounding zone expansion.

Relic count check: By turn 20 you should have 3–5 relics from the relic hunter circuit. Hold them unassigned for now (50 Science/relic/turn). Do not assign to hero slots until you have 4+ total — the slot-fill-order requirement means partial assignment is wasteful.

Minor faction relations: The early Influence lead makes the Nakalim naturally strong at minor faction relations. If a minor faction system is in or near your zone, prioritize converting them before a neighbor does. Minor faction fleet transfers provide free ships for the relic hunter circuit.
TURNS 25–40
First target system enters influence zone: Identify the first enemy or minor system that will enter your zone. Begin the hacking chain toward its capital if relevant. Confirm you are not at war before the system converts — the conversion action requires peace.

Academy competition awareness: The Nakalim is well-positioned for early Academy competitions (strong Influence, Dust from curiosity sales). The Spear of Asunder role is generally preferred for the early game. Win it by Turns 30–40 if possible — the first competition win provides laws and bonuses that compound through the mid-game.

Republic + Species Stability: If your government is Republic, take Species Stability immediately. Every new population unit in a conquered system adds a Nakalim pop — scales all your per-Nakalim-pop building bonuses as you expand.

Super Biofuel Factory path (optional): GUD identifies this as the late-game production inflection point (~turn 98 in his game). The factory converts food production into industry on the same planet. Nakalim (Religious, high pop, food output) can route food surpluses from non-temple planets into production without sacrificing FIDS. Begin the research chain that enables this in the mid-game.
THE NAKALIM TIPPING POINT
Unlike Horatio (which tips at turns 50–70 through population scaling), the Nakalim tips at turns 70–100 when the influence network, relic economy, and temple infrastructure all compound together. The first 40 turns set up the infrastructure. The middle game expands it. The late game deploys it — Academy conversions cascade, pressure extracts tribute, and the Science deficit stops mattering because you are winning on every other axis simultaneously.
