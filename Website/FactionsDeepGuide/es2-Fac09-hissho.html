ES2 // Fac.09 // The Hissho

The
Hissho

Warrior birds who replaced Approval with honor, colonization with conquest, and diplomacy with violence. The Hissho are a tall-play faction disguised as an aggressor — their real engine is a single, dense home system fed by mining probes stripping the galaxy bare while their warriors collect Keii from every kill. They are not a rush faction. They are a compounding fortress that cannot stop fighting without losing what it built.

00

Faction Identity: The Tall War Machine

Fewer systems than anyone. More output per system than most. Always at war.
🪶 Core Traits and What Makes the Hissho Different OVERVIEW ▾
◈ HISSHO — BIRDS OF WAR ◈

The Hissho are the only major faction in ES2 that fully replaces the Approval system with something else. Where every other empire manages population happiness to maintain FIDSI output, the Hissho manage Keii — a centralized honor resource that functions as an empire-wide multiplier and a currency simultaneously. This is not a cosmetic swap. It changes how expansion works, how laws function, how you fight, and what winning looks like. The player who treats Keii as "Hissho Approval" and plays them like a standard faction will perform below average. The player who understands that Keii is earned through violence and spent on growth — and that the entire faction identity is built around that loop — will find one of the strongest mid-game factions in the game.

HONOR BOUND — THE APPROVAL REPLACEMENT
Hissho have no Approval. Approval-based FIDSI multipliers, Approval-based overcolonization penalties, and Approval-based laws are all replaced by Keii equivalents. Systems cannot rebel due to low Approval. What they can do is become severely unproductive — and ultimately enter Anarchy — if Keii collapses. The Sacred Tradition trait ensures the government type doesn't change in rebellion, making Anarchy a production crisis rather than a political one.
RESOURCE RECOVERERS — THE ECONOMIC ENGINE
Each active mining probe in the empire adds a FIDSI bonus to the home system equal to 15% of the probed planet's FIDSI per population unit living in the home system. This is multiplicative with home system population — a dense home system turns every mining operation across the galaxy into home system output. The starting Economic Behemoth is the delivery mechanism for this entire economic identity.
PIRATE SLAYERS — DIPLOMATIC EXILE
Hissho cannot interact diplomatically with pirates at all. In exchange, they earn twice the loot from destroying a pirate lair. This removes the option to buy off pirates as a raiding tool, but every pirate lair in your territory is worth actively hunting, and every early skirmish generates Keii and double loot simultaneously.
BEHEMOTH BUILDERS & DISCOVERER — THE ECONOMIC HEAD START
Hissho start with +1 Behemoth production cap beyond the normal limit, and begin the game with an Economic Behemoth already deployed on their home system. This Behemoth cannot be upgraded or modified until the Behemoth Blueprints technology is researched (unlocked via the faction quest). Until then, it carries 1 probe module and a +10 FIDSI module — functional but locked.
COLONIZATION CONSTRAINT — THE DEFINING WEAKNESS
The Resource Recoverers trait reduces the colonization threshold by 1: the overcolonization penalty activates at 2 systems instead of the normal 3. Each system above the limit imposes a flat −5% FIDSI penalty on all systems empire-wide. Only two expansion limit increases exist in the game: Federation government (+1) and Autonomous Administration (+1, late game). Plan to run 2–4 colonies maximum for the entire game.
STERILE WORLD IMMUNITY — ILLO COLONIZERS
Hissho have no Approval penalty for colonizing sterile planets, and empire development technologies grant cumulative population cap increases of +3 on sterile worlds. Gas giant systems — high FIDSI, sterile planets — are excellent mining targets and secondary colonization candidates without the output penalty that would cripple other factions.
THE HISSHO IN ONE SENTENCE
Maximize a single dense home system via mining probes and Fealty specializations, maintain Keii above 85 through continuous warfare, and raze everything you conquer rather than trying to govern it.

Keii: Full Mechanics

The FIDSI multiplier, the currency, and the resource you must never let run dry.
⚡ How Keii Works as a Multiplier MECHANIC ▾

Keii is a single empire-wide value ranging from 0 to 100. It replaces the per-system Approval score and functions identically to Approval as a FIDSI multiplier — the percentage output of all Hissho systems is determined by the current Keii value according to the same scaling table Approval uses. Unlike Approval, which varies per system, Keii is consolidated: the entire empire rises and falls together.

KEII THRESHOLDS & FIDSI EFFECT
Keii operates on the same 0–100 multiplier curve as Approval. Critical operating zones:

85–100 (Devoted/Loyal): Maximum FIDSI multiplier. Fealty Center construction is gated here (~95+). Maintain this range as the default operational target.
70–85 (Ideal operating range): Strong output. Community guidance recommends 70 as the practical floor during active wars where Keii fluctuates turn to turn.
50–70: Noticeable output loss. The FIDSI modifier degrades meaningfully — avoid spending extended time here.
Below 50: Severe FIDSI penalties. Empire enters crisis state. Unlike other factions, systems do not rebel — but near-zero Keii cripples total output simultaneously.

⚠ NOTE: Exact percentage values at each threshold match the Approval table (wiki-sourced). Threshold labels (Devoted, Loyal) are community conventions, not in-game text — treat as operational guidelines.
GOVERNMENT MODIFIER — REPUBLIC MULTIPLIER
The wiki FIDSI modifier table for Hissho shows that Republic government provides a higher effective FIDSI multiplier than Dictatorship at the same Keii value. Republic also intensifies the parenthetical law bonuses — including Hatched at Home. Republic at 85 Keii outperforms Dictatorship at 85 Keii. This is the primary mechanical justification for eventually transitioning to Republic despite election overhead.

Retreating costs 10 flat Keii. Losing an individual ship in a fight you win costs zero Keii and may generate significant Keii from kills. Resource Recovery Engine

The Economic Behemoth is not support infrastructure — it is the primary economic engine of the faction.
🌑 How Mining Probes Feed the Home System MECHANIC ▾

The Resource Recoverers affinity: each active mining probe anywhere in the empire applies a bonus to the home system equal to 15% of the probed planet's FIDSI output, multiplied by every population unit currently living in the home system. The probe does not affect the system it is deployed on — it redirects a fraction of that planet's potential output back to your capital.

CALCULATION EXAMPLE
A large Gas Giant planet with values of 5F / 8I / 6D / 4S / 2E: the probe takes 15% of each value and adds it as a per-pop bonus to the home system. With 8 pops at home: (0.75F + 1.2I + 0.9D + 0.6S + 0.3E) × 8 = meaningful bonus across all FIDSI categories. A dense home system (10–12 pops) turns a single Gas Giant probe into the equivalent output of a well-developed colony added directly to your capital.
STARTING BEHEMOTH — CONSTRAINTS AND UNLOCK
The starting Economic Behemoth cannot be upgraded or have its modules changed until you research Behemoth Blueprints (unlocked by the faction quest, Chapter 1). Until then it runs with its default 1 probe module and +10 FIDSI module. This is functional but means the stacking strategy only becomes available at quest unlock — an additional reason to prioritize questline progression early.

THE STACKING TRICK — 1 ENGINE + 4 DEEP MINERS
Community consensus (Knofbath mini-guide via wiki): load the Behemoth with 1 Engine + 4 Deep Mining Probes. Duration stacking gives approximately 40 turns of continuous probe uptime. At a full home system this equates to adding the equivalent output of a fully populated planet to your capital for those 40 turns — repeated indefinitely as the Behemoth cycles positions.
PROBE FRAGILITY — PROTECT YOUR INVESTMENTS
Any hostile fleet entering the probed system destroys the probe immediately — including rival outpost ships, pirates, spawning pirate bases, and quest fleets. The ideal setup is an entire arm of uninhabited systems reserved exclusively for mining. Station 2–3 cheap defender ships in each actively mined system. They don't need to win major engagements — they only need to intercept colony ships and deter casual incursions.
GAS GIANTS — TOP PRIORITY TARGETS
Gas Giants have the highest FIDSI total per planet in the game. They are sterile (no penalty for Hissho), typically in uninhabited deep-space arms, and generate outsized Resource Recovery output. Identify Gas Giant systems during scouting and route your Behemoth to mine them first.

Expansion Doctrine: The Colonization Constraint

The Hissho overcolonization penalty isn't a soft suggestion. It is the reason they raze everything.
🗺️ The −5% Penalty and Why Conquering Beats Colonizing MECHANIC ▾
THE OVERCOLONIZATION MATH
Hissho have a base colonization limit of 2 systems (−1 from the standard 3, per Resource Recoverers). Each system above that limit imposes a flat −5% FIDSI penalty on all systems empire-wide. At 4 systems over cap that is −20% across the board — equivalent to losing almost a full Keii tier of output. The wiki notes this penalty was added as a patch mechanic after the original Keii-only gate was insufficient to deter infinite expansion.
THE TWO WAYS TO RAISE THE LIMIT
Federation government: +1 system before penalty. Meaningful but Federation is often not the optimal government choice (see Topic 6).

Autonomous Administration: +1 limit. Requires a level-4 system — late game only.

Practical conclusion: plan for 2–4 colonies maximum for the entire game. Accepting a small FIDSI penalty (5–10%) for one or two strategic systems is different from sprawling into 8+ systems and destroying your output.
OUTPOST FOREVER — THE DELAYED CONVERSION TRICK
Hissho outposts do not automatically become colonies — they stay outposts until you spend Keii to convert them. The Keii cost is high when you first establish the outpost and drops over time. You can claim territory and block opponents with outposts while paying zero FIDSI penalty — the penalty only triggers on full colonies. Queue Ancestral Reverence on any system you do convert: this building exempts the system from Hatched at Home's Keii upkeep.
CONQUEST IS COST-NEUTRAL; COLONIZATION IS NOT
Taking an enemy system via invasion gives Keii from subduing and +5 from razing. Colonizing an empty system costs escalating Keii and adds to the FIDSI penalty. The economics almost always favor conquest — except for systems adjacent to your home system (covered by one Behemoth) or holding unique strategic resources you cannot acquire elsewhere.
SYSTEM QUALITY THRESHOLD
When evaluating whether to colonize (and accept the penalty), apply this filter: does the system have 5+ planets, high-value resources (Titanium, Hyperium, or premium luxury deposits), or a position that lets your Behemoth cover it and another target simultaneously? If not — raze it, mine it, move on.

Observances: Three-Cycle Ritual Management

One of three appears each election. One pop sacrifice for a powerful empire-wide effect. Only one of the three is a trap.
🔥 The Three Observances and When to Use Each MECHANIC ▾

Every election cycle, one of three Observances becomes available. Performing one sacrifices a single population unit from any system and applies the effect empire-wide for 10 turns.

SUN GOD'S RITUAL
FOOD → MANPOWER CONVERSION
Converts food production to manpower for 10 turns. Useful only in a specific scenario: you are at war, manpower is critically depleted, you have significant food surplus, and cannot wait for normal recovery. Outside that window this is actively harmful — it stops population growth for 10 turns at the moment your home system should be densifying for mining synergy. The YT01 narrator explicitly warns against reflexively triggering it. Do not use this as a default action.
BLOOD RITUAL
+5 KEII FLAT
Grants +5 Keii empire-wide. Useful when below 85 and needing to push into Devoted territory before queuing Fealty Centers, or when Keii has taken damage from a recent colonization and needs rebalancing. Perform this on your cheapest or least productive system (to sacrifice the least useful pop). The cost is 1 pop; the return is a permanent Keii bump worth roughly half a turn's combat income.
DILIGENCE RITUAL
+50% DUST FOR 10 TURNS
The most consistently valuable Observance. A 50% Dust bonus for 10 turns stacks with other Dust multipliers (laws, system improvements, developments) and funds ship upgrades, buyouts, and high fleet upkeep during continuous warfare. Prioritize this whenever available unless you have a specific, urgent manpower crisis that cannot wait for natural recovery.
TIMING RELATIVE TO ELECTIONS
Observances are tied to election cycles — one becomes available per cycle. You cannot defer them to the next election. If the current Observance is Sun God's Ritual and you have no manpower crisis, the 1-pop sacrifice for a currently-useless effect is generally not worth performing.

Fealty Specializations & System Development

The Hissho permanent system buff. Costs 25 Keii. Returns +3 FIDSI/pop forever.
🏛️ Fealty Mechanics, Banking, and Luxury Priorities MECHANIC ▾
WHAT FEALTY SPECIALIZATIONS DO
Fealty Center, Fealty Foundation, and Fealty Institute are unique planet specializations unlocked by researching Behemoth Blueprints. Each costs −25 Keii to queue. Once built, they permanently add +3 FIDSI per population unit on that system. This bonus persists through conquest. The structures auto-upgrade as you research higher-tier versions.
TIMING — BUILD ONLY AT HIGH KEII
The −25 Keii cost triggers on queue, not on completion. Queue Fealty structures only when at 95–100 Keii. Do not queue them below 85 — you knock yourself out of the Devoted/Loyal tier precisely when the FIDSI multiplier matters most for the building's output. Community guidance: "Whenever you have 95–100 Keii, build one in your home system. Don't let Keii get out of loyal for too long."
BANKING TECHNIQUE — CONVERTING COMBAT GAINS INTO DEVELOPMENT
Pre-queue Fealty structures before a battle at 95–100 Keii. The −25 cost immediately reduces your current value, making room for incoming battle Keii. You fight, gain Keii, don't waste overflow — it flows into the now-lowered total. The Fealty structure builds from your Industry queue. Net result: combat income converts into permanent FIDSI improvements rather than hitting the cap and vanishing.
APPROVAL LUXURIES — THE HISSHO REPLACEMENT EFFECTS
Since Hissho have no Approval, luxury resources that normally grant Approval instead grant FIDSI bonuses based on Keii state (from wiki economy data):

Transvine (common): +20 FIDSI on System if Devoted; +15 if Loyal.
Lost Cities (uncommon): +3 FIDSI/pop if Devoted; +2 FIDSI/pop if Loyal.
Benthic Gems (rare): +15% FIDSI per System Level if Devoted; +10% if Loyal.

Lost Cities and Benthic Gems are exceptional for a tall, dense home system — per-pop and per-level bonuses multiply with population density. The Steam guide describes Benthic Gems at tier 3 as equivalent to four tier-3 standard luxuries combined. Prioritize finding these on the market early; they compound with Fealty Centers for multiplicative output gains.

🏛️ Government Progression: Dictatorship → Ecologist → Republic STRATEGY ▾
STARTING DICTATORSHIP — EXPLOIT IT EARLY
Hissho begin as a Dictatorship: 1.5× Senator skill effects, election stability, but only 2 law slots. Priority on Turn 1 is to adopt Hatched at Home immediately. Second law slot holds Hissho Holiday Act during wars, or another combat law once Militarist senators develop.
FIRST ELECTION — ECOLOGIST FOR COLONIZATION
Bring in an Ecologist representative at the first election. The primary reason: Ecologist government or laws unlock colonization of planet types without researching the relevant tech — specifically useful for establishing your second system on whatever good planet is nearby without waiting for a tech unlock. The wiki mini-guide confirms: "Choose Ecologist during first election to colonize planets in your second system without research."
MID-GAME TRANSITION TO REPUBLIC
Republic is the endgame government. The Republic intensification of Hatched at Home (from +25% to ~+31.25% FIDSI) and improved law bonuses make Republic substantially more output-efficient. The wiki FIDSI modifier table shows Republic multipliers beating Dictatorship at the same Keii value. Election overhead and potential law disruption during the transition are the only real costs.
FEDERATION — RARELY WORTH IT
Federation adds +1 to the colonization limit. For a faction that wants 2–4 colonies anyway, this is marginal. Federation comes with Influence overhead and political complications not justified by +1 colony. Republic is the better default endgame government.

Ships & Fleet Philosophy

More uncontested attack slots than most. Less dedicated defense. Three distinct build directions.
🚀 Hull Philosophy and the Three Build Directions MECHANIC ▾

The defining characteristic of Hissho hulls is more uncontested attack slots, at the cost of dedicated defense slots being replaced with hybrid Defense/Utility slots. This is not a weakness — it is a design language. You choose whether to fill hybrid slots with defense (survivable brawler), utility (amplified glass cannon), or a mix. Three fleet archetypes emerge, all viable:

THREE BUILD DIRECTIONS (across all hull classes)
Armored Slow: Fill hybrid slots with defense modules. Max armor/shields. Slower, survives longer — ideal for sustained sieges where Keii zone stacking is available.

Fast Glass Cannon: Fill hybrid slots with engines. Maximum speed, minimum defense. Works well with Hissho Holiday Act — frequent fights, fast deaths, cheap replacement cycle.

Weapon-Amplified Offense: Fill hybrid slots with damage amplifiers and utility modules. Prioritizes killing the enemy before they kill you. Best against lower-defense opponents.
THE VENERABLE STANDARD — JUGGERNAUT MODULE
Hissho have a unique Juggernaut module: the Venerable Standard. Effect: ×2 Keii gain for the entire flotilla, +10% Energy damage, +10% Projectile damage. This is the only Juggernaut module that doubles combat Keii income. If running a Juggernaut, the Venerable Standard is always included. Combined with Hissho Holiday Act, Tombs & Trinkets, and the Shrine of Eternal Sacrifice, late-game battles near a Juggernaut become enormous Keii generation events.

War Doctrine: Escalation, Keii Warfare & Ground Combat

Subdue → Raze. Repeat. The Hissho war machine is not built to hold territory — it is built to strip it.
⚔️ The Escalation Sequence and Combat Economics STRATEGY ▾
ESCALATION SEQUENCE: PIRATES → MINORS → NEIGHBORS
Early game: Deploy the starting Behemoth to contest pirate lairs (Pirate Slayers: 2× loot). Use cheap combat-refitted scouts. Collect Keii and loot simultaneously. Explore aggressively — every new system is +1 Keii.

Mid-early: Attack and subdue minor factions within striking range. Subdue yields significant Keii; raze if no value in keeping. Leave as tribute if they have a useful FIDSI bonus.

Mid game: First neighbor war. Use Jingoist Joy Bill (if available) to declare without diplomatic cost. Target the weakest neighbor's outermost systems first. Subdue → Raze for maximum Keii and minimum system overhead.
SUBDUE vs OCCUPY vs RAZE — THE ECONOMICS
Subdue (Hissho unique, replaces Pillage): Yields a large Keii gain. No Approval penalty. Diminishing returns on subduing the same system too quickly — wait 10+ turns before subduing the same target again for full value.

Occupy (capture system): Useful only for enemy capitals in a Supremacy Victory run. Otherwise creates FIDSI penalty and Keii upkeep without commensurate benefit. Avoid for non-capital systems unless they meet the quality threshold (5+ planets, strategic resources).

Raze: +5 Keii. Destroys the system permanently. Default action for most captured systems.
SACRED TRADITION — THE TACTICAL INTELLIGENCE ADVANTAGE
At Hot War status, Hissho can see the movement paths and destinations of enemy fleets. This lets you intercept reinforcements before they arrive, avoid unfavorable engagements, and pick fights selectively. Combined with Pirate Slayers' forced aggression posture, Sacred Tradition partially compensates for the lack of diplomatic options by letting you control the military information landscape.
MANPOWER MANAGEMENT IN EXTENDED WARS
Keep Food output high and research Food-to-Manpower conversion technologies. A tactical trick for forward operations: abandon an old system and annex a forward system as a manpower resupply point, eliminating the need to return to your home system between engagements. Effective for extended Supremacy campaigns where returning to base costs 20–30 turns of fleet idle time. (Steam guide)

Faction Quest: Path Choices & Rewards

Chapter 1 is a meaningful fork. The Gouge of Tonatsi is almost always the right choice.
📖 All Four Chapters: Conditions, Choices, and Rewards MECHANIC ▾
CHAPTER 1 — MILITARIST vs RELIGIOUS

Choice A (Militarist): Wrath of Tonatsi — offensive Juggernaut probe module; combat-oriented.
Choice B (Religious): Gouge of Tonatsi — mining Behemoth probe module that enhances Resource Recovery.

Both paths unlock Behemoth Blueprints, removing the starting Behemoth modification lock.

Recommended: Gouge of Tonatsi. The Gouge directly synergizes with the core economic engine — a better mining probe multiplies home system output for the entire rest of the game. The Wrath is useful for a dedicated combat Juggernaut build, but economic compounding typically outweighs the combat-specific reward.
CHAPTER 2 — GLORY vs POWER

Branch choices yield different system improvement rewards. Civilian Emergency Centers (Glory path) or Koimachi Academy (Power path). Both provide useful mid-game improvements. The exact reward difference between branches is less decisive than Chapter 1's probe choice — follow whichever branch conditions align with your natural play patterns.
CHAPTER 3 — BERSERKER vs ENDURE

Unlocks one of two unique Battle Tactics:

Berserker: Offensive tactic with aggressive combat bonuses. Suits glass cannon and weapon-amplified builds.
Endure: Defensive tactic, higher survivability. Suits armored slow builds and garrison fleets.

Choose based on your fleet composition and the enemy types you face most often.
CHAPTER 4 — SHRINE OF ETERNAL SACRIFICE

The Chapter 4 reward is the Shrine of Eternal Sacrifice: a system improvement that grants +50% Keii after battles permanently.

Timing matters: complete Chapter 4 when your battle Keii economy is already strong. The +50% stacks with Hissho Holiday Act (+20%), Tombs & Trinkets (+3%/pop), and Venerable Standard (×2 flotilla). In a fully stacked late-game war, single battles can generate 30–50+ Keii.

🏆 Victory Path Analysis STRATEGY ▾
SUPREMACY — THE NATURAL PATH
Supremacy Victory requires conquering enemy home systems. Every Hissho war generates Keii and removes enemy systems from your colonization penalty counter. Taking a home system is the natural endpoint of the Hissho escalation sequence. The Keii loop, subdue-raze doctrine, forced combat posture, and Sacred Tradition fleet intelligence all point here. Pursue Supremacy if you are fighting neighbors who haven't allied against you.
SCIENCE — VIABLE TALL PATH
A dense home system running Fealty Centers, Benthic Gems, Super Biofuel Factories, and Urgent AI Research (converts 50% of Industry/Food to Science) can generate Science output competitive with Sophons. The Steam guide recommends Offensive Laboratories + declaring war on everyone in the late game for the Science per CP destroyed. Science Victory is legitimate, especially if your galaxy favors a protected tall position.
CONQUEST VICTORY — AWKWARD FOR HISSHO
Conquest Victory requires controlling a minimum number of systems simultaneously — directly conflicting with the colonization penalty. Supremacy Victory accomplishes the same war-win outcome without the system-count requirement. If winning through domination is the goal, Supremacy is strictly better for Hissho than Conquest.
ECONOMIC VICTORY — VIABLE SECONDARY
Hissho generate strong Dust output from a well-developed tall home system. Trade Clearance Bureau (taxes rival trade routes) and Dust-enhancing luxury development make Economic Victory achievable. Less natural than Supremacy but viable if your neighbors have stabilized or been eliminated and you want to coast to an economic win on accumulated FIDSI output.
