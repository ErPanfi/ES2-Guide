ES2 // Vol.7 // Faction Selection

Faction Selection Guide

Twelve factions. Wildly different rulesets. This volume is not a summary of each faction — the deep dives cover that. It is a selection tool: a structured way to match your playstyle, experience level, and game context to the faction that will produce the most interesting, learnable game. Two difficulty axes, a primary challenge label, victory path ratings, and cross-faction matchup analysis give you the filters needed to make an informed choice before the first turn.

00

How to Read This Guide

What the two axes measure, what the chips mean, and how to use this volume before choosing a faction
📐 The Two Difficulty Axes MECHANIC ▾

Every faction card in Topic 2 displays two bar indicators. They measure different things and do not add up to a single "difficulty" score — intentionally.

MECHANICAL COMPLEXITY
How far does this faction deviate from standard ES2 rules? A low score means the faction uses familiar FIDSI, colonization, and diplomacy mechanics with minor twists. A high score means you are learning a parallel ruleset — one that replaces, rather than modifies, the baseline game. The bar fills in the faction's own color to emphasize that complexity is identity, not a warning.
PUNISH SEVERITY
How badly does misplay compound? Some factions are forgiving — a slow turn-10 can be recovered by turn 30. Others enter death spirals: one wrong decision compounds into irreversible deficits within 5–10 turns. This bar fills red because severity is a direct risk signal, independent of complexity. A mechanically simple faction can still be brutally punishing (see: Cravers).
THE FOUR ARCHETYPES THIS CREATES
Low complexity + low punish = learning-friendly (United Empire, Vaulters). Low complexity + high punish = constrained aggressor (Cravers). High complexity + low punish = experiment-friendly expert faction (Horatio). High complexity + high punish = high-ceiling specialist (Vodyani, Umbral Choir). Your experience level determines which quadrant you should target.

Each card also carries a Primary Challenge label — a single sentence naming the specific reason players most commonly lose with that faction. It is the thing the complexity and punish scores cannot fully express. Read it before dismissing a faction as "just a higher bar version of something simpler."

🏆 Victory Path Chips MECHANIC ▾

Each card shows a faction's viable victory paths, color-coded by tier. These ratings are drawn from the faction deep dives and cross-referenced against community analysis. They represent how naturally the faction's mechanics point toward each win condition — not the maximum achievable tier by an expert player ignoring faction identity.

Chip ColorLabelMeaning
EconomicPRIMARYThe faction's mechanics actively point here. Pursuing this path amplifies your faction's identity rather than working against it.
ScienceSTRONGA natural second path. No fundamental mechanical conflict; requires deliberate tech choices to prioritize.
ConquestVIABLEAchievable but requires building against the faction's natural tendencies or accepting a slower approach.

Victory paths not listed on a card are not necessarily impossible — they are simply paths where the faction's mechanics create friction rather than leverage. An experienced player can pursue any path with any faction; this guide is about informed default targets.

Playstyle Archetypes

Six recurring patterns across the twelve factions — a vocabulary for filtering before reading the cards
🧩 The Six Archetypes STRATEGY ▾

Factions cluster into recurring strategic patterns. Some factions belong to more than one archetype — the overlap is informative, not an error. Knowing which archetypes you enjoy (or find tedious) narrows the card grid from twelve to two or three candidates before you read a single complexity bar.

THE GENERALIST
⚔️ United Empire 💰 Lumeris
Standard mechanics, flexible win conditions, low friction on expansion. The engine is a familiar FIDSI loop with a single amplifying mechanic on top (Influence buyout for UE; Dust conversion for Lumeris). No alien ruleset to internalize. Correct for players who want to learn strategy from the ground up, or who want reliable performance across varied maps.
THE TALL FORTRESS
🦅 Hissho 🌀 Vaulters 👁️ Umbral Choir
Fewer systems than everyone else. Higher output per system than most. Force concentrated rather than distributed. The Vaulters achieve this via portal teleportation; Hissho via Keii compounding on a single dense home system; Umbral Choir by holding exactly one system and projecting power through Sanctuaries and hacking. If you want to prove you don't need quantity to win, this is your archetype.
THE SNOWBALL AGGRESSOR
🪳 Cravers 🦋 Vodyani
High ceiling with a death spiral underneath. Both factions are engines that produce exponentially increasing returns — as long as they keep moving. Stop the Cravers' expansion or the Vodyani's Essence loop, and the engine collapses. The payoff curve is steeper than any other archetype; so is the crash. Not recommended as a first "hard faction" pick — understand the engine before trusting it.
THE PATIENT INVESTOR
🧬 Horatio ❄️ Riftborn 🏛️ Nakalim
Weak or conventional early game followed by compounding mid-to-late power. Horatio's splicing engine takes 25+ turns to ignite; Riftborn's constructed pops require Industry investment before they generate returns; Nakalim's relic and temple network builds slowly toward a dominant Influence sphere. All three reward the player who can absorb early vulnerability without panicking into suboptimal emergency plays.
THE NETWORK BUILDER
🌿 Unfallen 👁️ Umbral Choir
Expansion through connectivity, not colonization. The Unfallen spread a vine network that converts territory into a diplomatic and economic engine; the Umbral Choir deploy Sanctuaries that serve as remote production nodes feeding a single hub. Both factions treat the galaxy as a graph problem rather than a territory-capture problem. The Unfallen approach is intuitive once the vine is understood; the Umbral Choir version requires full mechanical re-learning.
THE SCIENCE RACER
🔬 Sophons 🌀 Vaulters
Technology lead as the primary strategic lever. Sophons exploit Omniscience — a decaying science discount that rewards discovering techs before everyone else. Vaulters combine a science-typed population, portal bonuses, and Hyperium system development to produce competitive science output without a dedicated affinity. Both factions can pursue Science Victory; both can pivot to non-science wins if the race goes wrong.
OVERLAPS ARE INFORMATIVE
Umbral Choir appears in both Tall Fortress and Network Builder. Vaulters appear in both Tall Fortress and Science Racer. These overlaps signal factions with multiple viable strategic directions — but also factions with higher complexity, since the player must choose which archetype to lean into per game.

Faction Matchups: Galaxy Context

Which factions collide, which coexist, and which combinations make a multiplayer session interesting or lopsided
⚡ Victory Path Congestion STRATEGY ▾

When multiple factions in the same game share a primary victory path, the race to that condition becomes the defining mid-game conflict. This is not necessarily bad — some of the most interesting games emerge from two factions sprinting for the same win condition — but it is worth knowing before lobby selection.

Victory PathFactions with it as PrimaryCongestion Notes
Supremacy 🪳 Cravers, 🦅 Hissho, 🦋 Vodyani, ❄️ Riftborn, 👁️ Umbral Choir Most contested path in the game. A lobby with Cravers + Hissho + Vodyani will escalate to all-out war by turn 30 regardless of player intent.
Economic 💰 Lumeris, ⚔️ United Empire, 🧬 Horatio Moderate congestion. All three can coexist early; conflict emerges when Trade Company routes overlap and one Dust engine starts cannibalizing another.
Diplomatic 🌿 Unfallen, 🏛️ Nakalim, 👁️ Umbral Choir Low congestion but self-limiting: Diplomatic Victory requires a Senate majority — three factions chasing it split the Senate and none wins cleanly. One Diplomatic-primary faction per lobby is ideal.
Science 🔬 Sophons, 🌀 Vaulters Direct race. Sophons' Omniscience discount erodes faster when Vaulters research the same techs. These two push each other harder than any other science pairing.
Expansion 🌿 Unfallen Rarely contested as a primary condition. Low congestion by design.
🔗 Natural Allies and Natural Enemies STRATEGY ▾

Some faction pairings create natural diplomatic stability; others make war structurally inevitable. This is based on mechanical incentive alignment, not lore.

NATURAL ALLIES
🌿 Unfallen + 🔬 Sophons: Both have strong Pacifist senate representation and no structural reason to go to war. The vine network can boost Sophon-controlled systems; the Sophons' science lead benefits any ally through shared tech timing.

🌿 Unfallen + 💰 Lumeris: Both require a stable, non-war environment to run their engines. They compete for the same peaceful conditions and both benefit from maintaining them.

⚔️ United Empire + 🏛️ Nakalim: UE generates Influence surplus; Nakalim generates Influence through Temples. Neither gains from fighting the other early, and both benefit from Senate coalition building. Diplomatically stable pairing.
STRUCTURAL CONFLICTS
🪳 Cravers vs. Anyone: The Cravers cannot make permanent peace. Their mechanics structurally require expansion into occupied space. Every neighboring faction is a future target — plan accordingly.

🦋 Vodyani vs. Nearby Factions: Essence leeching drains neighboring systems passively. Any faction sharing a constellation with Vodyani will feel the drain before the first formal declaration of war. Proximity is aggression.

🦅 Hissho vs. Diplomatic Factions: The Hissho Militarist senate bias and Keii upkeep from sustained peace make diplomacy economically costly. 🌿 Unfallen and 🏛️ Nakalim players will find Hissho neighbors consistently unreliable as long-term partners.

Galaxy Setup Recommendations

Faction performance is not map-agnostic — some factions are transformed by galaxy settings, others are nearly unaffected
⚙️ Settings That Shift Faction Strength STRATEGY ▾

Galaxy size, density, and enabled victory conditions are the three settings with the largest per-faction impact. The table below flags cases where a specific configuration dramatically changes how a faction plays — not marginal adjustments, but fundamental shifts in viability or dominant strategy.

FactionSettingImpact
🪳 Cravers Economic Victory disabled Removes one of their strongest late-game pivots. Cravers who cannot close via Economic Victory must sustain military expansion indefinitely — exhausting and potentially unsustainable on large maps.
👁️ Umbral Choir Small galaxy Dramatically stronger. The Sanctuary network covers less distance; hacking operations reach opponent systems faster; the Nexus is harder to isolate. Small galaxy UC is a noticeably different experience from large galaxy UC.
🌿 Unfallen High density / many star lanes More star lanes means vines spread faster and block more corridors. High density also increases the number of nearby factions, multiplying the peace treaty FIDSI engine.
🌀 Vaulters Large galaxy, sparse lanes The portal network's value scales with distance. On a large sparse map, instant teleportation is worth far more than on a small dense one where every faction can reach every system in 3–4 turns.
🦋 Vodyani High density / many minor factions More nearby leech targets reduces starting location variance — the faction's primary weakness. High density is the closest thing to a Vodyani safety net the game settings offer.
🔬 Sophons Many factions in lobby Omniscience decays faster when more factions research the same technologies. A 6-player game collapses the discount much earlier than a 3-player game. Factor this in when picking Sophons in large lobbies.
🏛️ Nakalim Diplomatic Victory disabled Removes their primary win condition. They can still win via Conquest (Academy conversion counts toward it), but this setting narrows their natural path significantly.
❄️ Riftborn Standard — no strong setting dependency Performance is relatively map-agnostic. Planet type distribution matters more than galaxy shape, but no single setting dramatically shifts their viability.
SOLO VS. MULTIPLAYER NOTE
Several factions rely on AI passivity to establish their engines safely. 🦋 Vodyani need early leech targets that don't proactively destroy their Arks in turns 1–5; 👁️ Umbral Choir need opponents who don't beeline for their Nexus before the Sanctuary network establishes. Experienced multiplayer opponents will specifically target these pressure points. Solo play is generally more forgiving of faction-specific early vulnerabilities.
